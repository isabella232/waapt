    We've been concerned about some things that have been happening since you've returned. It's been a week or less since you've been back, and you've had several issues already, including:

    • Being overly insistent on certain ideas and trying to get them to work

    • Bringing iffy elements into canon (eg. Burst Hearts, and especially sexualized Pokemorphs)

    • Attempting to set up your PC as top-percentage and unbeatable. (I know you planned on her being an incompetent trainer but among other things I'm worried you'll jump to Origa beating up anything in her path more quickly as a result)

    • Derailing long-standing pairings on a whim (possibly insulting Daisy in the process, though we need to confirm with Daisy herself.)

    • Shoehorning excessive Kamen Rider refrences in any chance you get.

    • Shoehorning yourself into established subplots without setting up context first. (Eg. the Other!Tracer subplot. To be fair on this one, you ran it by Tracer first, but the lack of in-RP context still makes it jarring.)

    Look, I don't think you're a bad guy, Ori. You've said you don't really want to step on people's toes. But the fact we have to point these issues out to you so bluntly is an issue in and of itself. Ideally, people shouldn't need too much hand-holding to keep things under control, but some of what you do either has a risk of getting out of hand or has already.

    So... I really hope you take this advice seriously and try to improve. Run stuff by us first. Keep your ideas more scaled down, for the most part. Do things one thing at a time in a battle, and remember you can only control what your character WANTS an attack to do. And most of all, actually listen to us, and know when to take no for an answer. Hopefully then we can all stop worrying and just enjoy ourselves.
