    High Priestess of Fabulous

    Cognitohazards and You!

    So, this is another in my series of long essays regarding the as of yet woefully unexplored depths of what Psychic powers can do in the WAAPT multiverse.

    So, what is a Cognitohazard?

    A Cognitohazard, for the purposes of this RP, has been defined as any stimulus (be it a sight, sound, taste, smell, or feel), that has been infused with psychic power, such that merely perceiving the stimulus causes you to react in a way in accordance to the crafter of the Cognitohazard. Ember's music in the RP, for example, which has been engineered to be extremely catchy, is an example.

    What makes Ember's music more than simply catchy? Simply the fact that other ways of counteracting psychic energies have been known to stop it, such as putting up your own mental walls as well as using Dark Types as Head Pets.

    What can a Cognitohazard do?

    Well, as far as I figure it, most Cognitohazards can only do as much as the upper limit of Human Psychic influence, maybe a little more if there are more psychics working on the problem, or it there is a Psychic patron. Therefore, Ember's catchiness effect is probably not the upper limit of what Cognitohazards can do, but it's about an average level, and the most anyone would manage to get away with without severe Psychic consequences.

    There are, as I see it, four types of Cognitohazard effects, ranging from easiest to most difficult to create.

    1) Emotion, sense, or memory altering: These effects would be something like Ember's music creating a lasting memory into someone's mind that overrides others. Other good examples would be a color that causes mood swings, a taste that tickles your feet, or a sound that causes nausea.

    2) Compulsive: These effects create a strong urge or curiosity to do something. These effects are typically minor, temporary, and able to be resisted with the foresight to do so. Typically compulsions will not make someone do something that goes against their core beliefs or instincts.

    3) Mind-Affecting: These effects (usually permanently) affects the way a subject thinks (most commonly by altering personality or behavior, or inducing psychiatric conditions). This is distinct from a compulsion because of its permanence and in that this is usually an intense obsession, not just a mild urge. A good example would be the Siren's Song, that forces sailors to jump into the water to die, though unlike most Mind-Affecting effects, this effect typically goes away when the subject is no longer exposed to the stimulus. Mind-Affecting Cognitohazards are far beyond the capabilities of any human psychics acting without the aid of extremely powerful Psychic Patrons

    4) Body-Affecting: I want to start by strongly suggesting that no Body-Affecting Cognitohazards have ever been created by humans, and any created by Pokemon have been heavily shrouded in myth. Body-Affecting Cognitohazards are just like the name says, a stimulus that induces actual physical changes in a human. Something like this would be a song that turns people into Pokemon. As is obvious, this kind of Cognitohazard is a Dangerous Forbidden Technique, that the most powerful of psychics would rather die than use willingly. Maybe this is something powerful Psychic Legendaries are capable of, maybe, but it's still heavily shrouded in myth.

    Why are Cognitohazards scary then, if they are so weak on average?

    Well, they really aren't? At least not when we're dealing with Psychic Pokemon. Cognitohazards are a relatively uncommon, though manageable ability for Psychic Pokemon to exhibit, and researchers have regarded their use as mostly harmless. Or at least, as much as any other psychic ability exhibited by Pokemon. Pokemon-Human relationships have been mostly regulated by the Vow, and most Pokemon would not actively use a Cognitohazard on any human except in self-defense.

    Psychic humans on the other hand, have almost never been known to be able to create Cognitohazards, to the point that for a long time it was considered impossible, the stuff of legends and old wives' tales. Recent discoveries have made it clear that they have been documented, but it is still widely believed that there are no living humans that can create Cognitohazards, or at the very least, that there are incredibly few that can.

    Also, because they are so incredibly rare, there are currently no human laws dictating the use of Cognitohazards, any more than the use of telepathy to induce thoughts into other people.

    Finally, because they are incredibly rare, the full effects of human spread Cognitohazards have not been fully documented. The last known documented case of Cognitohazards was the Church of the Silent Song, disbanded about 500 years ago. It is still unknown exactly what humans have to do in order to create a Cognitohazard... Or maybe it's being hidden, because the power is so terrifying. No one knows. Basically, Nothing Is Scarier is in effect, at least for Psyche.

    So, what is the Church of the Silent Song?

    The Church of the Silent Song was a minor gag I put in my characters' personal ending of the Conquest Arc. It was a small group of women that worshiped Meloetta, but took a vow of silence. Now, given Meloetta is notorious for her contributions of music and dance to the world of humans, this was meant to be a mini gag, but I have since decided what I want them to have actually been, given this Ember subplot.

    The Church of the Silent Song is a cult, consisting of a group of powerful psychic women, that have learned the ways of the Cognitohazard. Rumor has it, these women can entrance men with their very words, though it is much more pronounced when they sing. As such, these women all take a vow of silence while in their church. They were disbanded because of... unsavory practices.

    The test to get in was rumored to have been behind numerous disappearances in the nearby villages. The idea was to abduct a man between the age of 18-40, lead him into the woods, and keep him there for a week. The potential candidates were not allowed to touch the man or speak to the man except in song, they had to keep him there with song, and song alone.

    This test alone would probably be chalked up to strange goings on, and would be likely attributed to unfaithful husbands and rowdy young men, if it weren't for the fact that some of the men did not come back, and the men who did recalled absolutely nothing of the experiences.

    The cult's activities quietly stopped around 500 years ago, and there are no records of what happened. They found the Church building completely vacant one day, with no signs of any struggles or even signs of life at all. It's rumored that there perhaps may still be people who follow the cult's doctrines even today. But that's just a myth, right? :P

    So that's about all I've got on this so far, please feel free to comment with thoughts, as always.
