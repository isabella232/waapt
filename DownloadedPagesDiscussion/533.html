    I am thou. . . Thou art I. . .
    After taking into consideration what was said in chat yesterday, I have reworked my Orre sub-plot idea. Here it is:

    Rain has stopped falling in Agate village. [Someone] is visited by Meloetta, and tells them that the J-Team needs to save Celebi's forest. Cipher's trying to get rid of Celebi's threat to the shadow pokemon project again, but this time they're aiming for directly Celebi.

    Meanwhile, [Team member who likes to explore places] is exploring a cavern with what appears to be a musical composition carved into the wall and the passage. Said personmon takes pictures to study later.

    The J-team finds out what's Cipher plan is: Cipher is weaponizing a Shadow Ninetales' Drought ability, preventing rain from falling, causing Agate Forest's irrigation systems to dry up, which is killing the forest. With the Agate Forest dying, Celebi will soon be weak enough for capture or eradication. After squad of J-team members successfully destroys the lab and the machine, various members scavenge the computers for useful info. Unfortunately, according to plans [One of the search group] found, they were supposed to hit the off switch before destroying the machine! The effects, while weaker without the Shadow Ninetales, will take a long time fade.

    Experimentation with Rain Dance and Drought Politoed shows that while rain effects stop and weaken the Drought Effect quicker, the rain effects won't stick as the Drought refreshes itself every so often. Strengthened by the sudden influx of water, Celebi appears and tells those present to use the Time Symphony, a relic site. So, [personmons can play an instrument, sing, or dance] get called to Agate while those already there set up.

    Once [everyone who needs to be there] arrives, the song to activate the Time Symphony can be played thanks to [Team member who likes to explore places]'s photos. After what appears to be a wild Swanna chase, [one of the performers] has an Eureka Moment! The phrase in the passage "keys of the dead" meant the song must be played four times, in a different key each time, and in a specific order. The order? D to E to A then back to D. Cue the most awesome music and dance number in J-team history so far with Meloetta and Celebi as an audience.

    And the best part about saving Celebi? That Cipher's computers can yield new leads!

Cipher is going after the Relic Stone/ Agate!Celebi connection ... again!(?) But this time Cipher's trying to get rid of Celebi, not the Relic Stone.

    Meanwhile, some personmon(s) is exploring a cavern with what appears to be a musical compositions carved into the wall and the passage:

    Far one must travel, And much one must see
    For in one's heart they must search for the key
    As the epilogue sounds, the darkness recedes
    Reversing all threats to the Guardian's trees
    With a melody played on cataclysm's eve
    The Muse's song being the forest's reprieve
    In the final hour bring out what's inside
    For the keys of the dead can change what's outside

    Said personmon(s) takes pictures to study later and reports their findings. Well that's cool and all, but it doesn't help Agate!Celebi! the J-team finds out what's Cipher doing: they're weaponizing a Shadow Ninetales' Drought ability, preventing rain from falling, and drying up irrigation systems and killing the forest to weaken Agate!Celebi!

    Better get rid of that machine fast! One successfully destroyed outpost/ lab later, various J-team members are scavenging the computers for useful info, but why is it still not raining? Well according to plans a personmon found, we were supposed to hit the off switch before destroying the machine! The effects, while weaker without the Ninetails, will take a long time fade. Cipher got smart - their plan can still work.

    Experimentation with Rain Dance and Drought Politoed show that while rain effects stop and weaken the Drought Effect quicker, the rain effects won't stick as the Drought refreshes itself every so often. Well we can't let a few trainers and their Drought Politoed sit here for 20 years, so what to do? Well, Agate!Celebi appears, slightly strengthened by the rain, and recommends the Time Symphony, a relic site created by Meloetta and itself and used by people of Ancient Agate to regulate the sun and rain before irrigation systems. That could work, more power behind the moves means the Drought Effect will dispel sooner! So, who which personmons can play an instrument, sing, or dance? We got volunteers? Yes? Good! Get to Agate while those already there set up!

    One spelunking trip later, the instruments and stage are found, so where's the music? On the walls of the cave of course! Small problem, it's too worn and faded to read! So what now? Use pictures that were earlier of course! One jam session later ... Nothing changed?! Everyone performed it right, so why didn't it work? Should we try playing it in D minor... Eureka! The phrase "keys of the dead" meant the song must be played four times, in a different key each time, and in a specific order. The order? D to E to A then back to D. Cue the most awesome music and dance number in WAAPT history to date!
