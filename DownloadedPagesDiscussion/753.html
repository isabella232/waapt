    Dogs, about Contests

    Pretty much yes. Though Ren has earned all but two of his ribbons during my "Christmas break" (remember contests can last several hours) so Ren isn't totally in the clear for entering all four Grand Festivals (Kanto, Johto, Hoenn, Sinnoh) either.

    On Grand festivals themselves

    I honestly recommend using the info on Bulbapedia Grand Festival page as the basis for flow.

    I usually align Johto Contests with Sinnoh Contests; as Johto Contests were constantly referenced to, but never directly shown in the anime, and the GS remakes where Gen IV. And Hoenn and Kanto use the same format as each other, I find this a nod to the RSE and RB(RG) in Gen III. Is everyone cool with that?

    EDIT: This part of the post is for showing off guidelines that are already in place.

    On normal contests

    For those who are new or haven't seen the Contest thread in the other forum, here are the guidelines for contests from that thread:

    -Contest have two portions; the Appeals Round and the Battle Round. Only 8 to 16 coordinators may move on to the Battle Round. Winner of the battle round is declared the winner and receives the Ribbon.

    -Contests will be done mostly in the style of the anime with a few elements from the games or the main RP added in.

    -The Appeals round will be done like they are in the anime, but have to demonstrate at least one of the Five Contest Categories from the games.

    -Props can be used for appeals. All props should be smallish and/or easily moved.

    -Any form of outside interference means immediate disqualification.

    -Mon on stage can not have contact with audience or judges in any form or fashion.

    -A single volunteer can be used for an appeal, the mon can not inflict any harm whatsoever to the volunteer. The volunteer can not be a plant. If the volunteer is discovered to be a plant, this results in a disqualification and/or revoking of a ribbon.

    -The Battle Round will be like the anime with the "points gauge" that empties as a player's mon are shown up by their opponents, and fills as they show up their opponents, perform a particularly good looking move, or being stylish in general. Since the points gauge was rarely used to decide a winner, the gauge shouldn't present of much of an issue RP mechanics-wise, but don't forget that one can lose if their mon perform poorly enough.

    On Appeal Exhibitions

    This is An WAAPT original contest type. They are basically talent show-esque events where all types of trainers of all skill levels come to show off their mons' contest-like appeals - but without the move or number of Pokemon used limits in place.

    The finer details still need to be hammered out, but we haven't done a lot for it IRP yet either.

    Please comment or give some input on the Grand Festival and Exhibition portions as they're what needs to be hammered out.

    Sebby: Hooray, all the newbies are returning! This is good!

    Marin: If you happen to be reading this, I can add to what Moon said with a simple statement: your problem appears to stem from a fundamental misunderstanding of how the RP combat system actually works. Let me explain further by analysing each issue you seem to have. (WARNING: INCOMING WALL OF TEXT. PLEASE READ CAREFULLY. TL;DRING WILL NOT BE TOLERATED. THANK YOU.)

    Problem 1: Battles go on for ages due to the increased bulk

    The bulk increase isn't actually that large. It's enough that a Mon is very, very unlikely to go down in one hit, because let's face it, having all your characters one-shotted in a text-based format would get boring fast. But - and this, I think, is the crucial "but" of my argument - it does not turn every Mon into a tank. OHK Os are next to neonexistant, but the limit has only been increased slightly - the norm for a mon of average bulk against a neutrally effective opponent seems to be between 3 and 4HKO. For attacking Mons with paper-thin defences, 2HKOs are not uncommon. I should know, my main character's main Pokmon is a Blaziken. Battles are long enough to be interesting to read, but not, as you fear, drawn out enough to become dull.

    Problem 2: Pokmon can dodge every hit because the accuracy has gone down

    You appear to be basing this on Lady, who by the way is a Chandelure, dodging a single Shadow Ball. You didn't even wait to see whether or not any other moves would hit, you just decided "loldodging". In that case, I suppose "the accuracy has gone down" is sort of true - moves like Shadow Ball that normally have a 100% hit chance are no longer absolutely guaranteed to hit, just very very likely to. The honour of being "the move that never misses" belongs to Aura Sphere and Aura Sphere alone, since it's a homing attack that couldn't be outrun by Speed Form Deoxys riding his bicycle. But - again, important bit here - moves still hit far more than they miss. 80 base speed isn't exactly high, but it's respectable enough, so a Chandelure dodging a move that is no longer a guaranteed hit is not impossible. It was just unlikely to happen again... not that you waited long enough to try a second Shadow Ball. Seriously, it kind of pisses me off how quick you were to dismiss battling based on one move.

    Problem 3: My characters will look incompetent because of this

    It's not incompetence if everyone else is on the same level as you. This is the reason we can't just give you exactly what you want - allowing Valorie and co. to OHKO regularly and use moves that never ever miss would put them head and shoulders above the rest of the cast, and nobody likes an overpowered main character. If Valorie is a strong trainer, trust me, it'll show. She won't look incompetent, because nothing in the rules says she has to.

    Problem 4: It'll be like the ridiculous battles in the anime

    We all think the anime is ridiculous, believe it or not. The system we use actually has the game as a basis, rigid type effectiveness and all. The changes made were attempts to make the battles flow better as text. In fact, Every's essay - which, if you did things properly, you should have read before signing up - explicitly discourages anime-style ass pulling, using "AIM FOR THE HORN" as an example.

    Sorry if this comes off as offensive in any way. If I happen to be wrong, don't worry, I won't flip out. Just explain exactly what I got wrong, and I'll see if I can work out a better compromise.

    ...and because it probably got forgotten amidst my ranting, I'll say it again: welcome back, Sebby!
