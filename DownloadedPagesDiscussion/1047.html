    26157 Herbert40k8th Sep 2015 05:08:05 PM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
    Imperial Commissar

    Future

    Thanks to Crow for going over the key points of divergence for my characters. I don't think there's anything for me to add, other than the fact that Orre went about ten times worse for Lucius than it did in the main timeline and may have resulted in Maul's death. (And I can't think of a better name than "Lillifuture" either.)

    Gates

    Seeing as a lot of people seem to be saying that they don't see any issues with the Gates, I figured I'd make an effort to explain my reasons for opposing the idea quite so strenuously. Hopefully me being awful at explaining things shouldn't get in the way.

    As Dune's post here said better than I ever could, Power Creep is my main concern. I understand that the power boost provided by Gates is temporary and has severe side effects, but the fact that they exist as a last resort still vastly diminishes the menace of villains who aren't capable of similar feats. A character like RP!Tagg already has mons that could solo entire teams by themselves, a suite of legendary artefacts, and Auric powers that bring him to practically Nigh Invulnerable levels of strength without opening a single Gate. If he can go Super Saiyan when he needs to on top of that, then after Constantin, it's hard to see how any villain could ever pose a feasible threat without resorting to Gates or other techniques. Given that we've had problems with villains getting too nasty and having too many powers in the not-to-distant past, I'd rather not run the risk of further escalation.

    Which brings me onto my second big concern: the Gates give preference to characters whose power levels are already incredibly high. It's the main reason why I don't think comparisons to Deltas and Purification moves are valid, since at least those are relatively attainable by comparison. I'm not overly concerned about bandwagoning, though I admit it could be a problem - there have been cases in the past where characters have gained powers without going through a proper permissions process, though with something this risky I'd imagine policing would be more stringent. My big worry is that the introduction of the Gates widens an existing power divide. Many of the RP's newer characters are just normal trainers with no special abilities or powers, who are never going to reach the crazy power levels of some of the RP's old hands no matter what. It's already difficult for them to meaningfully contribute to raid scenarios or battles against villains when some of the guys on their side could end the fight with a snap of their fingers. In my mind, an arc villain is ideally someone who should pose a threat to the whole team and can't be defeated by any single one of them. When it becomes necessary for the villain to be so powerful that he can only be defeated by a Gate Auric in order to pose a threat, then the arc becomes about that Gate Auric, and the other participants fade into the background. I'm of the opinion that the top-tier characters in the RP handle enough plot stuff already without bolstering their power further.

    I have other concerns too. There's the fact that RP!Tagg used Heroic Safe Mode during the Orre arc to subconsciously activate powerful Auric techniques and severely injure friends in the process, as well as his use of the Adamant Orb to facilitate the VBT. The circumstances have changed, certainly, but it's still the case that he can do enough damage with the power he has. There's also the issue of how the lasting effects of using the Gates would be handled - this isn't an attack on anybody's writing skills, but there have been plenty of past instances in the RP where the long-term consequences of a character's actions haven't been as lasting or long-term as one might expect. (I'm plenty guilty of this myself.) Most characters still need to take part in day-to-day activities, after all, and if the consequences of an action might interfere with that then waiving those consequences can become a necessity for the story to continue.

    These are all just my personal opinions, though, and I don't want to get in the way of anybody's plots. I'm still against the Gates in principle, but if enough people are in favour then I'll drop the issue.

    Checkmate.
    Newf: The existence of the Gates is not up for debate. They're an established part of the RP and have been around for close to a year.

    Bittersweet: Tagg and several others have acknowledged a permission system insofar as Gate usage by PCs and PC mons goes. Which I happen to agree with. It's very much not "This thing only RP!Tagg can do."

    As far as the concerns about Pokemon, the Gates are much less non-Pokemon than many other factors of the WAAPT universe. They're simply a spiritual interpretation of an existing powerset that humans and mons are capable of using.

    Insofar as RP!Tagg's usage specifically, I'm in favor. Constantin's mons need to be taken down by other mons, but Constantin himself is a human foe, and a particularly arrogant one at that. Story-wise, it's only fitting that the way to defeat him be to ascend to his level, which is presumably something that would assist in the deconstruction of Cons' worldview.

    That said, I do think it's worth considering things like Herbert's "burnout" idea. To look at this from a metafictional perspective, there is reason to be concerned about power creep. If it's possible to unlock the Gates, even with the issue of it burning out the user's life like flash paper, it means that every interaction in the future needs to address the Gates. Either villains escalate (The DBZ approach) or every villain who is a credible physical threat (which many of them in the RP are formatted as) needs to have a way to shut down the Gates (the Cortosis approach), either way cheapening the understanding of the Gates. It's unrealistic to expect that the Gates, especially if they're used to defeat Cons, would not be something known to at least the more informed class of villain.

    It seems to me that the best approach for the current question is that, when RP!Tagg uses the Gates to defeat Constantin, that usage locks them completely for a time due to overload. From that standpoint of the Gates as a oneshot "cutscene" ability, we can work forwards from there to determine their ability to be used within standard gameplay, as it were.
