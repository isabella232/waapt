    Burger Scientist
    Okay so, I have had not one but TWO epiphanies. I shall put them in order.

    1) The pacing of the main thread is due to a dichotomy between two groups of players: "chatty" players, and "plotty" players. The chatty players will usually make smalltalk amongst other chatty players, while plotty players would usually make smalltalk in lieu of recent plot developments. This lead to Bittie mentioning that she observed that the players who would usually chat and stick to their own small-time personal plots joined in Kalos, while most of the ones who would wait until prime hours to interact with other people in their plots joined in Orre or before.

    This lead to epiphany numero dos.

    2) The nearly non-existent plot of Kalos had the side effect of splitting the base between the aforementioned groups. The players who joined in Kalos did not have a plot to join in on, and thus never had to learn how to join a plot. Thus, when we went back to an overarching plot in Rangers-2, the roleplayers who joined beforehand went right back to the plot, but the recent joiners were mainly left on their own and formed somewhat of a group of their own (I would use the word "clique", but that has different connotation and there has been some crossover between the two). Now, I'm not saying that a complete conversion is in order for one group's manner of interaction or the other, but easing the two groups together would likely go miles toward salving the strain being placed on the RP.

    So, you might be asking, "Dune, you realized these things - surely you must have some idea about what to do."

    Well first I would tell you to not call me Surely. Second I would say that I do have an idea. This is just my initial plan, straight of off a "Solved Puzzle High", so it's just barebones and open for fleshing out. Anyway, the plan: The newer members of the J-Team, the ones that joined in Kalos, are brought into a single plot (or a few slightly smaller plots) tailor made for them, joined by a few older members either GM'ing or assisting with their own characters or NPC's - it would help ease them into the plots of the more experienced, older posters. This would, in a way, bring the two groups together and get the group on one or two tracks of pacing rather than having one slow lane, one fast lane, and one "ROCKET SLED FAST" lane that leaps ahead when nobody's looking.

    Again, this is just my observations, but the data I have adds up as given above.
