Since I feel I'm still emotionally unwell overall even though I'm feeling better now (my mood goes though very distinct highs and lows at the right procovation and lows at least for the past few months have been far more common and far easier to trigger), I'm still not entirely sure to what extent I should communicate with you guys until I, pardon my Kalosian, CALM THE FUCK DOWN.

    However, either way, there's been one, particular source of miscommunication that was only a slight factor in the debacle a couple nights ago... But at the same time has been such a thorn in my side for my entire time in the RP I felt I should do something about it even though it's admittedly rather late in the game to do so:

       Three Deadly Words   

    (OK, two of them are phrases but what the hell)

    Anyway, there are three words/phrases that cause great psychological unease on my part at best and rift-inducing arguments at worst whenever another player says them to me over IM: "I don't know what to say", "I see", and "Heh".

    The phrases themselves are innocuous; the problem is the way my brain instinctively reads and interprets them. Basically the problem with all three is it sees hidden incincerity and/or malice that (at least most of the time) isn't actually there, but for specifics:

    • "I don't know what to say" - usually said after me venting about negative feelings about something, and intended to be literal - they're at a loss for words and literaly have no clue how to properly respond - but my brain reads it as the speaker having mixed feelings about what I said and many of them are unsympathetic, or worse, they're using it to hide a complete lack of sympathy.

    • "I see" - used in many situations as a genreral "neutral" response to something I'm saying, but given how cold it reads to me it sounds like there's hidden malice twoards me and/or whatever I'm talking about.

    • "Heh" - usually said when I try to say something humorous, and meant to indicate mild amusement, but to me reads extremely sarcastic to the point the person saying it sounds like they're saying "your joke sucks and you suck for ever daring to think I'd find it funny instead of obnoxious."

    So... When I Ming me, keep all that in mind and use the following alternatives?:

    • "I don't know what to say" - "I'm at a loss for words", "I can't think of a proper response", anything to more clearly indicate you literally just have no idea how to put your feelings into words.

    • "I see" - "Oh, I see/OIC" (the "oh" helps for some reason, I have no clue why, my brain is weird) "Oh, OK", "Ah, OK", etc.

    • "Heh" - Just slap a ":P" or a "o3o" on the end, it sounds more sincere in my head to me because again my brain is weird. Or just go for "lol" or something, even that sounds more sincere and less sarcastic than "heh".

    Please keep this post in mind when talking to me; I'll have a link to it on speed dial to gratuitously chuck at you if you don't. Thank you.

    But that's all I wanted to adress; otherwise for now I'm just glad I'm feeling better and more motivated after a huge downer.

    High Priestess of Fabulous
    So, last night, I mentioned that I thought of the Dreamscape in terms of five words: Massive, Organic, Chaotic, Powerful, and Eldritch. I just wanted to touch more on what that means for the dreamscape plot itself.

    Massive: Psyche's Dreamsscape is enormous. It's grown to border the mindscapes of everyone she's met, and is still growing, at a terrifying rate. If it expands much more, she'll be too far diffuse to ever recover fully. It's starting to bleed into the collective unconscious of the J-Team at large, and even further perhaps. This growth is uncontrollable by Psyche and definitely not desired or self-motivated.

    Organic: Psyche's dreamscape is almost as alive as she is at this point. It's growing and eating as if its life depended on it. The Figures, Psyche's corrupted aspects, act as its immune system, almost, while areas of the the dreamscape represent either pieces of the brain or organs in the body.

    Chaotic: The Dreamscape is reacting violently to the massive identity crisis it has achieved by gnawing on others memories and dreams. As such, massive spikes of power create unpredictable changes in the world. Those with Auric powers who enter the dreamscape may find their abilities altered, weakened, strengthened, or even awaken or lose abilities. Even Psyche's abilities, typically very strong in her own mind are far too dangerous to attempt to use, lest she awaken the natural defenses and ruin everyone's day. As such, Psyche's attempting to only use her powers to give hints and items to the party, as anything else could cause catastrophic spikes of power.

    Powerful: Psyche's lingering power is laying around in artifacts she's dubbed Relics. These artifacts allow Psyche some semblance of control over the environment normally, but right now, their power is far too amplified for her to use herself. Right now, she needs help from others to collect these Relics in order to ease the defenses of the Dreamscape. But, if used incorrectly, these Relics can stimulate the world's defenses and irrevocably change the landscape. That said, the world itself is incredibly powerful, especially around these relics. The world warps and twists in arcane ways, sometimes moving entire groups of people to a new part of the dreamscape, or dropping in the Figures to screw with the intruders in (usually) violent manners.

    Eldritch: Psyche's dreamscape is still warped by Grohl's influence, whether she likes it or not. Many parts of her dreamscape just feel... off. They show scenes of things she should have no recollection of, distant dreams of lands far off, things the human mind should not know about. This world is old, it's terrifying, and it's unknowable. Physics warp in ways lost to time and space, senses will attempt to perceive things far beyond our limited abilities, and if lingered upon, will slowly lead to long tern damage.

    As such, Psyche is using the last of her power to give people in the Dreamscape a multitool named the Dreamcaster, akin to a Poketch. This Dreamcaster is how Psyche will send hints to everyone, let them track their sanity meters and map the currently known parts of the Dreamscape, as well as mark spoopy areas. However, using the hint function will increase the general threat to them from the environment as well as from the Figures, so use with caution. If the sanity meter grows too much, it would be best to leave the Dreamscape and do something less mentally taxing to recharge, because if it gets too high... Bad things might happen.
