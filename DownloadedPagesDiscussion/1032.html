    This is a bit late, but...

    As we all know, Satoru Iwata passed away last Saturday, which hit us all hard. But as I read about his life I learned something that made it hit me even harder.

    You see, for a while I've been worried some people don't consider Pokemon a "real" Nintendo franchise because it's developed by a company technically outside Nintendo. And while I'm pretty sure the lack of Pokemon acknowledgement in other, non-Smash Nintendo franchises is mostly due to that, I still had the feeling the developers of Nintendo itself were like that as well.

    But after Iwata's death I learned he wasn't like that. He made Pokemon Stadium possible. He made Kanto in GSC possible. And he continued to support the franchise the rest of his life.

    So goodbye, Iwata, and thank you for not just caring about one or two or three Nintendo franchises, but caring about them all.

    So, I want to bring back an idea for a subplot I had way back in Kalos but never actually did anything with. I've finally worked out how it's going to work, so...

    The Battle Carnival

    Several J-Teamers, as well as a few associated people outside the J-Team, end up getting invited to an island somewhere for a battling event known as the Battle Carnival, run by the mysterious Spiegelmann.

    In it, teams of two are pitted against hordes of Pokemon, trying to outlast an opposing team as the hordes gradually become more chaotic and overpowering. Not only that, but each team can make the opposite horde more difficult for their opponents through a point system, earning points by knocking out Pokemon in a horde and spending them to either increase the size of the opposite's horde, grant buffs or debuffs, and other things.

    Each round, the remaining teams are randomized — for example, one battle in one round may have Dune and Tagg paired up against Jacob and Blitz. If Jacob and Blitz are defeated and eliminated, the next round might have a battle involving Dune and Eskay against Tagg and Ellen, or possibly not even have Dune and Tagg participate in the same battle at all. (Entirely hypothetical, of course, I dunno whether Dune, Tagg, Verax, and Blitz actually want to participate.) It continues up until the final round, in which the rules change and the winners of the semifinal match are pitted against each other entirely on their own.

    As the tournament goes on, it may become increasingly apparent how questionably it operates. For one thing... where are these hordes even coming from?

    All I really need to know is who's interested, and when do they think it would be most convenient? Post-Delta? Wait until Sinnoh? Some other time? Or maybe it could happen on the other forum and be in its own TS bubble?
