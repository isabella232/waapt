    Imperial Commissar
    So... as you might have noticed, I haven't exactly been around much over the last few days. This has been due to a number of factors - school getting on top of me, illness, sporadic internet access, general lethargy and burnout, et cetera et cetera. I've been having ideas (including one for a third PC) but I don't know if I have the time or motivation to go through with them, not to mention that I've been getting sidetracked by other projects, both RP and non-RP related.

    All of this means that I might not be around much for a while, and for once when I say that my availability's taking a dive I actually mean it. So apologies for any inconveniences that my inactivity may cause. I should still be around if my presence is required, just don't expect much in the way of general interaction.

    On a somewhat related note, I'll be taking a full-on break at some point in the next few months in order to focus on schoolwork. I'm not sure of the exact timing, but by mid-April at the latest I'll be pretty much gone. I might pop into the chat every once in a while, and I'll happily read posts that are linked via PM, but I won't be following the RP. Said break will take a couple of months, so I'll probably end up skipping the arc after Kalos, but I've already got a way to offscreen most of my characters and once it's over I'll have plenty of free time on my hands.

    However, this does leave me with one fairly major problem - namely, what exactly to do with Neo-Galactic.

    See, with the way I've been taking Titan's character, it's looking pretty likely that he's going to HeelFace Turn at some point, and some point soon at that. I have some ways of re-establishing and maintaining his villainy in mind, but I'm still worried that it'll seem like dragging things out. Not to mention that if the arc after Kalos does end up more focused on combatting Illusion/Neo-Galactic, the guy who RPs the leader of the latter organisation disappearing for a while is going to cause some issues. So with that in mind, I've come up with a few different solutions, and am currently debating which option would work best from both a narrative and meta standpoint.

    1. Keep Titan in his current position throughout Kalos and my break. Though it's the simplest option, it's probably the option I like the least as well. It drags out Titan's development unnecessarily, and there's no way a control freak like Titan would go for several months without supervising his subordinates
    2. Have Titan defect, and hand Neo-Galactic's leadership over to a new villain of my own creation. This solution avoids dragging out Titan's development, and my absence could be handwaved away by said new villain having a more hands-off leadership style. My main worry is that a virtual unknown taking over Neo-Galactic out of nowhere or saying that "they were there the entire time" might seem like an Ass Pull, though I reckon I could make it work.
    3. Have Titan defect, and hand Neo-Galactic's leadership over to a character RPed by another player. This option is probably the best from a meta standpoint, since it takes care of Titan and allows other people with Neo-Galactic characters a lot more freedom. However, whilst I'll go with it if it turns out to be for the best, I'm not overly keen on the idea of surrendering leadership, since there were a few Neo-Galactic plans that I wanted to put into place should they serve as actual antagonists during an arc.
    4. Some other option. I've never been a particularly inventive person, so if anyone else has a better way of handling things, then I'm open to suggestions.

    Again, apologies for the long and somewhat rambling post and for making plans that I'm not in much of a position to follow through on, but I just wanted to clarify things in advance and any advice about how to deal with the Neo-Galactic problem would be appreciated.

    So I fancied up this ol' girl I mentioned earlier and I plan on introducing her to the RP as a source of conflict and potentially plot. This is one of the only characters I own that has actual powers of her own beyond her pokemon and I had to struggle to figure out how to word and phrase things correctly in an attempt to make it appropriate, soooo tell me what you think of my initial attempt at translating her abilities into obscura.

    Your Trainer's name: Dardanette Korie
    Age: 15-16
    Gender: Female
    Appearance: A picture is worth a thousand words, or in this case about $25◊ Noteworthily, Dardanette is supposed to appear disheveled and unkempt. YMMV with the official art.
    Region of origin: Lilycove City, Hoenn

    Backstory: Dardanette's was a somewhat privileged and sheltered girl belonging to a wealthy family in Hoenn. She wasn't very social and took up hobbies in various artistic avenues such as drawing, dancing, and playing musical instruments to have 'fun'. Something went horribly wrong however; at some point around age 13 she became a felon after an investigation spurred by an eyewitness into the disappearance of Dardanette's brother and one of his friends brought uncomfortable amounts of attention to a sudden onset of the crazies. When asked where her brother was Dardanette merely giggled, replying that they were close. She seemed to have become detached from reality, having no sense of danger or better yet remorse; an eerily omnipresent smile refused to leave her face even as she was inevitably hauled off to jail by local law enforcement. They did however make the mistake of underestimating the young girl's strength and she lashed out, attacking the officers and fleeing with the help of her pokemon. Dardanette's brother and friend were never found despite her claim, and due to the inability to reclaim her no one ever quite found out why Dardanette had so suddenly descended into a psychopath; chances are however that it has something to do with one or more of the ghost pokemon she has been hanging around ever since, though. She now preys on others from her place in the shadows, usually avoiding crowded places.

    Dardanette would seem to be in an almost constant state of mild bliss and generally speaks in a relaxed manner, sometimes even outwardly demonstrating a gentle and affectionate nature. This often doesn't last long however as she is prone to suddenly becoming violent without provocation or seemingly any pattern for that matter. Dardanette has a strong ghostly aura to her, detectable by psychics and aura sensitives within a quarter of a mile. This is a self-control issue brought on by Dardanette being an obscura witch and has a habit of attracting wild ghost pokemon that find the energy pleasant to be around. Dardanette is capable of basic illusions, basic barriers and causing non-damaging nightmares in sleeping people; She is also capable of using watered down versions of the moves shadow punch, ominous wind and lick in the form of a kiss that drains souls/life force. She uses this Dementor Kiss of Death to sustain herself for the most part instead of actual food which... is probably not healthy, but she does it! While she is not all that strong of an obscura witch she is capable of performing more complicated and advanced obscura magic when one of her ghost pokemon dedicates itself to helping her. (See: Emphasis on dedicate) Noteworthily she does not sleep and therefore has never demonstrated the ability to do astral travel; She also has never demonstrated the ability to create Twilight.

    Species: Gulpin (Starter)
    Nickname: Piggy
    Gender: ♀
    Ability: Liquid Ooze
    Personality/Bio: Gulpin was Dardanette's original pokemon and the only pokemon she owned prior to the onset of sudden madness. She was caught by Dardanette herself. Gulpin is a simple pokemon; she wants food, and her definition of food is literally anything she can fit inside of her. (Keep limbs and appendages inside the vehicle the hell away from this pokemon at all times.) This pokemon usually doesn't have a lot to say and what she does say usually demonstrates a one track mind. Noteworthily, she was likely at Dardanette's side at the time of her descent into madness.

    Species: Gengar
    Gender: ♀
    Ability: Levitate
    Personality/Bio: Gengar is an evil clown, constantly pulling pranks on and terrorizing her victim of choice before either devouring their soul or leaving them be as her mood may fit. She is one of Dardanette's go-to choices for when she needs a pokemon to help her with obscura magic due to her ability to phase into Dardanette's shadow and seamlessly synergize with her. Conversationally, Gengar is a jerk with a taste for puns. She enjoys irony and probably trolls internet forums or something when she's exceptionally bored of trolling real life. She has an affinity for the cold.

    Species: Banette
    Gender: ♀
    Ability: Cursed Body
    Personality/Bio: This cursed doll is not quite so forgotten. Dardanette seems to become upset if she is taken from her and Banette quickly follows, becoming violent moments after being removed from Dardanette's arms. She has a tendency to stare at others and experiences a similar dissonant bliss to Dardanette when left to her own devices, though hers is much less persistent and vanishes when a threat is presented. Banette often feigns sweetness in a debatably effective attempt to garner trust. She is vindictive however and will respond harshly to those that have previously made her an enemy.

    Species: Froslass
    Gender: ♀
    Ability: Cursed Body
    Personality/Bio: Froslass is an ex proper lady, retaining much of her grace in the afterlife. This, of course, disregards the standard violent tendencies of a predatory pokemon. She loves art and likes to express herself and fight in ways she considers artistic.

    Species: Drifblim
    Gender: ♀
    Ability: Unburden
    Personality/Bio: Drifblim tends to grab and carry Dardanette off when things get tough or just when she is uncomfortable with the current location. Much like Piggy, Drifblim is quiet and doesn't usually have much to talk about. This is because she finds speech physically exhausting. Perhaps ironically she proved to be a bit aloof when she /does/ talk... You could say she's an airhead.

    Species: Gourgeist (Super)
    Gender: ♀
    Ability: Insomnia
    Personality/Bio: Gourgeist feeds on negative emotions, most notably those that can vaguely be defined as 'suffering'. It doesn't even extend to hurting others however, for this pokemon is not only a sadist but a masochist as well. In her own words, pain reminds her that she is alive. There is nothing she likes better than conflict and she can become 'stir crazy' when forced to be docile. Gourgeist apparently has a thing for hot sauce, both drinking it and forcing other people to drink it. Half the stuff this pokemon does requires censorship because of the sheer shocking cruelty contained within. A psychopath that makes even Dardanette look tame, Gourgeist is the absolute last pokemon you'd want to put your life in the hands in.

    Species: Honedge
    Gender: ♂
    Ability: No Guard
    Personality/Bio: This is yet another quiet pokemon. Honedge is a manipulative pokemon, a powerful blade that lures his wielders in and exacts a terrible toll on the wielder's body. This vampiric blade saps life energy with every tiny cut it makes, and when it isn't provided with such it drains it from a wielder instead. Dardanette is this blade's wielder, and for one reason or another she seems to be a willing one.

    Species: Spiritomb
    Gender: ♂
    Ability: Infiltrator
    Personality/Bio: Spiritomb is.. ambiguous. He does not speak, but not for lack of trying. His voice is a combination of a hundred and eight different voices attempting to speak at once, resulting in a loud and soul piercing noise that can't actually convey words. Mind reading unfortunately has no effect, as the same problem arises. Spiritomb is vaguely loyal to Dardanette; her motives, values and thoughts unheard.
