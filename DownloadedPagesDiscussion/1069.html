    The tl;dr of all this shit or at least the major players' roles

    Basically, you know how I wanted Robin to be the outright protagonist of the Delta episode before for character arc reasons?

    Well, now he's essentially the Decoy Protagonist, with Jacob, as noted before, being the true one.

    Around the start of the plot Robin teams up with Jacob after temporarily ditching Team Umbra for semi-spoilery reasons, and when the two learn of the impending asteroid impact and in particular Zinnia and RP!Eskay's antics they become two major players in the whole situation, Robin in particular to focus on all the clashes, ideological and otherwise, with Zinnia, while Jacob tackles RP!Eskay. In the end, however, it's Jacob that has to Mega Evolve big sky snek, smash the asteroid with it, and save the day, though Robin still finds ways to assist on the side and, more importantly, resolve his character arc.

    At the same time, a third major player ends up getting involved - Vyvyan. You see, in this version of the Delta Episode Zinnia lets slip she's trying to harness Rayquaza's power for her "evil" plan to save the world a bit early before her grandmother gives the full details, so Robin and Jacob recruit Vyv as "backup sky snek" for various reasons - causing him to get roped up into the plot and advance his own character arc.

    But that's just the basics of the major roles. For everything - and everybody - else, we'll need:

    The not-so-tl;dr of all this shit, AKA the actual motherfucking re-written outline

    As I said, I'd be rewriting this old dusty thing to accomodate all the recent changes in fine details, so here you go:

    • Scientists at the Mossdeep Space Center discover an asteroid heading to the planet; don't inform the general public just yet to avoid panic, but do decide to inform at least part of the J-Team since they're in the region.

    • Meanwhile, Robin ditches Team Umbra for the aforementioned semi-spoilery reasons and ends up running into Jacob and deciding to stick with him.

    • Meanwhile, someone -or rather someones - are stealing Key Stones from Trainers around the region. (Some people may volunteer to have their P Cs' stolen.) At some point Robin and Jacob meet Zinnia.

    • Steven and his father, President Stone, meet with several the J-Team and tell them what's going on; also that they have the technology to stop the meteor, but they need Meteor Shards first. (Note:I want to write Steven for one teensy bit of this but otherwise anyone who wants to handle him here can.)

    • The team goes on a Meteor Shard hunt. Jacob and Robin find a few in Granite Cave, but encounter Zinnia again. Robin gets into an ideological clash with her which leads to their first Pokemon battle.

    • Team delivers Meteor Shards to Mossdeep, is informed they need more. Zinnia appears and tells them the current plan is a bad idea. Robin has another ideological clash with Zinnia while Jacob has to deal with SUDDEN ESKAY

    • [SPECIAL EVENT; CODENAME: UNADULTERATED]

    • In the aftermath of the above, Zinnia inadvertently lets slip that she needs Rayquaza for her plans; this leads Robin and Jacob to recruit Vyvyan to Team Delta as a potential countermeasure.

    • Team goes on a scavenger hunt for more Meteor Shards; are told to meet up with Steven in Meteor Falls.

    • The team meets Zinnia's grandmother with Steven, who explains the deal with her and the role of Rayquaza in all this. She then gives the final Meteor Shard... And gives a mini-meteor to Vyvyan in secret.

    • The Meteor Shards fuse into a complete meteor, which Jacob carries for safekeeping; however, the team learns that Eskay and some recruited minions of his have stolen the Link Cable needed to stop the asteroid and they're headed to the Space Center.

    • Space Center; team fights Eskay's minions while Jacob fights Eskay himself; Zinnia reveals current plan will just punt asteroid to another 'verse, and after another ideological battle with Robin breaks the Link Cable.

    • [SPECIAL EVENT; CODENAME: BOOKEND]

    • In the aftermath of the above Zinnia tells the team to meet her at Sky Pillar.

    • Team discovers there's now a seal on the Sky Pillar; discover they need Wallace to do so and recruit him to unlock it. (Possibly someone battles him here.)

    • Team ascends Sky Pillar as Zinnia explains DA TAIL O' MEGAQUAZA!!1!

    • Team gets to top; Zinnia summons Rayquaza and performs some rituals... but can't Mega Evolve him. Robin challenges her over it, and the two have an EPIC FINAL POKEMON BATTLE.

    • Robin wins the battle, but after some other stuff with Zinnia attempts to Mega Evolve Rayquaza himself... But also fails. Then Rayquaza notices the Meteorite in Jacob's bag and swallows it; this along with Zinnia's earlier ritual causes Rayquaza to regain the ability to use Dragon Ascent after losing it in the Hoenn-1 arc (or possibly earlier), allowing Jacob to Mega Evolve him.

    • Team gets into Aqua/Magma suits and rides space-outfitted Stormchaser into space while Jacob rides Mega Rayquaza to go destroy the asteroid. Zinnia, Robin and Vyv are initially left behind... Until the latter two figure out a plan.

    • Jacob and Mega Rayquaza, with a leeetle bit of help from the other J-Teamers in the Space Stormchaser, successfully smash the asteroid to smithereens... Only for a Deoxys to emerge and do battle with them all. (Exact details here, especially on upping the ante, are still pending; see a later bit of this post.)

    • Afterwards, with the crisis finally averted, things wind down, Zinnia says her goodbyes to Robin and goes off to atone and find a new path in her life, and Hoenn-2's "true" postgame promptly begins.

    Though, even with more people, one thing's still a problem...

    Deoxys' Uncertain Destiny

    Alright, one particularly persistent thorn in our side in planning this thing is how to handle the Deoxys battle, since Deoxys is a lesser enough legendary in RP canon that a lone one probably won't stand all that much of a chance against the combined forces of the J-Team and BALANCE DRAGONS. So... We need some way to up the ante a bit.

    Tangent's pushed the idea of a Wonderful 101 tribute with the initial Deoxys being an expy of one of the game's villains that summons 100 other Deoxys/100 lesser extraterrestrial mons several times, but it's been shot down enough times with a frigging machine gun I feel trying to revive it once more would be an exercise in abusing deceased equines unless we decide we literally have no other options.

    Keys suggested a similar idea in taking a page from the movie I made another Mythology Gag towards in the title of this section and give this Deoxys the ability to make shadow clones of itself. Now IIRC in the actual movie said clones died in one hit and I think I remember at least one person arguing against the idea because of that, but really, that's something we definitely can fucking afford to take a liberty with, especially given Boss Battle Privileges. And overall it's a nice toned down-version of the whole "crowd boss" idea that would ideally let Jakey and Mega Rayquay focus more on the actual Deoxys while everyone else focuses more on the shadow clones.

    But I'd like to hear everyone else's thoughts still - though alternate ideas are definitely appreciated, and I honestly prefer anyone attempting to shoot down the shadow clone idea to offer an idea of their own, since reducing our options here is helping no one at this point.

    Joe.dek: Aroma◊

    Overview: Aromas are a Plant-based deck that center around gaining lifepoints. Each of the Aroma monsters has an effect that activates when you gain LP and an effect that's applied when your LP is higher than your Opponents. Aroma's control the game and maintain a lead with healing effects and synergize well with Plant support such as the P Lant Princess Series. Behold the Everlasting Garden!

    Justification: Joe is a cute character so he'd likely run a cute deck that's passive yet proactive at the same time.

    Deck (40)

    Monsters (23)

    • Aroma Jar: Flip monster that's immune to battle destruction and gives 500 LP after every End Phase. Great stall wall and can trigger your Aromas.
    • Aromage Bergamot: The fiery aggressive Aromage that gives your Plants a piercing effect and can boost her attack to a whopping 3400!
    • Aromage Canagan (x3): The quiet, bookish Aromage. She's more subtle than Bergamot, debuffing the ATK and DEF of your opponent by 500 each. She can also bounce nasty spells and traps.
    • Aromage Jasmine (x3): The littlest and quite possibly the most important Aromage, Jasmine gives you an extra normal summon per turn (provided it's a Plant monster) and lets you draw into your combo pieces.
    • Aromage Rosemary (x2): The watery Aromage, she can changes the battle positions of monsters and prevent your opponents pesky floaters from activating in the graveyard.
    • Cactus Bouncer: An annoying card to say the least, Bouncer prevents both players from making special summons. Sit on it and let your Aromages control the game.
    • Dandylion: Pretty much a staple in Plant decks. Summons two tokens when sent to the grave. Great for Synchros and Tribute Summons.
    • Lonefire Blossom: Also a Plant deck staple. Lets you get any Plant from the deck. ANY Plant.
    • Rose Archer: First of the non-Plant Plant support. Rose Archer is a warrior that can protect your Plants from trap cards by simply discarding it.
    • Rose Lover: Daaaamn girl. Erm, anyways, Rose Lover can be banished from the grave to summon any Plant from your hand and protects it from traps for the rest of the turn.
    • Rose Paladin: Another non-Plant Plant support Rose Lover is an 1800 beater that acts like a Lonefire when she kills a monster. Also in the off-chance of a Dark World match-up she can search any of your Princesses when sent from the hand to the graveyard.
    • Glow-Up Bulb (Swapped in for Spore): A Plant tuner that can reborn itself from the grave by milling a card. Great for Synchros or clutch Tribute summons.
    • Sunny Pixie: An interesting tech, Sunny Pixie is a level 1 tuner that heals for 1000 when it's used as Synchro material. Combos well with Ancient Sacred Wyvern
    • Twilight Rose Knight: The last non-Plant Plant support Knight is a level 3 tuner that brings out a level 4 or lower plant from your hand.
    • Talaya, Princess of Cherry Blossoms: First in the Princess series, she gains 100 for each Plant you control and protects your other plants from effect destruction.
    • Tytannial, Princess of Camellias: Lets you negate and destroy cards that target by tributing a Plant.
    • Chirubime, Princess of Autumn Leaves: A taunter that forces your opponent to attack it and no other Plants. Lets you bring out any Plant when it dies.
    • Marina, Princess of Sunflowers: I do like my FIRE Plants, Marina lets you pop any card after a Plant dies.

    Spells (9)

    • Aroma Garden (x3): Field Spell of the deck. Lets you gain LP and boost your monsters by 500 if you control an Aroma and gives you 1000 LP when one of your Aromas die.
    • Fragrance Storm: Pop a Plant, draw a card, reveal and draw again if it's a Plant. Simple on the surface but can combo with some of the other cards here.
    • Mark of the Rose: Plant-themed Snatch Steal (a banned card). Banish a Plant and equip it to an opponents monster and take control of it. Control passes back and forth during each turn like a game of hot potato :D
    • Miracle Fertilizer: Sacrifice your normal summon for endless Plant revival.
    • Raging Mad Plants: A game finisher. All of your plants gain 300 for each plant in the grave. Use it wisely because it destroys all plants at the end of the turn.
    • Wonder Clover: Send a level 4 or lower Plant from your hand to the grave to let one of your monsters attack twice this turn. Another game-ender.

    Traps (8)

    • Blazing Mirror Force: For some reason I thought the Mirror Force Series to be appropriate in this deck. Anyways Blazing Mirror Force pops all of your Opps. Attack Position monsters after an attack then inflicts damage to both players equal to their combined original ATK halved. Since the deck is about gaining LP you can afford to burn through some of your own life.
    • Burying Mirror Force: Same activation conditions as Blazing. This one sets all of your Opps. monsters and prevents them from being flipped back up.
    • Storming Mirror Force: Same as the two above but bounces monsters.
    • Dried Winds: Aromage Continuous Trap. Pops a monster when you gain LP and can nuke your Opps. field if your LP gets high enough.
    • Humid Winds (x3): Pay LP to add Aromages to your hand. Gain Lp if yours is lower than your Opps.
    • Pollinosis: Negate a Summon or a Spell Trap and destroy at the cost of a Plant Tribute.

    Extra Deck (7)

    Synchros (5)
    • Ancient Sacred Wyvern: A favourite card of mine that requires a LIGHT tuner (Sunny Pixie). Gains ATK when your LP is higher than your Opps. loses ATK when lower. Can reborn itself at the cost of 1000
    • Black Rose Dragon: Field Nuke, but also has a Plant-based trick...
    • Black Rose Moonlight Dragon: The above cards AU counterpart. Bounces a monster when it's summoned when your Opp. special summons a level 5 or higher monster.
    • Queen of Thorns: Annoying in a fun way. Forces players to pay 1000 for each monster summoned from the hand that isn't a Plant.
    • Splendid Rose: Halves one monsters ATK at the cost of banishing a Plant from the grave. Can also attack twice for the same price.

    Xyz (2)
    • Meliae of the Trees: Dump an Plant or revive a Plant. Simple.
    • Number 87: Queen of the Night: The Princess Series boss monster, this is a massive 3200 ATK that requires 3 level 8s to summon and comes with three effects: One that locks down set Spells and Traps, one that that sets Plant monsters and one that boosts another monster by 300

    Note: This deck isn't competitive in any way. It's a fun build.
