    Alright, since Wolf's last "primer" for this is kinda old, I think I'll do an update.

    CONQUEST PRIMER

    Ladies and gentlemen, this summer the future is in the past. ONWARDS AOSHIMA

    Wait, wait, what's this all about?

    The Conquest arc is based on the spinoff game Pokemon Conquest, which is a crossover between the Pokemon franchise and the Nobunaga's Ambition franchise. It takes place in the land of Ransei, where various Warlords vie for control of the region.

    Wait, but what does this have to do with WAAPT?

    Ah, you see, for the Conquest arc we're going to THE PAST for a sort of Flashback Episode . 600 years ago, our ancestors vied for control of the Ransei region and the rest of the Pokeworld.

    Of course, we're not forcing everyone to be in this Flashback Episode . If you want to stick with exploring Holon (Which is the same time) that's fine. But you're missing out on something cool~

    Ancestors? Are we playing as them?

    Yep. They can be at various levels of Identical Ancestor , so long as they are something like their descendant or descendants. They should also be mostly older than your modern characters, the minimum age being 16.

    (I'm kind of a weird exception to this, as three of my Conquest characters ARE my modern characters and the fourth isn't an ancestor to anybody, but whatever. :P)

    Our Ancestors are Warriors, which are kind of precursors to Trainers. They bond with mons by forming Links with them. Each Warrior has one Perfect Link, which may or may not be your modern character's Pokeform. They can link with anywhere from 4-8 mons at a time, though, with the average being 6. The chiefs among Warriors are Warlords, who rule the nations of Ransei and beyond.

    Also, in Conquest, Warriors can "wordlessly speak" to Pokemon. I'm interpreting that in-RP as the inhabitants of Ransei can get the general gist of what mons are saying, even if they don't truly understand Monese.

    Warriors can also "transform," which is akin to Pokemon evolution but gives your Warrior a flashier outfit (Usually themed around your Perfect Link) and better abilities. More about those later.

    What about the battles? Won't that get dark?

    Ah, not at all. The battles in Conquest are highly ceremonial, and the goal isn't to kill, just to prove you can beat the opponent in strategy. It's kind of like a precursor to modern Pokemon battles.

    Battles are conducted on special fields. The goal is to either defeat all of the opponent's mons or capture certain points on the map.

    Each Warlord can have up to five other Warriors on their side. That translates to up to six Pokemon on each side on the battlefield.

    Mons can use one move per battle (Though that may change depending on how much of an uproar that causes), and each Warrior has a Warrior Skill they can use once per battle. The list of Warrior Skills is here.

    The winning Warlord usually gets something for victory, usually dominance over/an alliance with the other's nation. Warlords can also recruit Warriors, if the Warrior is impressed enough by their skills.

    All of this is mostly bloodless. However, there is room for darker stuff. Duels, assassination attempts, and other acts of nastiness are not unknown.

    (Plus, Umbra's Batman Gambit to take over the world. :P)

    Wait, but where is this?

    This mostly takes place in the region of Ransei, which is shaped like Arceus and is divided into 17 regions themed around the 17 types. Since 17 isn't enough for everybody, we're gonna have to either have some people as subordinate Warriors/Warlords and/or have people in control of other parts of the Pokeworld as well.

    A preliminary list of who's where is here.

    How will the characters interact, besides battles and stuff?

    Any way you want. It is highly recommended you mix it up a little compared to the modern day, and interact with characters and players you don't normally interact with. Even with the ones you do, try to have a different relationship - maybe someone you're bitter enemies with is your BFF ancestor-wise, and vice versa. This applies to Pokemon too!

    This also leads to the subject of shipping~ Due to what is mentioned above, and the fact that even with all those generations of separation having your ancestors be in the same pairing is kinda squicky, it is required you have a different ship from the modern day. Feel free to get creative. For example, Umbra is married to a Pent ancestor (and thus is a Pent ancestor himself), while Lina is married to Ancestor!Eskay (And in a way is Ancestor!Ali).

    But what's the goal here?

    The main plot involves us conquering each other to unlock the secret to an incredible power... Which doesn't exist, as it was made up by main villain Umbra so he could distract the other warlords and focus on his real goal: an uber-powerful wish-granting Lunar Pendant.

    As for ties to the modern day, we'll be discovering info via research and visits to the Holon Museum.

    Aaand... I think that's all you need to know, unless anybody has any further questions.

    Now...

    An important request

    I've been a little worried about Conquest, and for one very good reason: RtAU.

    It pretty much spluttered and died, all because some of the big players in it got screwed over by life and no one else bothered to care. I am very worried no one will care about Conquest either, despite all I and a few others have invested in it.

    So please, everyone. Don't give Conquest a shabby treatment. Help it live. Because even though it's not our present-day characters, it's still important.

    Also, on that note we need to do something about Rt AU. I'm not sure what, but we need some way to properly resolve it. Because as much as some people want us to just dump the AU and leave it, I care immensely about its fate, and want to see this Darker and Edgier world and the characters that live in it earn their happy ending.

    The Hero No One Knows
    Ancestor Jacob time.

    Name: Celzvet Kaeqiri

    Backstory/Biography: A nobody from nowhere, Celzvet is an aspiring Warlord and was once a generic NPC person in a teritory that was conquered. Though it wasn't necessarily violent, the event nourished a hatred of weakness and failure in Celzvet. Afterwards he decided to vigorously train his mind so that were he ever in the situation of his previous ruler, he would not have lost.

    Appearance: For appearance, just take Jacob make him twenty seven, make his hair almost girlishly long, far more athletic looking, and time-period appropriate black-and-purple themed clothes. A key note is his eyes, which are a cruel, striking electric blue, and are without exception the first thing noticed about him.

    Personality: His personality is similar to Jacob too, just a bit more extreme. He is logical and very methodical about everything, from how he walks to how he goes through his day to how eats to how he battles, and thus comes off as sort of mechanical. He is also exceedingly cold, willing to put people in awkward or dangerous situations for his amusment or to further his own agenda, and will try to make it be both.

    He isn't all bad though, he has undying loyalty to those he becomes closely acquainted with, and willing to risk everything for those he cares for. He has a sense of humor, is rather informal and laid back and friendly when he doesn't have a reason to despise a person. He is a bit of a prankster, and will subtly set up people for embarassing moments, or play the matchmaker and set two people thinking the other one loves them, not too dissimilar from the characters in Much Ado About Nothing. He enjoys having fun when he can, but can go from likable to ice cold in a snap. He is a master strategist, setting up people to do exactly as he wants them, the cause of his rather frequent failures is lack of information on his enemies, underestimating them, and too much confidence in his own abilities, as well as his lack of powerful warriors.

    Region: I actually don't know. I was hoping for some suggestions.

    Warrior Skill: Cold Eyes, ironically I didn't even know this was a skill until after I made his appearance. It just happened to work well.

    Perfect Link: Aron line

    And that's that. Thoughts? Complaints? I know he seems rather villainy/anti-hero-y but I don't think he'll end up actually being one. He's sort of like Jacob in the regard that he can just be a jerk. Except Celzvet is actually likable when he's not being a jerk, as opposed to Jacob.
