    26915 DuneTheWanderer27th Nov 2015 03:47:38 PM from Nowhere, USA Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Seeking boyfriend-free girl
    Burger Scientist
    To burn off some of the nasty mental gunk that's been building up for me, I'm gonna bring back an old series:

    How to Improv!

    Today we're going to talk about one of the most primary rules of improv, and one that I can't believe I had forgotten up until this point.

    Convenient Location

    Doofenshmirtz: <Wait, where the heck am I?>

    Norm: <I do believe we are currently inside one of our author's demonstrations on how to improvise!> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <GAH! Okay, well, what the heck are we supposed to do?>

    Norm: <You start off! Give us a set up and I'll continue!> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <Start off where?>

    Norm: <Anywhere! This exercise is to show how flexible this rule can be!> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <Uh, okay. So... I'm... going down the street, when suddenly, I see a kickball on the side of the road.>

    Norm: <Yes, and the kickball is flat, so no one can use it!> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <Wait, what?>

    Norm: <Just keep rolling with it, sir!> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <Rrrrright... anyway, so I see this flat kickball on the side of the road, and... and...>

    Norm: <Yes, and?> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <And, um, and then a Beedrill popped out of the bushes! HE was the one that made the kickball flat!>

    Existentially-Questionable Beedrill: <Yo.>

    Norm: <There you go, sir! Yes, and then the Beedrill started chasing Dr. D, because he had invaded his territory, just like the kickball!> =D

    Doofenshmirtz: <Wait, what?!>

    Existentially-Questionable Beedrill: <Buzz buzz, bitch.>

    Norm: <I think that's all the time we've got for this demonstration! Back to you, Mr. Author!> =D

    Thank you everyone, for the demonstration. Now, what words did Norm use? What key combination of letters lead to Doofenshmirtz and him creating this interesting (and mildly painful) scenario?

    The words you're looking for are "Yes, and?"

    This one turn of phrase is an excellent basis for improvisation, especially with other people. It acts as a rule in two parts:

    "Yes" represents that you are accepting the situation presented by your partner(s) - it shows that you're not trying to subvert or change the situation as given, but are rather trying to build off of it. You are agreeing to the basic reality given by others - this is important in improv, since otherwise things become a jumbled mess of continuity. It's here that the collaboration commences.

    "And?" shows that as well as accepting the premise of your partner, you are also adding to it. Certainly, be reasonable in what you add - don't just throw ninjas into every improv you do or that'll get boring!

    And then you repeat the process as needed. Even Doofenshmirtz picked it up after a bit! And trust me, if Doofenshmirtz can do it, so can you!

    Doofenshmirtz: <Gee, thanks for the vote of confid- OUCH!>

    Existentially-Questionable Beedrill: <I'm still here.>

    So don't be afraid to use this whenever you and another person are in need of something to do! Just remember that if you make any serious choices, you or your character will likely be held accountable. But then again, fixing that could also be part of the improv, or make for an amusing Noodle Incident.

    That wraps it up for today's How to Improv! Remember - these are guidelines, but they're damn good guidelines!
