    26845 Verax22nd Nov 2015 12:46:06 PM Get RP Mod
    The Hero No One Knows
    Okay, gonna summarize a few key points here.

    Keys suggestion is primarily focused on the fact that life does continue and there are reactions to events where the narrative isn't focused. I don't see this as a problem. The problem lies in that some people find the idea of widespread panic... unappealing. Despite the fact it would be not only realistic, but unavoidable.

    Also, while the DE is complex as is, perhaps this part of it, involving others not actively involved in the main plot, needn't conflict with the everything running in the main plot. It can be handled in smaller groups or on each writer's own, who want to do so on their own. This is not saying "this is what people are going to be doing", but rather opens up an avenue for people to do things who otherwise are sitting around doing nothing, it wouldn't be required participation.

    We could tone it down a bit, the gym leaders and other League officials requesting help to quell people agitating the situation and help reassure the public who are already aware that it's being dealt with, without it being full out riot control.

    But all in all, I see no issue with Keys suggestion. Life will go on regardless of where the narrative focuses. How we decide to play that is up to us, but I think ignoring it is the wrong decision, since it can open up opportunities for people who want something to do to do something, related to the DE but without overcomplicating the running plan.

    Not to say "we're going to have the public panic regardless of how the community feels", but putting a hard NO on that is just, in my opinion, unrealistic. Not only unrealistic, it is purposefully shutting down potential ideas for interaction. If we want to minimize it, we can, and I think minimizing it in story is a wonderful way to actually have people who want to do something have a potential course of action.

    Otherwise if people WANT something to do, they can either

    • A: Think up something to do on their own

    • B: Try to participate when opportunities are offered

    • C: Come up with some other side thing to do

    • D: Do nothing and wait for the story to progress. Choosing this option, in my opinion, does disallow complaining about having nothing to do.

    So yeah. That's my piece in this.
