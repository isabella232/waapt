    I am thou. . . Thou art I. . .
    Here's my problems and a (possible) way to make it better.

    The biggest problem is probably the scope of the idea. The idea is just too big. One or two songs would be a good number to keep the idea at "sub plot" level.

    The next thing is the power idea. Powers like you're describing generally happen in certain settings were it is okay for characters to have extra powers. e.g. Like Lyokoplot - where characters got powers based on the fact that the setting was on Code: Lyoko (though mon usage was encouraged while the human PC supported their mon)- or in a mindscape plot - where the characters get extra powers due to not having mon. And at best, all said powers should only be active during the plot and go away at the end of it, or on the other side of the scale, be de-powered thoroughly.

    However, in what your proposing runs counter to that - at least that what it seems to me - as the plot requires human characters to master unique powers to bring the plot to a happy ending. Yes, I know I shouldn't be one to talk, but the idea in the RP is that if a human character doesn't have innate powers like aura or obscura, they should let their mon do most of the work, and even then, only do things themselves during a "fight for your life" scenario, or at the other end of the scale when the situation calls for it. (e.g. RP!Umbra or Lina breaking out shadow claws against a normal human when someone they care for is threatened is a appropriate, but pulling shadow claws on a thief isn't - it would be perfectly fine to have them command a mon to use a disabling move like Thunder Wave or a sleep move.)

    To make it better I recommend changing the time loop/stream/line idea to something like "a mon-based-villan-expy drags some P Cs in to a cutoff area away from the team and said P Cs must escape", dropping the powers idea entirely, picking one song (three at the most?), and then having events in the be based on the song.
