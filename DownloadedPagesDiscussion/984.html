    Cenus

    I feel that if a past mon that if a past/retired mon is still important to the RP today it could be counted - albeit in a fashion that calculations that figure them in is tallied separately. As for future mon, I'd only be willing to count them in the census if they've made an Early-Bird Cameo or two, and it has become clear that they will be caught by an active PC - then they could be counted, but like statistics involving past mon, I feel that future mons should be included in separate calculations.

    e.g: Mon that have hatched from eggs: XXXX Percent of total mon: YY%

    Mon that have hatched from eggs (including future mon): XXXX Percent of total mon (including future mon): YY%

    Mon that have hatched from eggs(including retired mon): XXXX Percent of total mon (including retired mon): YY%

    Mon that have hatched from eggs (including retired mon and future mon): XXXX Percent of total mon (including retired mon and future mon): YY%

    Choreography

    Since some people don't understand why there should be a move like Choeography, I'd like to point out that there has been precedence of the only difference between two moves is one is physical and one is special and their distribution with Giga Impact to Hyper Beam being the most obvious example. Although it isn't as common as re-typing moves - like the many variations of Swift and Quick Attack - but is does happen (e.g. Horn Leech became the Physical counterpart to Giga Drain this gen). So then I thought, "Since there a move to make mon make noise/sing/speak in sync, why can't there be a move to make mon move/dance/attack?". So then I thought up Choreography (or whatever we're calling it) to be the physical counterpart of Round.

    And while I've been wanting a physical version of Round since around my second playthrough of Black before B2 and W2's release, although it has been moved to the back of my mind every now and then before something makes me revisit the idea before re-shelving the idea. But when I saw how the appeals segment in contests got those few extra mechanics, the idea of a physical counterpart started to really start tugging at the corners of my mind and doesn't want to be shelved again easily.

    Pippi: That was not, and never will be, my intention for Choreography. I liked the idea of a move that would allow mon to - at least to most human's eyes - move in perfect synchronization without the needs for weeks of non-stop practice, having excellent chemistry while performing, and/or having a psychic link (although have any or all those things would just raise the synchronization level).

    On the battle-side of the spectrum, it could be an effective way for mon who lack teamwork skills pull off attacks that would likely have missed the target and/or hit an ally. Since the way I see Choreography working it doesn't force mon to perform a synchronized dance-like attack, but makes mon move in such a way that the attack was rehearsed to near perfect levels - in other words, it makes an elaborate group attack look like it was choreographed.

    Census

    We're still debating the finer points, but that only affects a few Pokemon, so...

    The 2014/15 OU Census is now open!

    Please add Pokemon from the present of the OU that fit one of the following criteria:

    • They appear in an average or more than average percentage of your posts.
    • They are/were integral to any arc, plot, or subplot since your entry into the RP.
    • They are planned to be or are currently part of some sort of event/appearance in the RP.
    • They are important enough to your character that it warrants notice and representation of the species as a whole.

    EDIT: This doesn't mean 'don't include your Pokemon if you don't think they've done anything significant,' it means 'one-off characters shouldn't count for the census'

    Please only add your own Pokemon characters, as you know them best and to avoid duplicates. If we do end up adding any retired characters' Pokemon, that will likely be decided by everyone.

    Pokemon are organized by evolution line and final evolution, with base form Eevee having their own category. Please put yours in the appropriate column along with which Pokemon they are specifically.

    And I would like to clarify that this is not just a census of a Player Character's Pokemon or trained Pokemon in general. If an NPC Pokemon meets any of the above criteria, feel free to add them.

    If anyone has a reason to want to do an updated census for other universes or Conquest time, you can make your case, but we'll just have the OU one until then.

    And if any of these points are unclear, I'll try and revise them.

    Anyway, for the other points...

    I agree with Keys' comment on 'if retired player's Pokemon are still significant, they should be added,' but the rest seems a bit unnecessary, especially for the future mons. The main reason we'd go with past Pokemon is because of the 'was' significant bit, I think. My main reason for arguing in favor of future mons is because I, and possibly other people, will be introducing Pokemon of a line that I don't think has representation IRP, so the derived results for that would be a bit misleading in the near future. Other cases, I don't know how much they'd affect the tally.

    And, like I stated above: This is not just a census for PC's Pokemon: Significant NPC Pokemon (trained or not) should be counted too.
