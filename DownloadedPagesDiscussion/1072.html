    26792 Tangent12815th Nov 2015 08:09:28 PM from Virginia Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I LOVE THIS DOCTOR!

    Fluff vs Crunch

    In the context of this RP:

    Fluff is approaching the RP world as a story; decisions are made more on the basis of characterization and narrative.

    Crunch is approaching the RP world as a game, to potentially be won. Decisions are made more on the basis of "strategic advantage".

    This RP, unlike somenote Though with a friendly DM, any game can be played from a Fluff approach, whether or not it is balanced from a Crunch perspective stat-based tabletop games, generally leans in the fluff direction.

    For example... take Forte.

    Forte: Hey!

    Forte is very much a Fluffy character.

    Forte: Yeah, I'm a furry, but you don't have to shout it out loud.

    Er... that is to say, Forte's designed around a theme. Their choices of Pokmon are based on their personality, and their life goals may not necessarily involve competitive battling.

    However, even if Eeveelution teams are not the best competitively, Forte's mons often do fare better in battles than they "should" from a game-mechanics perspective. This is because WAAPT battles generally defer to "dramatically interesting" rather than hard numbers.

    A Crunchy way to design a character would be to pick their team of mons with the intent of assembling a team that would be competitively viable in-game or online. It's by no means against the rules, especially if your character is meant to be a competitive battler in-universe. However, it is not something you "need" to do to have a good time.

    And if you assume that having a Smogon-Approved team means you are for sure going to be able to beat, say, a Wooper, you may very well have a bad time.

    (Note also that the normal battle rules can be thrown completely out the window if all involved players agree. Generally to allow something extremely dramatic/hammy/JoJo. However, if you really really like crunch, you can agree to play by stricter rules if all players agree.)
