    Alone in a world without true light...
    27210 Umbramatic21st Dec 2015 05:15:56 AM from WAAPT or PEFE Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: In another castle
    The eternal dusk returns
    Double post a fair bit disparate from my last one, but I woke up a tad early and have had a hard time falling back asleep, plus I wanted to get it out of the way, so...

    So, as you all probably saw this past weekend, the Werehuman Madness subplot finally ended, and the penultimate post had the plot twist of Seishirou and Fuuma (spoilered in case anyone reading this actually hasn't seen yet) being made pretty much permanently human. Now, this twist was planned for quite a while, and I told almost no one the full truth... but I wound up consulting Crow on handling it because of some concerns he's been expressing to me over the past few months, and he and I decided to whip up a D!Post about said concerns.

    As you can tell, this is that D!Post. I felt it appropriate to time it soon after the WHM finale because I feel it kinda drives some of this post's points home. I currently don't feel it should be an addition to one of the essays, though, for the following reasons:

    • There's still a mutual issue me and Silent had with part of the Crunch vs. Fluff section of the Tone Essay I need to get around to frigging fixing.

    • It's less of a newbie problem (for most aspects) and more something we've seen in established players.

    • The post's long enough to be an essay in its own right (though iffy on making it one for reasons, including those listed here, though I may possibly be swayed.)

    • Probably most importantly... The concerns here aren't so much "BIG FUCKING PROBLEMS" and more "things people should more actively think about as they RP".

    But with all that I give you:

    On Pokemon Perspective

    Yup, this is a post on writing from the perspective of the Pokemon franchise's titular critters in this RP and some concerns Crow and I had in that regard with how people sometimes handle them. Said concerns are varied, but most of them boil down to "Pokemon are people, but as in any work of fiction with multiple sapient species running around, "person" doesn't necessarily mean the same thing as "human" and that should always be kept in mind". I'll start with a couple that aren't quite a big of a concern as most of the others, but are still relevant enough to the subject matter to bring up:

    You sure that Heliolisk isn't a cardboard cutout?

    This is just saying the following: Pokemon are just as much of characters as humans are, and should be treated as such, rather than props or attack animals. As I said, this is easily the least of the concerns - most players are pretty good about treating their Pokemon as actual characters even if they have more character focus on the humans, and WAAPT as a whole is refreshingly miles better about that than the vast majority of Pokemon R Ps out there, so this concern mostly crops up with newbies and/or particularly bad players. Still, it was tied enough to the other concerns to be worth mentioning with them.

    Hey guys, I just popped into existence a few seconds ago! :D

    This one isn't as widespread either, but it's a good one to keep in mind since it's likewise intertwined: Like how we ask for a backstory for trainers in the Signup thread, Pokemon don't just suddenly pop into existence when they're first posted for, they have some sort of past that may or may not affect their actions now. That said, it's not like you need to give each of your Pokemon as intricate a backstory as you give your trainer(s), especially if you're not planning on focusing on them as much as your trainer(s) and/or you have a lot of frigging mon characters. Just keep in mind they were born and raised somewhere, most likely in one of the main series regions, may or may not have a family or some other group they've run with, and ran into their current team and started travelling with them by some in-character circumstances.

    Next we get into more widespread iffy bits...

    Gothitelle: Does this dress make me look fat?

    Since Pokemon and humans are both essentially people, it's obvious they think and do things similarly to a decent extent, so that's not the problem. The problem is when Pokemon think/act like humans in ways that make no friggin sense for their species.

    As Crow elaborated on in his gender expression post, Pokemon wouldn't have the same standards of masculinity/femininity as humans, so them having those standards is really jarring and their players/Trainers trying to change or force them to adhere to such standards in any way - or giving other players' mons that don't adhere to those standards ill treatment of any sort in-universe and/or meta-wise - can get outright obnoxious or even disturbing. On a very related note, standards of physical attractiveness are very similar, and quite jarring both in and out of universe, especially in extremes like this:

    Amoongus: -to Arika- <How did you get so pretty?>

    Arika: ...You're a giant mushroom.

    (Note if a mon goes human that makes things complicated in that specific department - as Arika there full well knows - but mons still wouldn't have human standards of attractiveness as mons and some stuff related to humanization is covered later in this post.)

    There's also the case of mons reacting to more general things in a way that's normal for a human but rather nonsensical for their species for no real in-character reason. These tend to be relatively subtle, but still jarring when they crop up, and at their worst can lead to cringeworthy unintentionally ironic extremes like the attractiveness example above; for a similar example in this vein:

    Human: There's going to be record lows weather-wise tonight...

    Snorunt: <Definitely staying inside then. I can't deal with the cold.> >_>

    Human: -stares at the Snorunt-

    -curls up in a ball-

    -contemplates his questionably meaningful existence for a good long while-

    This may seem a bit tricky to deal with for an obvious reason: All us R Pers are human except for Verax who's pure Pomeranian and obviously see things in a human-y way. To better deal with it... just try and think of what you know about the Pokemon species in question and use that as a guide for those kinds of things? Additionally there's a whole genre called xenofiction revolving around writing from a non-human perspective, so looking up advice on that would be helpful, and some other players may have resources in that regard.

    "[INSERT POKEMON SPECIES HERE] are all the same, gaiz"

    This is a section on avoiding stereotypes with whatever species you're writing for. To elaborate, I'll use one in particular as an example: Gardevoir.

    Gardevoir characters are very often written as wise and motherly figures who are super-loyal to and super-protective of their Trainers. However, while certain characteristics may make sense given species characteristics, Pokemon are individuals, and can easily have personality traits that mostly or even completely defy species stereotypes; back on Gardevoir, you could make one a backstabbing, sadistic sociopath if you damn well wanted to and knew how to make it work. Besides, it's boring if all Pokemon of the same species act the same way.

    Though, the other way around also applies because...

    Pigs is pigs, but Charizard aren't Bronzong

    ...Turns out, sometimes people have an opposite-yet related problem to both the above problem and the "Pokemon acting too human" problem, where they assume two Pokemon species act the same just because they're both Pokemon. While, again, stereotypes should be avoided, each Pokemon species still has its own distinct quirks that would influence them as characters somehow, and it's pretty odd for mons as disparate as a Medicham or a Magnezone to have the same perspective on everything.

    Now for the whopper.

    Humans aren't necessarily "special"

    Yup, this is a whole section on handling Humanity Ensues with Pokemon characters. There's two main sub-issues I wanna tackle here, as such:

    Transformation Central Double Standard

    This section's more addressing a trend (which I feel I inadvertently started >_>) of Pokemon -> human transformations being historically more "acceptable" than human -> Pokemon ones, though that's gotten a fair bit better as of late. Note I don't necessarily mean relative commonality; the number of Pokemon characters in the RP's cast is greater than the humans, which means naturally from a statistical standpoint mon -> human transformations would probably be more common. The actual problem here is that there's a rather disproportionate number of Pokemon characters all too willing to be human from the start compared to the other way around.

    Explaining what I mean by that is covered by the next section...

    You sure those opposable thumbs were worth it, m8?

    You see... Tying into the issues above, a large number of mon characters are all too quick to forget who they truly are/were when becoming human, and they're immediately enthusiastic about being human and adopt human traits just as quickly as discarding all their Pokemon traits even when they don't have much in the way of in-character reason to, and just giving off a vibe of being human being inherently superior to being a Pokemon.

    First off, human superiority is definitely not what we want here for obvious reasons of speciesism. Second... tying into much of the above, becoming human for the first time would be awkward at best and horrifying at worst for most Pokemon.

    For an example... imagine you're an Aggron. A motherfucking Aggron◊. You can toss boulders, uproot trees and chew up metal with ease, you can Head Smash anyone who pisses you off with no problem, and can shrug off many blows while giving zero fucks thanks to your thick, metal-and-stone hide. But one day one of those boulders you throw inadvertently hits a Mew, and in retaliation the Mew turns you into a perfectly normal human.

    You look down in horror at your smaller, thinner, more frail body, your thick metal-and-stone hide gone, replaced by grotesque squishy naked skin and flesh. Your mighty jaws that can rend steel beams are replaced by small feeble ones with disturbingly blunt teeth. Your hornless, thin skull is no good for Head Smashing anymore - not that you could use it anyway, your powers have all been stripped from you, you're nearly helpless. Your claws have been replaced by feeble hands with no sharp bits to weaponize at all. All in all, you're shocked and disgusted by the abominable thing that you've become.

    ...Doesn't sound so nice now, does it?

    Things can get even more awkward depending on species. Seviper? Good luck dealing with no venom and a whole new body plan with weirdass new extremities. Talonflame? Good luck being grounded literally and dealing with using teeth instead of a beak. Golurk? Oh, humanoid, you're mostly fi- wait, what the hell is in between your legs?!

    Now, none of this means nobody can do mon -> human plots, or that a mon can grow used to or even fond of being human, or that they can't have an in-character excuse to wanting to give being a human a try - I'd be a huge hypocrite if I said so. It's that becoming human is a fair bit strange and complicated and takes some getting used to, and it's something that generally needs more acknowledgement with Humanity Ensues in this RP.

    And that's it for the actual concerns. However, before closing this off I'd like to make two big caveats about all of them:

    The RP's called "We All Pokemon Trainers", yo

    Many players have expressed that they're more comfortable writing their human characters over their Pokemon ones because, as mentioned earlier, we're all human. So, while we still heavily recommend you give your mons some focus even if the humans get most of it, this isn't a demand to write for your mons more than you feel comfortable with - just some stuff to keep in mind whenever you do write for them.

    Second's a bit more personal...

    Humans aren't necessarily "special"... But they're still fucking important.

    Yeah, while I mentioned above treating humans as superior to Pokemon is bad, the other way around is similarly icky - as kinda mentioned at the beginning Seishirou and Fuuma found out the hard way... More than once. I've seen trends in another Poke-writing community I follow that give me vibes of people there viewing humans as a waste of space in the Pokemon world - including people there I otherwise view as close friends - and that really deeply worries me because this RP and much of the franchise as a whole treats humans and Pokemon as companions and equals that are better off with than without each other, and that's something I personally have grown to deeply value, to the point some future fics of mine have elements explicitly designed to kick the "humans are a waste of space" mentality in the 'nads. So yeah, this post isn't saying Pokemon are superior to humans - it's just advising on making the Pokemon more themselves so their interactions with said humans are more dynamic and interesting.

    But that's all. Feel free to ask any questions; me, Crow, and possibly Tagg and Silent will be all too willing to answer.

    I don't really think pseudonyms are a problem (and I'd be a hypocrite if I did.)

    Though, if you're worried about it comes off, try and make it more nuanced.

    I guess what I'm worried about is mon->human transformation getting portrayed entirely as a positive experience with no drawbacks that any Pokemon would do, given the chance. To use Rembrandt as an example... he clearly has a fair number of reasons to want to be human, but try to not just think of it in terms of benefits, but also in terms of drawbacks to make it not so much 'There are no drawbacks to being human' as 'There might be some things I miss about being a Sylveon, but I overall prefer being human.'

    Also, try giving dissenting opinions. Rembrandt's team members or other positively-portrayed Pokemon acquaintances making it clear they don't know if they'd be willing to go human like Rembrandt did. It makes it come off a bit more like 'I'm a human because I want to be human' instead of 'I'm a human because Pokemon want to be human.'

    They might seem like small or insignificant things, but those kind of things give mon->human plots more credence, IMO.

    Also, this is admittedly more a 'me' thing, but for Pokemon that become humans or humans that become Pokemon, try not to have them completely take on the behavior of one species or the other, unless they outright hate what they used to be and never want to be it again. I guess, ideally in most cases, you couldn't look at the character and say 'If I had introduced them as their current species, they wouldn't be any different from how they are now.'
