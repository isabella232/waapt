    Okay, with the interest in a Pokesona Plot being rather high, I'm going to go ahead and post an abbreviated primer here for Final approval.

    So with out Further ado, here we go....

    What is the Pokesona Plot?

    The Pokesona plot is meant to be a mini side-plot that will run along side the main plot similar to how the HTYD side-plot ran to the Hoenn-2 arc. The events of the Pokesona Plot will not have any majorly noticeable effects on the main plot until End Game - which is how almost every Persona game has worked to date - so the PCs not involved won't feel forced to join in.

    The Pokesona Plot itself will draw inspiration from the Persona Series as a whole but the plot and its Flow will draw mainly from Persona 4: Golden (P4G). For Those who have never played P4G, or don't have time and/or a way to play it, or watch an LP; I highly recommend you take the Time to watch Persona 4: The Animation (P4A) and Persona 4: The Golden Animation (P4GA). P4A covers P4 as a whole, while P4GA covers the new events that occurred in P4G. Protip: Because if how the both of animes actually show the dates of when stuff happened, you can actually watch both series at the same time and get a better grasp of the story as a whole.

    People who haven't played any games in the Persona series - or any Shin Ten series for that matter- please be aware that the series' plots have a very large scope, and to diminish the scope would be a disservice to the series. But to prevent plot issues, I have reigned in the scope of the Pokesona Plot a bit to make sure it's more isolated and won't directly affect the main plot we have going - unless for some bizarre reason we want it to.

    Who is running the Plot?

    I have already asked Dune to be my number 2 on helping me run the side-plot smoothly. If others want to help, contact Dune or I and we'll talk.

    So what is the overall story?

    A Legendary - most likely one of the Lake Trio - asks a group of J-Team members to help deal with what seems to be a minor problem - an entity that it locked away in the Collective Unconsciousness (the CU from now on) a VERY long time ago has broken out of its "cage". Fortunately, the entity is weak after breaking out, so there is no reason to get the Whole J-Team involved in the problem. But the group will need help because battles can't be fought through conventional means in the CU. So the Legendary helps the group get to the Velvet Room, and get aide from the Room's inhabitants. To Properly fight the entity properly in the CU, the group must use Personas and Pokesonas. The occupants of the Velvet Room agree to aide the group and give each member of the group a Key that will allow the holder to use the signature Portal Doors of the Velvet room to travel in between the Physical Realm and the CU. Hence the Velvet Room will act a hub for the Pokesona Plot. However it eventually starts to become apparent -read, "literally right before the final stretch of the Plot"- that the entity is much stronger that the Legendary originally thought so the Group is forced to fight the Big Bad of the Pokesona Plot without the full J-Team to back them up.

    What is the Difference Between a Persona and a Pokesona?

    On a Functional level, there really is no difference between a Persona and a Pokesona, but here is a rundown of what they are (note that there can be exceptions to the following rules):

    • A Persona is an anthropomorphic manifestation of a human's ego.
      • Personas will take the form of a human character's Borked Pokeform. Note: a Borked Pokeform is when a human PC's pokeform transformation doesn't fully take, which results in the PC becoming what is essentially a Petting Zoo Person.

    • A Pokesona can mean one of two things:
    1. A manifestation of a Pokemon character's ego.
    • This type of Pokesona is unique to a given Pokemon Character
    • A Pokeseona can be an "altered" version of the the Character's evolutionary stage or their evolutionary line (e.g. A Turtwig's Pokesona can be a Torterra with hover jets for legs and have tree-like cannons on its shell)
    • A Pokesona Can be an "altered" version of a Pokemon/ evolutionary Line that the character identifies with (e.g. A Turtwig's Pokesona can be a Sawk in Full plate armor that wields a spear.)

    2. A manifestation of power that comes from within a human's or a Pokemon's soul.
    • This type of Pokesona are generic and are not restricted to a specific Character whether human or Pokemon
    • This type of Pokesona are generally weaker than a Pokesona unique to a Pokemon character.

    How do you get a Persona or Pokesona?

    As far as the Pokesona Plot is concerned, characters will get Personas and Pokesona in one of two ways. The first way is a character can receive use of a generic Pokesona from an appropriate NPC, or said NPC can directly unlock a Character's Unique Persona or Pokesona.

    The second way is for a character to accept their Shadow Self (Trivia: "Shadow" is a catch all term for the enemies in P3 and P4, while "Shadow Self" refers specifically to the anthropomorphic personification of a Character's personal issues.). This is a LOT harder than it sounds, as if a character denies their Shadow Self - and by extension, deny the fact that they have personal issues - their Shadow Self will get stronger and will eventually gain enough power to become a separate being.

    If a character denies their Shadow Self at this point, the Shadow Self will enter a berserk state, transforming into a Pokemon-based monster-like thing and actively attack all present with the intent of killing the original host and anyone who tries to prevent that. At this point, a boss battle will occur and the berserking Shadow Self must be defeated, forcing it to revert to its normal form in a highly weakened state. If a character continues to deny their Shadow Self at this point, the Shadow Self will get stronger and the process begins again. Although once accepted by a character, the Shadow Self will transform into the Character's Persona or unique Pokesona.

    One big thing I'll go ahead and note here: NONE OF OUR CHARACTERS WILL HAVE "THE POWER OF THE WILD CARD" AS SEEN IN P3, P4, OR PQ! The ability it far to overpowered - even the watered down version seen in PQ is too exploitable - for an entire group of P Cs to have, and just giving it to one PC makes said PC a little too special. So only one Persona or Pokesona per PC at a time.

    How do Personas/ Pokesonas and combat work?

    Due to the properties of the UC, using Personas and Pokesonas is the only effective way for any character that is not at least on the power levels of a Mega Latii to fight hostile forces in the UC. This means that even fully evolved Pokemon and talented Aurics and Obscurists like RP!Tagg, Duke, Sol, and Lina wouldn't be much more effective than a normal human. Due to this, Personas and Pokesonas are needed for combat.

    In combat, Personas and Pokesonas function identically to each other; They use a the movepool and ability of the mon that they are based on. Also, once a Persona's or Pokesona's ability is set, it can not be changed - even if the user's ability changes

    Personas and Pokesona are linked to the user's mind and body. As such, Personas and Pokesonas can be commanded to move and act by thoughts and subvocalization as well following the user around if not being actively commanded.

    But because of this link, the user will feel what their Persona or Pokesona feels; the pain, exhaustion, and to a minor degree, physical feedback. And while no physical harm can be inflicted directly on a Persona/ Pokesona user via the link, the user can still suffer injury by being attacked directly, being hit by debris, or if the the physical feedback causes the user to hurt themselves or be knocked into something.
    • e.g. Tying a Persona or Pokesona's legs together will also cause the user's legs to be bound too, but inflicting a burn on a Persona or Pokesona will not inflict a physical injury on the user unless the pain of burn causes the user to fall or bump into something.

    What do you need do if you join in on the plot?

    First off, everyone will need to write for their Character(s) Shadow Selves, the berserked Shadow Selves, and small battles leading up to that point.

    Next, be prepared to to give feedback if asked. The second part of the plot will be run by myself and the others helping me run the plot - and we'll need the participants input to make things go smoothly.

    Finally, be prepared to have all transformations nullified while a PC is in the CU. As we're going with P4's theme of "Truth", all PCs will revert to their "original state" while in the CU.
    • e.g. Dezi would revert to Magnezone!Galvani once she entered the UC and would not transform to her Human state until she entered the Velvet Room or returned to the physical world; or a Gender-flipped Pent with strawberry-blonde hair thanks to his amulet would revert back being his male body with his normal hair color while in the CU.
      • Please note that this doesn't mean that the CU removes transformations, it just negates the effects of a transformation.
        • e.g. If Sholto enter the UC he would revert to Human!Crow while in the CU and revert back to Sholto once he left because he still has Murkrowvirus; or if Dezi entered the UC as a human, and activated her bracelet while she was there, she wouldn't change into her human form as her bracelet had already transformed her back into a Magnezone - even if there was no visible cue for this.

    What do you do to Join?

    I'll post another D!Post detailing what you need to do if this gets approved.

    What do you get for joining and Participating to the end?

    Well other than the satisfaction of a job well done, I proposed the idea to Dune and Verax and the agreed with me.

    During the Final Battle, our PCs' Personas and Pokesonas will evolve into their Ultimate Personas and Ultimate Pokesonas, gaining a signature skill in the process. My idea was as a reward for participating all the way through, our PCs will be retain able to use these moves when in their Pokeform in the real world.

    Obviously, the moves will have to be approved, and thus can't be too broken. And like certain moves IRP, can't be used in official and league-approved events.

    For example, Ren's signature move, as his Pokeform is a shiny Delphox, would be a more powerful version of Mystical Fire - Mystical Meteor. Fitting in with Ren's shtick of being able to appear to be stronger than he actually is, Mystical Meteor only looks like a huge, 300 BP AOE Fire attack - but in actuality it's just a single-target 90 BP Fire-type special attack that lowers the opponents Sp. Att if it connects.

    The logic for this is that most involved in the plot would not be able to use these moves on a regular basis - baring another PMD arc - as most PCs lack a reliable method of changing into their Pokeform, and those that do have a reliable method wouldn't be able to use it for most battles except for special occasions or fight-for-your-life scenarios where the normal league rules don't apply.

    Any thoughts or concerns before I ask for a vote of approval?
