    The eternal dusk returns
    OK This is something Ive actually been planning for nearly a year for Pokemons 20th anniversary, and full well intended on dropping it multiple places, so while many of you already saw it those places, and I dropped an in-RP counterpart as well, I figured I'd drop it here too. Note this is also being shown to people outside of here who arent quite as familiar with Pokemon, so forgive my terminology lapsing to accommodate in places.

    But I humbly present

    Spark: A Memoir Of Monsters

    It all started with a gift.

    No, not a gift to me, actually. One to my sister. She ultimately got to share in it too, in a sense, but Ill get to that eventually in more ways than one.

    But the gift was one of a semi-transparent, lavender-colored Game Boy Color, along with some video game called Pokemon Red.

    Now, I had never touched a video game in my life before that point, aside from the simplistic edutainment games on the family computer, perhaps, if they came before and/or counted. But I decided to give this one a try, because hey, it had a pretty cool looking dragon-y creature on the cartridge!

    Its here I should probably note that since toddlerdom Id always been utterly fascinated with the animal kingdom. I would sit for hours on end reading books or watching videotapes of animals of all shapes and sizes, not judging or being too choosy about them, just marveling at them no matter what they were.

    And so when I booted up the game and chose my characters name and all that, I was awed it contained a world full of a fantastical array of animals, with powers over forces of nature beyond the creatures of our world, called Pokemon. And I got to choose one to be by my side for a whole adventure into this strange new world.

    That was the first spark. A spark of the imagination, one that drove me, compelled me to explore. Despite the dragon-y beast known as Charizard that caught my attention I chose a squat little creature called Bulbasaur to be my first Pokemon companion in this strange new world. But many others soon joined it, and they grew and changed as we all worked together to take on what came our way, help friends, thwart foes, and conquer impossible odds.

    But alongside that was a television series, one my sister and I could enjoy together, one where another take on that amazing new world could unfold before us in a silly, lighthearted way we could both enjoy, one where the humans and Pokemon could have both their personalities shine through.

    That was a second spark.

    And amidst it all, I found my fellow kids at school were amazed as well, played the games, watched the adventures unfold on TV, exchanged all these pretty little trading cards, (and simply gifted many to me, to my delight) and it was wonderful.

    And then came both a rise and a fall.

    A new era of Pokemon, first heralded by a golden phoenix of legend known as Ho-oh, came to be. And I was thrilled - there were new places to explore in this world, new faces to meet, new Pokemon to discover and befriend - my favorite being the mighty Typhloshion - and new adventures to both partake in and witness.

    But near the end Many others drifted away. Theyd grown to veiw Pokemon as - well, the phrases they used are decidedly too ableist and homophobic to say here, but from those descriptors youll see exactly what Im talking about - childish. For theyd grown and entered the stage of life where fantastical, good-natured things like Pokemon were childish and they had a desire to discard that and be very grown up.

    I was confused, hurt. Why would they do this? There was still plenty of this world to discover and explore and new Pokemon to learn about and befriend, and soon enough a third era came that let me do all that further! But by then I was far more alone; only my closest friends would share the experience. Even my sister drifted away, in a sense. And everyone else had either discarded it entirely in their new quest to be very grown up or lambast the new Pokemon as ugly, unreal, not true Pokemon. I never got that - I had never really judged any member of our worlds animal kindgom for such things - they were all just different! Why should I treat these wonderful creatures called Pokemon any differently, especially when the TV show had enraptured me with the idea they had complex, thinking minds of their own?

    But I felt more alone in my passion, and it grew more insular as a result. But memories of multiple Pokemon adventures of many sorts still resonated within me, and one day, as I was reading a fantasy novel, three characters, all different species of Pokemon I liked, popped into my head, and from there I began to cope by making up silly adventures of them visiting other works of fiction.

    That was the third spark, and one vital to who I am beyond Pokemon - it was the catalyst that eventually drove me to want to be a writer. And what happened next was a vital part as well.

    Eventually, in exploring the internet, I discovered I was not as alone as I thought; there were others out there, like me, who still cared about these creatures, this world, these characters, in multiple incarnations. And there were those who wrote such wonderful stories about them! Stories about obscure Pokemon - Wobbuffet, Grovyle, Galie, Chinchou, Slugma - and characters, which enraptured me in their expansion of what I loved about Pokemon, and even when they took far, far darker turns than Id expect from Pokemon, they still managed to feel at home in its world.

    And then came the fourth spark - if I was already making up both silly headstories about Pokemon and more serious original stories in my head, why couldnt I tell stories within the Pokemon world like these people?

    My initial attempts floundered. But gradually, my confidence grew, especially because in the grander scheme of things The magic was starting to come back.

    People were slowly starting to realize Pokemon was still pretty wonderful after all. Some even realized that desire to be very grown up was ultimately childish itself. And on my end I kept enjoying it as always, with me still discovering new stories, creatures and characters within the franchise that made me love it even more.

    And eventually, from there, I started finally telling Pokemon stories of my own. And that led me to discover friends, wonderful friends who shared that passion, whod been through much the same sparks of imagination I had - and eventually, I went right back around and befriended people who gave me that fourth spark and learned of the sparks of their own.

    And even in real life, I helped people rekindle their wonder. Several years ago, a young man saw Reshiram and Zekrom stickers on my old laptop; he asked if they were Pokemon. I replied yes, told him a bit about the two, and he grew excited, said he didnt know it was even still going, was from a foreign country (I sadly forget which, I think India?) and adored the show as a kid and was so sad it got taken off the air in his country very early in his run. And as we talked about it, he asked if we ever found out who the gold phoenix in the first episode was. I told him yes, it was Ho-oh, showed him some webpages about and pictures of the Pokemon. He was fascinated. I was just so happy he found out that mystery after all those years thanks to me and was so overjoyed because of it.

    Today, I have no fear of stating and showing how much I love Pokemon. I openly talk of my stories about it, chat up a storm about developments in the games or the show, show people my memorabilia - and most people accept it, and its wonderful. And today more and more Pokemon fans are doing the same - being open about how the franchise and the sparks of the imagination made them happy. Even my sister is being drawn back in by my enthusiasm to an extent - I hope to watch the show again sometime with her, or at least some of my favorite of its movies.

    And Its what the man who started this all, Satoshi Tarji, could feasibly ever want. He grew up like me - fascinated by bugs of all shape and size, collecting them and studying them. But as Japan grew more and more urbanized, he was able to do it less and less, and as he grew older he focused on programming and video game journalism instead

    Until he saw the Game Boy Link Cable for the first time and had a spark of imagination - he envisioned bugs crawling along the cable, and got the idea for a video game that would let people enjoy a recreation of the old hobby he lost - and do it together.

    He dreamed of creating something people would join together and be happy about And he succeeded.

    The video games he and his buddies like Ken Sugimori and Junchi Masuda released twenty years ago today caused a cascade of sparks of imagination like his, and from there the animation and comics and even card games caused more, and while they sputtered a while, they ultimately ignited a flame in the hearts and souls of millions, greater than those of even Charizard and more akin to the Blue Flare of Reshiram - and its burned ever bright within me.

    Pokemon is something deeply important to me. Its positive impacts on who I am as both a person and a writer are always standing strong just as the sun and moon are always standing tall in the sky. Some of the games introduced me to my favorite fictional character ever, who I deeply empathize with in all his virtues and vices alike. One of the TV shows movies taught me more about the importance of powerful platonic friendships in fiction than almost any other story. Pokemons taught me so much, about so many things, in so many ways, and made me many of my fondest friends. To me its more than some video games or a TV show or such - its something beautiful and special.

    So Heres to 20 years, Pokemon. Keep being the very best like nothing ever was.

    -Umbramatic,

    Lifelong Pokefan
