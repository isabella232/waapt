    So now I'm going to take a page out of Umbra's book and...

    Okay! So here's my Pokesona plot pitch post.

    Since to the basics of combat were covered in a previous Post, I'm just posting some basic Pokesona Plot questions here. And I'll probably have a primer ready shortly - even if this idea gets shot down.

    Basic Plot questions

    What is the overall plot?

    One of the Lake Trio (and possibly another Legendary or two) forgets about something they sealed away and it evolved into the Big Bad of the arc, and then they call in the J-Team when said entity breaks out of their little corner of the Collective Unconsciousness, with strength beyond "easily handled" levels - prompting the plot to be kicked off.

    Why the Lake Trio? And what the Heck is the Collective Unconsciousness?

    Because the spheres the Lake Trio deal with (Emotion, Knowledge, and Willpower) all have to deal with the Collective Unconsciousness, which is distinct from the Dream World in that the Collective Unconsciousness is a world made from thoughts, dreams, etc. ALL sentient beings in the Universe - not just Pokemon, not just Humans, and Not just from Poke Earth. One could go as far as to say as the Dream World and Mindscapes are part of the "stuff" that makes up the Collective Unconsciousness.

    So what is the Big Bad's goal and how do they intend to it?

    Well this goes back to the first question - remember how I didn't specify who or what the Big Bad was? Well here's the reason: Since I wanted to include elements of several Persona games and not just P4, I propose a vote for the Big Bad. All three have different goals and different plans and methods, but all three of the goals will result in a The End of the World as We Know It Apocalypse How scenario.

    P4's Izanami

    If we choose Izanami as the arc's Big Bad, her goal is to cover the "Phyical/Waking World" with a fog (that she is the source of) that cause people and Pokemon to ignore "the Truth" - i.e. it makes humans and Pokemon stop caring about their inner problems as well as the stuff that makes those problems worse - basically this would eventually result in the stagnation and the eventual collapse of sentient life. Izanami, seeing herself as a god, would take a back seat to and not actively involve herself directly in her plan, preferring to work through agents and beings she would vaguely guide into doing the job she needs done. Izami's Apocalypse How is Plantary Complete Societal Collapse that will eventually Poke Earth devoid of sentient life over the course of couple of thousand years.

    P1 and P2's Nyarlathotep

    Next up, Nyarlathotep, unlike Izanami is straight-up evil and his goal is to to harness the properties of the Collective Unconsciousness - primarily The Power of Hate an Your Mind Makes It Real /Clap Your Hands If You Believe - to unlock all sentient life's destructive urges - i.e. basically making all sentient life CompleteMonsters whose actions, even towards themselves, are soley driven by For the Evulz - causing all life in the universe to destroy itself as well as giving Poke Earth a head start by stoping the Poke Earth's rotation. Nyarlathotep, like Izanagi, also use agents, but actively empowers them and isn't afraid to get his own hands dirty. Nyarlathotep's Apocalypse How is Universal Total Extinction via Hate Plague. This would take a few centuries to a few millennia fully achieve, the life on Poke Earth just gets spared a slow, gory death along with the rest of the universe in favor of everything dying in a few seconds. Lucky us.

    P3's Erebus

    And the third and final candidate is Erebus. Erebus is another straight up Big Bad that is just evil. However, unlike Nyarlathotep, Erebus' goal is much more localized, and unlike Izanami's goal, it's much simpler: Erebus just wants to kill every sentient being on Poke Earth by a Yveltal-based avatar to Colony Drop the moon. And to do this Erebus just needs a single agent to make this plan work. Erebus' Apocalypse How is Planetary Physical Annihilation via an Mundane Plot that results in an Earth-Shattering Kaboom.

    • Personally, after having played or watched a LP of P1-P4, I'd honestly prefer Izanami or Erebus due to that their plots are easier to match RP!tone-wise. And while Nyarlathotep's plot is easily the easiest of BigBads' Evil Plan to justify J-team involvement, you run into scope problems and tone problems - which you can't turn either of those down because then Nyarlathotep would just be a glorified Erebus that would prefer a big, elaborate, victories so he can rub them in Legendaries' faces rather than Erebus' rather mundane plot.

    • The primary agent the Big Bad would use is that villain I had an idea for a while back - the one who takes the "Since do the right thing cause bad things to happen, I no longer do good deeds" stance.
      • So while I haven't got anything more than motivation and a skeletal backstory, I'll prioritize their development if this idea takes off.

    • Other than Erebus, who just needs one guy. Izanami and Nyarlathotep would most likely just grab whoever they need from the real world to act a a agent for them.

    SO HOW DO YOU EXPECT OUR PCs TO FIGHT ANY OF THOSE GUYS?!

    Well our PC's are sent on the Character Development bit of quest specifically to get the tools (i.e Personas and Pokesonas) to fight them on "a level that doesn't result in the Big Bad handing out a Curb-Stomp Battle, but still strong enough to still wipe our PCs from existence if our PCs lose". This is where an idea Dune gave me comes in, sure the Legendary could have pulled a Philemon and just GIVEN everyone a Persona or Pokesona, but not facing your inner problems tends to lead to problems with world-saving teamwork down the line.

    Okay... So how does being in the Collective Unconsciousness affect or PCs?

    Basically, partially being made up Mindscapes and the Dream World, the Collective Unconsciousness combines the fact that humans and Pokemon are given extra powers to fight on their own (Personas and Pokesonas) - but combat uses the over-the-top, laxed physics, mon vs mon battle style that the Dream World uses.

    How do we progress the plot?

    The plot flow is as follows:
    • IRP Plot gets kicked off
    • Our P Cs then get thrown into the Collective Unconsciousness
    • Our P Cs spend the rest of this Portion going through Dungeons and facing Shadows and claiming Personas
    • Next up our PCs under shorter, but harder to cross hurdles that Prompt their Persona's and Pokesonas' evolution.
    • Challenge the Big Bad and win an Epic Boss Battle.
    • Our P Cs beat down the Big Bad to easily handled levels by [The Legendary that sent them on the journey] who shows up in time to slap the Big Bad back into storage for another few thousand years and sets an alarm reminder to check on them every couple of centuries.
    • (Possible) Participation prizes MIGHT be given out be for our P Cs are sent home
    • The End - what more do you want?

    Niffty, but I've seen a few things that interest me as well...

    That's not a question, but I'll try to answer things to the best of my ability here or via PM and then add it to the Primer I'm making.
