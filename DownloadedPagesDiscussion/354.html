    OK, since this arc needs planning even more than Rt AU (though if we're going with the "Start Rt AU in December but keep Kanjoh going" option, we can afford to take our time WITHIN REASON)...

    PEFE Exploration

    But wait, what's a peefee and how are we exploring it?

    It's The Pokédex - Extended Fanon Edition - or rather, WAAPT's in-universe organization based on it. More specifically, it's PEFE HQ, an unbelievably massive building located on an ambiguously-located island in the Sea Of Kyogre.

    ...What is there to do there?

    More than you'd think. (This was probably one of the main hindrances in planning earlier - people weren't sure what was there.)

    Paraphrased/copypastaed form the log...

    PEFE HQ is a giant open underground facility in a remote island, with a lot of techy stuff going around. The place itself is built as a compartmentalized system, where modules can be shifted and moved around via a special railing system. Think Johto Safari Zone, only 188% more awesome, and with SCIENCE.

    Now, modules can contain various stuff, mostly related to research. There's of course accommodations and residence for the personnel as well. Some of the modules are habitats conditioned for various kinds of Pokémon. They contain small populations that are essentially wild, thanks to the size and resilience of the place. (One of those habitats at least has been shown to have a couple of fields separated by hard!glass panes and stuff, and in one of those fields for example there is a Victreebell population who nommed some silly Pidgey.) Some of our characters's mons (EG: Mine) could also be living there, in accommodations that so far have gone mostly unexplored onscreen. Some of the other habitats comprise storage and cargo of containers and stuff. Presumably there's separate places for supplies (eg.: food, mostly) and tech that is going to the outside (Pokéballs, etc). There is a LOT of experimental technology lying around, which could be a potential plot hook. There are even some modules used to store and contain technology that was left there when the HQ was taken from Pokefutures, an evil Megacorp. Probably    evil    kinds of experiments and tech. Those modules would be fairly secured, although once the power goes out there's not much of a problem.

    The cave the place is build around opens to an even larger cave system where Kyogre goes to rest sometimes. Pokémon are allowed to work and hang out somewhat in the storage and control modules. There are also conference modules where the staff gather to... well, conference. At least one of those has an auditorium. There's also Tangent's office. There is also at least one airport module conditioned as a helipad, and zeppelin docking is a thing. There's also a rec room and sports modules for things like Blitzball. :P There's even sections just for the J-Team.

    And presumably, there's even more stuff waiting for you - yes, you- to discover it, or even make it up if you run it by the group first!

    OK, so where's the plot?

    That is something that needs more working out... Here's what we have so far.

    An alternate universe's version of PEFE (Or Evil!Goggles or something)    steals    ALL the generators! This causes a massive blackout, which affects the entire PEFE HQ, but doesn't shut it down completely. (Possible plot point: At the same time, some universe-displaced Scolemis Goons get lost in the HQ, including one destined to help beat the Dragons.) The group must then help get PEFE HQ back to full capacity (and possibly find Chekhov's Goon in the process).

    So, if we're going to do this, we need maps of at least some modules. Anyone with any sort of drawing capacity is free to contribute as many maps as they wish. Relevant wiki articles could also use some expansion, and we can use PEFE-B (which has been explored) as a reference.

    So... Any questions, suggestions, and ESPECIALLY general plot or subplot ideas (I have a couple) are greatly appreciated.

    PSA: Creative, Social and You.

    Lake: <Hi. I'm Lake the Feraligatr from the popular Play-by-post game, We Are All Pokémon Trainers.>

    Stratus: <And I'm Stratus, from the same topic.>

    Lake: <Today, we'd like to talk to you about the use of the two main chats associated with WAAPT, and their proper use.>

    Stratus: <There are two main chats: Creative and Social.>

    Lake: <The former is for discussion of WAAPT itself along with creative endeavors such as Fan-fiction, Fan-Art, that Pokémon Class you teach at College, or those ice sculptures that you've been working on so diligently.>

    Stratus: <While Social is for talking about everything else, such as Pizza, Chocolate, Soda, French Fries...Mmm...>

    Lake: <Dad, you're drooling!>

    Stratus: <EEP! Sorry!>

    Lake: <The problem is that recently, Creative has seen a lot of discussion that would suit Social better, while Social has been going without much use at all, and the problem with this is that all the off-topic discussion is making it harder to talk about important R Ping matters, as well as reading the logs to find anything relevant that may have been discussed while people were away.>

    Stratus: <To better get our point across, we've prepared these two scenarios to help you better understand the issues here.>

    The Right way to use Creative

    Treeo: <Hey, I think the Return to AU Arc could use some more planning. Could we work on that, please?>

    Comet: <Sure!>

    The Wrong way to use Creative

    Treeo: <Hey guys, can you help me wi->

    Woolly: <Did you guys see the latest episode of Valiant Knight Skarmory?! I couldn't believe it when it turned out the Truck was still alive after he fell into that crusher in the last episode!>

    Treeo: <Uh...>

    Lake: <As you can see, off-topic matters only get in the way of planning important things for the RP.>

    Startus: <BUT! That doesn't mean we don't want to discuss these things with you!>

    Lake: <That's right! We'd like nothing better than to talk about the things you like with you. Which brings us onto another issue.>

    Stratus: <When peoplemons go off-topic, we eventually ask them politely to take it to social. But more often than not, nobody takes it to social and just takes it as a sign to stop talking about it instead.>

    Lake: <Not only do interesting topics that we would like to talk about get suddenly dropped, but Social ends up going almost entirely unused.>

    Stratus: <Observe...>

    The Right way to use Social

    Unite: <Did you see that ludicrous display last night?>

    Flare: <What was Wenger thinking sending Walcott on that early?>

    Unite: <The thing about Arsenal is, they always try to walk it in!>

    The Wrong way to use Social

    Kricketune: *Chirp noisily.*

    Stratus: <Far from ideal, wouldn't you say?>

    Lake: <The point we're trying to make is, we do want to talk to you about off-topic things. When we say "Take it to Social" we don't mean "We don't want to talk about it." We just want to discuss it in a more suitable place.>

    Stratus: <So to sum it all up, when you want to bring up something potentially off-topic in Creative, ask yourself this: "Would this be better in social?" If it is, let peoplemons know that you're talking about it in there and see if they want to join you.>

    Lake: <That way, you get to talk about what you want without having to worry about potentially disrupting RP planning or discussion. Everybody wins!>

    Stratus: <And that's all we have to say. Stay cool, peoplemons!

    ...So, did you see Valiant Knight Skarmory?>

    Lake: <Yeah, I totally did not see that plot twist coming.>

    Stratus: <I didn't even think that the Truck being Valiant's Father could even be possible! Shows what I know!>

    (TL;DR Version: Try to keep off-topic conversation in Social. We do want to talk about these things, just not in Creative where it could potentially get in the way of RP planning and discussion.)
