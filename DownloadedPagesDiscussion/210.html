    Twilight FAQs

    How does it work?

    Under twilight conditions, electronics don't work, and apart from Souls (Human and Pokemon alike), the Aura or Obscura sensitive of any or no Attributes, and Ghost types, every living thing in its Ao E gets turned into a wisp, which is aware of the twilight haze and the 'tombs, but not that they've been transfigured.

    Souls and the Twlight

    Souls of Silver are immune from the effects of Twilight, but are invisible to everyone except the other exceptions and those they've made bonds with. However, as a result, the Spiritomb are actively hunting for them.

    5234 TracerBullet2nd Mar 2012 11:03:21 PM from A Dark and Rainy Alley Get RP Mod
    Guess Who...?
    As I'm sure that many of you have gathered, today was a bit of a rough one in the chat... After seeing a critique of potentially canon-affecting worldbuilding pop up by Silent not making it onto the Discussion Thread (which in all likelihood could have prevented more than a couple headaches today), I have decided to put the current direction that I have been taking Johto's Legendary Beasts up in a more accessible location:

    Raikou

    Is presently intended to be depicted with behavior that trends feminine. Johto!Raikou is presently being written under the assumption that the character will be assuming a "team leader" position amongst the Johto Beasts, will be results-oriented, be a bit of a snarker, and have a bit of a mean and petty streak.

    Entei

    Is presently intended to be depicted with behavior that trends masculine. The character is presently intended to be assuming a "subordinate" position, and is intended to be a pragmatic, somewhat overly-serious character whose focus is on the duties expected of a creature serving as a herald of Ho-Oh. If Johto!Entei were a fighter, "he" would be the "boring but practical" choice.

    Suicune

    Is presently intended to be depicted with behavior that trends masculine. The character is presently intended to be assuming a "subordinate" position, and is planned to be lacksidasical, have a preoccupation with flash and style, and get on the nerves of the other Gerbils Beasts for perceived incompetence and lack of seriousness. Were this character a fighter, Johto!Suicune would be the "awesome but impractical" choice.

    If you're wondering what gender these guys are. My official answer is "Whatever you think they are." After seeing the chat implode today over arbitrary gender roles for legendaries (which as much as I hate to admit, I was also guilty of exacerbating), I have decided that the best solution is to take a page from Rich Burlew's depiction of one of his characters and write characters such as these with my own mental assumptions regarding who/what they are, while attempting to ensure that I do not impose said assumptions onto other players in WAAPT. From this point out, I officially do not take a stance regarding the official genders of legendaries in this RP and whether they are male/female/hermaphrodites/sandwiches unless A: They are gendered in the main games. (ex. Heatran, Cresselia, Lati@s) B: Word of Masuda says that they lean one way or the other. (ex. The Taos.) C: They are WAAPT-generated characters independent of any official canon (ex. Mini-Legends) or otherwise are characters that have a relationship with a WAAPT-generated character that is DEPENDENT upon them having a specific gender role (ex. Pleo addressing Lugia as "Daddy" or some variation thereof).

    If there are any objections to the direction of these characters as they exist in this post, please voice your objections. And let's continue to try and build ourselves a broader, more expansive, and most importantly enjoyable world in WAAPT.

    Thank you for your time.

    Cool Blue Magic
    I found this list of RP Combat rules and thought it'd be a good idea to share. Edited it to make it fit this RP more.

    1st Rule: Be fair and sane!

    You can't kill somebody with one strike on the head when you are standing at the other corner of the room.

    2nd Rule: One step after the other!

    To continue the upper example: You have to cross the room (1) to break through the defense of your enemy (2) and to hit his head (3). After each step your opponent can do something on his own.

    3rd Rule: Don't "do" but "try"!

    There is an essential difference between "I hit" and "I try to hit". When you hit your opponent, he is hit. He has to deal with that and can`t prevent anything. But if you try to hit him, he has the chance to dodge or to do something else. It is a matter of the first rule to give your opponent this chance.

    4th Rule: Your opponent decides the effect!

    When you managed to hit him on his head, it is your opponent's turn. HE decides the effect. If you feel your character has some sort of ability that allows them to do more damage than would be considered normal, discuss it with the group in either the chatroom or the discussion board PRIOR to the fight.

    5th Rule: Use your brain and don't add things while you are in combat!

    To add things you can't explain from your character's past is also a sort of lie. Think about what your character carries around and use those items regularly. RPers usually get their things by RP with others.

    6th Rule: Don't be too fast!

    If you want to escape from your opponent remember that you are still in the fight! This means you try to escape, but you are not gone yet. If you run away without respect of the this rule, you spoil your opponents RP-experience because he is not able to stop you IC.

    Okay, so I forgot to post this when we discussed it (irony), but anyway:

    We need to make a pact. Every time there's an important conversation in the Creative chat, please:

    A) Give the people in Social a heads up. Most of them may be in both, but there's always the chance someone isn't that would be interested.

    B) MAKE. A. DISCUSSION. POST.

    C) Change the topic. This makes it a lot easier for people looking through the log to sift through minor stuff (like, say, character development or minor derails) to find the big stuff.

    D) MAKE. A. DISCUSSION. POST.

    E) Make sure you realize just who's online. Plenty of conversations happen while people are asleep (because TIMEZONES), and they probably have an opinion. If possible, have the conversation when lots of people are on, or at the very least keep in mind that not everyone is aware of what you say.

    F) MAKE. A. DISCUSSION. POST.

    And just a note: It would be nice if a discussion post had a link to the log where the conversation happened, but at the very least summarize what happened. Don't leave out details unless they aren't necessary to know to be able to act on the conversation (i.e. if an arc involves exceptions to something, provide those exceptions in as much detail as possible, but it isn't necessary to provide, say, Pokemon nicknames or something, unless you really don't want to answer when people ask what they are).

    Don't just say "Someone post in Discussion" and then drop the issue. Assign someone to post (probably the person who started the conversation, or someone who volunteers).

    I know I'm not like, a mod or something, but time and time again I've entered a conversation where everyone knows more about the topic at hand than I do, because they were present during a conversation that wasn't posted in Discussion. And it's really, really frustrating, especially when everyone assumes you know as much as they do.

    Which brings up another point: DO NOT ASSUME EVERYONE KNOWS WHAT YOU'RE TALKING ABOUT, ESPECIALLY IF YOU HAVEN'T MADE A RECENT DT POST ABOUT IT.

    Aaaand... I think that's it. I think the Creative/Social chat system is working, but it could work a whole lot better if a few simple things are done.
