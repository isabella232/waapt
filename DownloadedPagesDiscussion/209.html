    On the subject of filler subarcs, there was one little thing that I've been meaning to formally make a statement of intent about for the last few months...

    The World Ends With Pokemon

    Namely this little subarc... Which as of present is intended to be run against the backdrop of us getting our souls collectively impounded via the ex-owners of that telecom that's popped up IRP.

    And that leads me to the crux of what I wanted to get at...

    Initially, TWEWY week was envisioned as a solo endeavor, something not wholly unlike many of the video game mini-arcs in the OI. However, after getting some feedback and statements of interest from other players in this RP, it made me stop and think about how to redesign the subarc so as to be open to other players.

    It was that, coupled with some observations of some other RPing endeavors that got me thinking... Could this subarc provide a way that we could sucker draw people who would normally not particularly care about WAAPT into our fold? There's clearly a community of RPers out there that are into TWEWY. The fora has seen a number of TWEWY RPs in the past. Coupled with a phenomena that Tangent has noticed of Pokemon making just about anything more awesome, I figured that it might present a fairly decent draw to outsiders...

    So that takes me to my proposal:

    Is it acceptable for me to use this subarc for the express purpose of attempting to recruit some fresh blood into this RP? Even if it's just for cameo purposes? If so, does it seem appropriate to make an interest thread for it in order to plan out its events and get folks integrated without derailing this Discussion thread?

    5224 TracerBullet27th Feb 2012 11:08:05 PM from A Dark and Rainy Alley Get RP Mod
    Guess Who...?
    Actually... All this talk of Pokebunaga made me curious about something...

    Pokebunaga Arc Tone and Direction

    While I will gladly say that a PxNA arc does intrigue me, even if we'd probably have to pull a bit to make everyones' ancestors warlords or that ilk... (Though considering how this setting has Reshiram and Zekrom ordering pizzas over the phone, it could be argued that that's not farfetched enough. :P) The overall premise of the arc makes me a little jittery...

    Nobunaga's Ambition is a game that first and foremost revolves around political conquest. And given Oda Nobunaga's rap sheet, it's something that can very easily be cast into a rather unpleasant light...

    Of course, from a game standpoint, it's easy to avoid these things. One can simply abstract away all the ugliness that accompanies armed conflict away. Your units can respawn, your enemies are there, and then disappear when defeated, on terms that your HC is free to define however it wishes...

    HOWEVER. We are an RP, it's literally impossible for us to dodge potentially unsettling implications via abstraction, which would force such an arc onto an extremely narrow tightrope act between veering off into rather dark and depressing material, and abstracted chessmastery off of the battlefield that could potentially cut into the overall coherence of such an arc.

    So... My main question is... If we were to do a PxNA arc... What do we allow to happen? How do we depict it? And how do we keep things from becoming a darkfest without blatant sugarcoating and while keeping things enjoyable?

    Some commentary would be greatly appreciated.

    5225 BadWolf2127th Feb 2012 11:16:37 PM Get RP Mod
    The Watcher on the Wall
    My chief interest in the arc was political intrigue. The alliances, betrayals, and shifts in the balance of power that occur between the generals (i.e. us).

    As for the issue of battles, combat is not the only thing that you actually have to do in Nobunaga's Ambition.

    One may transfer soldiers between fiefs, go to war, increase taxes (which causes a decrease in peasant loyalty which may lead to rebellion), transfer rice or gold to another fief, raise the level of flood control (which decreases productivity), make a non-aggression pact or arrange a marriage, cultivate (which increases productivity, but decreases peasant loyalty), use a merchant (to buy/sell rice, borrow funds, or purchase weapons), recruit for the military (soldiers or ninja), train the army (which increases fighting efficiency), spy on a rival, expand a town (which increases taxes collected, but decreases peasant loyalty), give food/rice to peasants/soldiers (to raise morale), steal peasants from rival daimyos, allocate military strength, recuperate (even a daimyo can get sick), turn over a controlled fief to the computer for administration, or pass a turn (hint: when one has no idea of what to do, train the troops.)

    When one throws in that Pokemon can be used in all of this, too, I think we have a lot of options without going too grim-and-gritty-war-film.

    EDIT: Oh, also, the opportunity to explore the history of the WAAPT-verse. That was high on the list of priorities, too.
