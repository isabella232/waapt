    OK, two things...

    From Here To Delta Episode

    As you all hopefully know, last night after over three months of illness-induced delays the Odinplot, one of my biggest loose ends I needed to tie up before I retire, is finally done! -shoots confetti-

    Bad news is that in the last few days of getting it done I've been unusually antsy in the chat, lashing out at people and/or panicking at certain provocations. I'm not exactly sure of the cause, but just in case I'm taking a break from the chat again.

    ...Not to mention this is a perfect time to do so because Serebii's doing another contest I've got a fun idea for that I really wanna write. However...

    Even with those two things I'm still gonna be R Ping as much as I can, (probably posting most when I've got writer's block on my contest entry), with most posts focusing on Odinplot aftermath, HTYD, some other shenanigans (like where Umbra and Ammy have been all this time) and various minor loose ends I feel are best done before the Delta Episode rather than after (At least some of these will be done with other people who'll I'll contact ASAP.). Additionally, thanks partially due to my long illness-induced hiatus, the contest entry is ultimately a lower priority; if I feel I can't juggle both WAAPT and the contest entry and that the latter is jeopardizing important stuff in the former I'll withdraw from the contest and (hopefully) finish my entry later to post it separately (which Tracer ultimately did with his entry to last year's contest). The likelihood of this happening will shoot up dramatically if the Delta Episode gets rolling before the entry is submitted.

    If I actually pull through with contest entry the chat break will likely end when I submit it. If not, it'll end once I've determined my latent stress level has lowered enough for me to not lash out at people and/or publicly panic on a daily basis.

    Speaking of which, one of those incidents gave me an idea...

    WAAPT Early Access Program

    We've had our queue system in Signups for a while now, and while it seems to have done its job of preventing large influxes of newbies causing the RP's pace to achieve ludicrous speeds, it also has its fair share of problems; even with recent laxer restrictions on how often the queue is advanced, it usually still takes several months for them to be allowed to sign in, and many lose interest and/or leave TV Tropes before we get to their place in line.

    Then Menoshe showing up reminding me that we've let people with no interest in actually joining hang out in the chat in turn reminded me of how Fletcha wanted to get to know the community first and we were A-OK with that as long as he still waited his turn to sign up. And I thought... Only fair we let everyone else do so, right?

    Thus, I give you: the WAAPT Early Access Program.

    The premise is simple: Anyone who goes through the proper sequence of expressing their interest and being added to the queue is immediately given the option of full access to the chat and possibly the Discussion Thread (more on that "possibly" later.)

    I feel this is a good idea because...

    1. It lets all prospective newbies get to know the community and the RP better, easing the wait, allowing them to possibly get more invested and more likely to stay, easing things up when they do get around to joining, and ultimately allowing them to know whether the RP's community is right for them before they join rather than waiting 6+ months to find out.

    2. This will probably rub a few people the wrong way, but... It may very well help weed out problem players better, since giving a person who stirs up trouble or is generally obnoxious/stupid/jerkish meta-wise the boot before they properly join is ultimately far, far FAR easier than figuring out how to deal with them after the fact, especially since in this RP permabanning is always the very last resort.

    Of course, something like this is definitely gonna need plenty of rules, and I've figured out some baseline ones:

    • Obviously, no actually participating in the RP until we get to them in the queue. They can read the thread and ask questions and stuff about the RP all they want, but no actual R Ping until they go through the whole shebang of the signup process.

    • Similarly, no getting wiki accounts or editing the wiki; though again they can read it all they want.

    • We probably shouldn't let them contribute to any major decision-making either; as mentioned earlier this could include being barred from posting in the D!Thread, though I'm not so sure about that.

    • Also mentioned earlier: Any prospective newbie that proves themselves enough of an annoyance will promptly be removed from the Early Access Program, removed from the queue, and barred from joining the RP.

    Thoughts on either thing, especially the second?
