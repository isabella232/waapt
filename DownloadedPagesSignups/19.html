    Gillespie Giacomo Garibaldi

    Age: Late twenties to mid thirties; hes a little reluctant to reveal his exact age

    Gender: Male

    Gillespie has, overall, an unkempt appearance; dark, long hair pulled back into a messy ponytail. His short and stubbly beard helps to soften his otherwise sharp and jagged features. He has a cleft chin, and a strong jaw, with dark green eyes that peer out from an almost constantly furrowed brow. He tends to wear loose-fitting, baggy clothing, but his arms and legs (and his torso, as well) are noticeably covered in hair, when visible. He is of a medium height, with a slender frame, and, despite from his apparent ragged-ness, isnt really anything special to look at. He also really despises the smell of an execrable effluent, particularly in the morning.

    Gillespie doesnt really have any affiliation with any organizations in any of the regions, or any notable people.

    Region of origin: Kanto

    Backstory: Gillespie led an ordinary life from his childhood up through his adolescence. Like most residents of Kanto, he had aspirations of being the Champion of the Pokemon League, and so ran off to play at the prospect, setting off with a Rattata. his dreams were crushed, however, when a tragedy played out at home during his delusions of grandeur, resulting in his mother passing away. Disillusioned, he released his Pokemon and returned home to help his father through the emotional time and got himself a full-time job working at a Poke Mart. Two decades, a mid-life crisis, and a thorough shove out the door by his father, Gillespies once again gone on a quest, though this time not for glory; but a quest to find a purpose.

    Species: Oshawott.

    Nickname: Wottanabe

    Gender: Male

    Ability: Shell Armor

    Moveset: Night Slash (breeding)

    Personality/Bio: Wottanabe hasnt been with Gillespie for very long, but they are already fast friends; they both have abrasive personalities, and despite Gillespie only catching Wottanabe shortly after arriving in the Unova region, they treat each other as though theyve known each other for a great deal of time. When Wottanabe and Gillespie are separated, Wottanabe behaves in a conspicuously similar manner to Gillespie; he acts tough, talks tough, and doesn't really back down when it comes down to aggression. However, he is surprisingly talented at reading the emotional states of others; particularly humans, and attempts to assuage the more negative ones. Hence why he let Gillespie catch him.
