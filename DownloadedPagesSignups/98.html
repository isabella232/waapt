    ~This Guy 481 : Crow felt it wasn't really enough of a problem on his end to mention, but in addition to agreeing with everything he said above I had a bit of a concern with your team.

    Once you actually read the Tone Essay you'll see a section on "crunch vs. fluff;" you'll need to, you know, actually read the whole essay carefully to get the full gist of it (and find some of the vital information noted above), but the tentative tl;dr of that section specifically in relation to my concern is that we're not Smogon, treating the RP as a hyper-competitive game that can somehow be "won" by steamrollering everyone is a very bad idea, and this the sort of more story-and-character-based RP where a lone Sandshrew can beat out a villainous Dragonite if he's clever and cute enough...

    ...And despite all that I feel having a six-Unown team may be going too far in the other direction depending on character focus.

    See, as it says on the tin, this is an RP about Pokemon Trainers, and they do Trainer-y stuff, including battling. As mentioned above, this is the sort of RP where an NFE can trounce a team of Smogon-Approved mons if they do it right - but trying to get away with stuff like that every fight is a tad godmoddy in and of itself, especially if your team consists of six Unown in particular. Ctrl+f We Are All Pokemon Trainers on this page and you'll see why - lone Unown are pretty much totally helpless, and even the Sandshrew mentioned above is a stage above the vast majority of the human race. And six Unown isn't enough to get to reality-warping swarm level - see the third movie or look up a Youtube clip of the Arceus event in HeartGold and SoulSilver for how many you'd need for that - and deliberately trying to invoke that is really, really iffy.

    Additionally, in addition to standard battles, this RP has more action-y bits, but the players still rely entirely or at least mostly on their Pokemon for the vast majority of those, and I'm worried if your player character tried to participate in those he'd be The Load and even if you're invoking that intentionally for "comedic" purposes that would have big repercussions both in-and-out of character (unless they gain access to certain special skills hinted at in Every's Essay - which you also need to read very carefully - and even then A. said skills are off-limits to brand new players, B. they won't save you from still being The Load against the RP's bigger threats, and C. As also stated in Every's Essay, outright prioritizing your PC over your mons as the combatant in a fight is outright against the rules barring extenuating circumstances).

    Now, if you want to focus on something other than Pokemon battles that lends itself well to it then having an all-Unown team is totally fine for that! But it seems from your signup sheet you do seem to have some interest in battling of some sort (even if as mentioned above it's currently expressed in a very disturbing way that's no fit for this RP) and as elaborated above an all-Unown team doesn't lend itself well to that even with the RP's quirks. So if you still wanna get in on the literal action while keeping the basics of your character intact or toned down where necessary... Try a mono-Ghost team? As Crow mentioned above we think part of the problem may be you not playing the main-series games or something since Generation 2 (That's the original Gold/Silver/Crystal for ya) and we stay up to date with whatever generation's most current (as of this post Generation 6). If so we've had players in a similar boat before that as Crow noted get by just fine by consulting Bulbapedia - the most prominent Pokemon wiki - as well as existing players for things they're unfamiliar with. On the note of both that and my suggestion for a change in team overhaul, a Ghost-only team could very well have a lot more variety than back in Gen. 2; It's still one of the rarest types, but you still have a lot more to pick from.

    Okay, on name choice...

    First, the words you're talking about are 暗い and 影, right?

    If so, that word is 'Kage' not 'Kaze.' (Even if I got the wrong Kanji the first time)

    Second, this is something that I admittedly overly nervous about doing myself, but unless you are a native Japanese speaker or proficient enough in Japanese (which, based on the continued typo, I'm guessing you're not) or can confirm from a native speaker that such a mashing up isn't a problem and I'm being unreasonable (and I can ask a native Japanese speaker about it next Monday if you want me to), please change it. You can look through other Japanese names that might evoke the feel you're going for, simply call him an existing word like Kage, if you want to stick the Kanji of the two words together, you can get An'ei (though that's apparently not a common word) or, if you want to mix together two words, do it in English and get something like Dardow, but it's better to not make linguistic innovations like that unless you have the knowledge of the language to back it up and be ready to change it if you're not a native and get something wrong, IMO. We don't have any native Japanese speakers in WAAPT, so we can't check against someone within the community itself, but that's partially why I feel we need to be careful about stuff like that.
