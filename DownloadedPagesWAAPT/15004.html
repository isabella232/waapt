    Imperial Commissar

    Fall City, Fiore - Midnight

    -Somewhere in the darkened streets of Fiore's major metropolitan centre, a young girl of around fourteen or fifteen with thick circular glasses and long brown hair so messy it defies several laws of physics is scurrying around the city, constantly glancing from side to side and occasionally twitching nervously. A Rattata is following behind her, struggling to keep pace with the hurried girl.-

    Girl: Aw crud, what am I gonna do? It's really late and I'm totally lost and Dr. Nanako's gonna be super mad when I get back to work... oh man, I am gonna be so fired for this...

    Rattata: <No you're not! You just have to stay positive, Zoe. Remember, life's like a box of chocolates - you don't appreciate it until it's gone!>

    -Sadly, since the girl is unable to understand Monese, her Rattata's inspirational(?) words fall on deaf ears.-

    Girl Zoe: How am I supposed to get back to the office from here? I've got no idea where I am and gah why did I have to forget my Town Map I'm such a dummy...

    ???: Oi, you!

    Zoe: Eeep!

    -She wheels around to find that she is being approached by two men dressed in uniforms that look like spacesuits as designed by someone whose only experience with space travel comes from low-budget sci-fi, an Amaura and a Trevenant accompanying them.-

    ??? Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: What's a girl your age doing out this late at night?

    Neo-Galactic Grunt A: Don't you know that this is private property? Clear off!

    Zoe: Um, ah... I'm r-really s-sorry, I d-didn't know... if you c-could just s-show me which way to g-go, I'll be on my-

    Neo-Galactic A: Too late for that now. We can't let just anybody run amok over Neo-Galactic's turf, y'know.

    Neo-Galactic 1: Sorry, kid, but we have a zero-tolerance policy on trespassing. Nothing personal. Trevenant, Power-Up Punch.

    Trevenant: <After thousands of years of oppression, we trees will finally have our revenge!>

    -He surges forward and strikes the Rattata with a hefty punch, knocking her back into a crumpled heap on the sidewalk.-

    Zoe: Ah! Mikasa!

    Rattata Mikasa: <For what it's worth, I've always been a supporter of equal rights for trees...>

    -Zoe runs foward to try and aid Mikasa, only for the two Grunts and their Amaura to block her way.-

    Zoe: ...W-why are you...?

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: You got caught in the wrong place at the wrong time, kid.

    Amaura: <Luck of the draw and all that.>

    Neo-Galactic Grunt A: Don't worry, this ain't gonna hurt. Not for long, anyway.

    -Zoe's hand goes to the other two Pokeballs on her belt, but before she can send out another Pokemon she hears a bird's cry from up above.-

    ???: <Face front, evildoers!>

    Zoe: H-huh?

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: What?

    -They look up to see the silhouette of a Skarmory descending upon them.-

    ??? Skarmory: <Justice descends upon shining wings to bring an end to your reign of darkness!>

    -The Skarmory swoops down, striking the Amaura with a Steel Wing, and as he does the purple-and-black-clad figure riding atop him jumps clear, sending out a Golbat and a Zoroark whose fur appears to be going white.-

    Cowled figure: Kaylee, use Haze! Fulgrim, finish off that Amaura!

    Golbat Kaylee: <Got it!>

    Zoroark Fulgrim: <Focus on your own battles, human, not mine.>

    -Kaylee releases a cloud of smoke that envelops the street as Fulgrim's Focus Blast effortlessly KOes the Amaura.-

    Neo-Galactic Grunt A: -coughing- What the hell just happened?

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: Can't see anything through this smoke...

    -Suddenly, the shape of a cowled figure comes charging through the misty haze.-

    Trevenant: <There he is! Die, tree-hater!>

    -The Trevenant fires a Shadow Ball through the thick smoke, which strikes the attacking figure... and causes him to collapse into a cloud of illusory shadows.-

    Trevenant: <...Wait, that's not supposed to->

    -He is knocked out from behind by a Night Daze from Fulgrim.-

    Fulgrim: <Subterfuge and deception. Two words I imagine you can't even pronounce, yet alone understand.>

    Neo-Galactic Grunt A: Hey, there's his Zoroark! Nail it!

    Cowled figure: Looks like you need to be taught a lesson about threat prioritisation.

    -A palm crackling with grey energy strikes Grunt A in the back of the neck, causing him to crumple to the ground.-

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: Dammit, just stay still, you little bastard!

    -He takes a swing at the outline of the cowled figure and catches him a glancing blow across the jaw.-

    Cowled figure: Urk...!

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: See? Not so tough when we can see you, are you? Now stay right where you are so that I can-

    -A vine-like whip snaps out of the smoke and wraps around the grunt's wrist.-

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: What the?

    ???: I'd be grateful if you could refrain from striking my partner in future.

    -A metallic sphere floats up to the grunt, spraying a dose of sickly green powder directly into his face.-

    Neo-Galactic Grunt 1: -spluttering- Ack! Get this stuff outta my face! I can't... it's kinda... wow, I feel really woozy all of a sudden... I'm gonna take a nap now ma call you when I'm back in Hoenn

    -He collapses, the hazy smoke around him beginning to disperse. The sphere full of Sleep Powder, having completed its task, floats back to its original owner - a shapely young woman in a distinctly form-fitting Roserade-green bodysuit with a helmet that resembles a white rose mounted atop a Tropius. The purple-and-black-clad figure strides up to her, annoyed.-

    Cowled figure: Hey, I had everything under control, Gaia!

    ??? Gaia: It certainly didn't appear that way from where I was standing.

    -She notices the terrified form of Zoe, who spent the entire fight watching with a mixture of fear and awe, and turns to her.-

    Gaia: You hurt?

    Zoe: ...N-no...

    Gaia: Good. Head to the nearest Pokemon Center and get your Pokemon tended to. I can have Foxglove here accompany you, if you wish.

    -Zoe shakes her head numbly, scooping up the unconscious Mikasa in her arms as she does.-

    Zoe: N-no, I'll be f-fine... t-thank you for your h-help, though...

    -She stares blankly at the two costumed individuals in front of her, before turning and scurrying away.-

    Zoe: (Oh my gosh they were superheroes! Real, actual superheroes! Aw man, I hope people will actually believe me when I tell them about this...)

    -Gaia, meanwhile, turns back to her companion. Now that the smoke provided by Kaylee's Haze has faded, he can be seen more clearly. His costume appears to be modeled off of a Lucario, complete with chest and wrist spikes, but in Crobat purple and black rather than Lucario's traditional colour scheme. Assorted gadgets hang from the utility belt on his waist. Only his lower jaw and grey eyes are visible beneath his mask.-

    Gaia: I would say that was a reasonably successfully exercise in teaching criminals to fear the name of the Auric Avenger. Of course, there are still a number of improvements we could make to our technique, namely-

    Foxglove: <I don't think he's listening, dear.>

    -Indeed, he isn't.-

    Cowled figure The Auric Avenger: Aw man, did you see that? They were all like "we're gonna do some evil stuff" and I was all like "nuh-uh!" and then they were all like "no way" and then I went all "ka-pow!" and "ba-bam!" and-

    Gaia: Avenger. Focus. A certain level of decorum is expected from someone in your position.

    The Auric Avenger: ...Right. Yeah. Sorry. Forgot myself there for a moment.

    -Beat.-

    The Auric Avenger: That was still totally awesome, though.

    Gaia: It might have been if you had come up with some better one-liners.

    -She nudges one of the unconscious Neo-Galactic Grunts with the heel of her boot.-

    Gaia: Any recommendations on how to deal with our most recent batch of troublemakers, then?

    The Auric Avenger: Hmm... I reckon we should strip them of their uniforms and equipment. Leave their Pokemon, though, I'm not that mean. Given a choice between reporting to Titan empty-handed whilst in their underwear or turning themselves in, I reckon they'll throw themselves on the mercy of the courts in a heartbeat. And if they don't... well, we'll make them regret it next time around.

    -Gaia nods.-

    Gaia: I suppose that seems reasonable, if remarkably lenient by your standards.

    The Auric Avenger: These guys are nothing more than petty thugs. Not worth bothering with. Most evil team members aren't actually evil, not in the real sense of the word. They're just playground bullies who never really grew up, like Moron and Meathead here, or people who've found themselves on the wrong side because they're unlucky or misguided. A humiliation like this is usually enough to set them straight. It's the ringleaders we need deal with more permanently. Monsters like Titan or Allen who don't care about how many people get hurt or killed so long as they get what they want... they don't deserve second chances. Take them down, and the whole organisation falls apart, the rot within exposed to the-

    Kaylee: <Sorry to interrupt, but isn't monologuing usually something the villains do?>

    The Auric Avenger: Uh... usually, yes. Again, got a bit carried away there.

    Fulgrim: <Or you simply enjoy listening to the sound of your own voice. A trait I've found to be rather common amongst humans.>

    The Auric Avenger: Anyway, we can't afford to stick around. I take it I can count on you to clean up here?

    Gaia: Naturally.

    The Auric Avenger: Great. We'll meet up back at base camp in a couple of hours, then. Take care of yourself out there.

    -He turns to his Skarmory, which has come down to perch next to him.-

    The Auric Avenger: Come on, Shrike, let's fly.

    Shrike: <No time to waste! There's good deeds to be done and evil to be thwarted!>

    -The Auric Avenger recalls Kaylee and Fulgrim, then hops onto Shrike's back and flies off into the night.-
