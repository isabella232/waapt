    Fall Town

    In an alley in the middle of the night, a rather short adult woman found herself cornered by quite the muscular man.

    "H-Hey, I don't want anything to do with you and your friend's little crime band. S-So h-ho-how about you just let me go so I can, I dunno, go to a bar and get drunk so I'll forget everything that happened here or something?" the woman said, looking understandably nervous.

    There was a rather big space between the woman and the man, but he was soon going to close in on her. Worse yet, he was holding a knife.

    "As if I could just trust you to keep your mouth shut like that. Sorry, but it's your fault for overhearing things you shouldn't." he said, with a small smile.

    There was nowhere to run anymore as there was a wall blocking her way and she had no means to defend herself.

    "S-Stay away from-"

    Suddenly she's cut off by a sudden Ice Beam hitting the man on his feet and the ice that formed essentially glued it to the floor.

    "What the fuck?!" said the man as he tried to break free.

    "Hey! Look here!"

    Both the woman and the man then looked up at the ceiling of one of the nearby ceilings...

    ... And there he was, accompanied by four Greninja and all of them with their arms folded, he then jumped off the roof, thankfully the house wasn't too big so it wasn't a very dangerous fall, he landed right between the woman and the man.

    "Now that you're glued to the floor, I guess it doesn't hurt to reveal myself now" said Shingeko, with a shrug.

    The man just stares at Shingeko for a moment before looking utterly baffled "... What, a midget in a costume? Is this a joke?"

    "Oh man, I bet this won't be the last of that sort of comment." said a female voice comming out of the helmet's communicator.

    Shingeko sweatdrops, but then shrugs again "I guess it doesn't really matter, after all this just means that my opponents are always going to underestimate me. So, now that you're stuck..." he then sees the man pull a Pokéball from his belt "... Of course."

    "Golbat, get rid of this bitch, both of them!" he said as he sent out the Pokémon

    "Ice Beam." said Shingeko, apparently to the communicator on the helmet after seemingly changing who he was talking to.

    One of the Greninjas on top of the buildings then fires an ice beam at the Golbat pretty much an instant after it was sent out, upon being hit it was pushed against the wall, but wasn't KO'd, something that was soon remedied by another Ice Beam from another Greninja.

    "Sorry sir, I don't quite play by the rules when I'm saving people. After all, this is hardly an official battle." said Shingeko, with a teasing shrug.

    "Seriously?! A Golbat? What's the deal with thugs and those things? Don't they know what variety for Arceus's sake! It's a Pokémon that evolves by happiness, which 90% of all thugs aren't ever, ever going to manage!" said Hikari through the communicator.

    Shingeko sweatdrops "... Leave the angry ranting for later." he said through the communicator as he picked a small bottle with some spores inside as the Greninjas jumped from the ceiling and landed right behind him.

    "Language!" Shingeko then threw the spores right in the thug's face, who ends up breathing them and falls asleep. The ice then breaks as he slowly starts to fall forward, one of the Greninja slowing his fall so he wouldn't hit his head on the ground too badly or something of that sort.

    "So... I guess the job is done..." said Shingeko

    -Beat-

    "... I can't believe this actually worked!" he said, looking very excited.

    "See? I told you the chance of you becoming a successful hero would be something like 70%, while the chances of you dying a horrible death would be like, 30%." said Hikari

    "No matter, I can't believe I'm an actual hero now and-"

    Shingeko then feels a Greninja poke him and then points towards the woman, who seemed to be in shock.

    "Oh, right." he then walks over to her, rubbing his helmet as he went "So... Are you all right, miss?"

    "O-Oh... Right, you saved me. T-Thanks Mr... Uh..."

    "Shingeko, that's the name of this identity."

    "Right. Thank you." she then breathes a sigh of relief.

    "You're welcome, are you going to be alright?" he sounded concerned.

    "Yeah, sort of. My heart is racing and I feel like I just ran a marathon, but I'm fine."

    Shingeko chuckles "Just go home, eat some chocolate and take a bath, should help you relax." he said in a lighthearted tone.

    The woman smiles "That works, yeah."

    Shingeko then takes out the Holo Caster "Just tell me what you heard that got you in this mess. I think the authorities would like to know and it saves you the trouble of being interrogated."

    "Oh, ok"

    Shingeko then records the testimony from the woman in his Holo Caster.

    "Okay, that should do the trick, you can go home now."

    "Right..." the woman then shuffles awkwardly "So... Maybe you could give you your num-"

    "I'm 16"

    The woman then blushes so hard that it almost looked as if her skin was naturally red "R-Right, that explain the height... I thought you were just a... You know... I'll just go now."

    "Yeah, you probably should."

    She then hurries off, walking as fast as she could manage, in the meantime Shingeko could just hear uncontrollabe laughter coming from the comunicator, and it didn't take long for some members of the Squad to join in too.

    "Oh man! I don't think this'll be the last time this'll happen either!" said Hikari, amidst her laughing.

    Shingeko sighs "Being a man sucks..."

    He then starts calling the authorities so they can take care of the uncounscious thug that was now on the floor.
