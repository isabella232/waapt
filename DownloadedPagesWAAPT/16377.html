    Pueltown Hotel, a room far removed from all other proceedings

    (Alex is sitting on the side of his bed, staring blankly at the screen of his X-Transceiver/Holo Caster/who cares it's pretty much the same anyway. Vattenfallet is curled up beside him, her head tilted in interest.)

    Vattenfallet: <I do hope I don't sound prying, but what exactly has left you so enthralled?>

    Alex: (with much deliberation) ...the J-Team contacts list.

    Vattenfallet: <But you've had access to that list for roughly eight months now. Why would you suddenly take interest in it now?>

    Alex: ...it's changed so much, hasn't it?

    Vattenfallet: <I'm afraid I'd have to look at it to confirm that.>

    (Complying with Vatt's wishes, Alex places the Mobile Device Of Indeterminate Make, henceforth referred to as the MDOIM, in front of the Vaporeon. She spends around a minute examining the assortment of names before speaking again.)

    Vattenfallet: <While I agree that there have been considerable changes since we first joined the J-Team, I'm not sure what point you're trying to make.>

    Alex: See... that's the thing. Is this the same team I started out with?

    Vattenfallet: <Alex, I apologise for being blunt, but don't be daft. See these names?>

    (She gestures to several names which I shall leave ambiguous.)

    Vattenfallet: <I believe these people have been here since your journey started, and unless something considerably bad happens, I doubt they shall be departing any time soon.>

    Alex: There's still all those others, though...

    Vattenfallet: <And I'm sure the others feel much the same about those who went their separate ways as you do. You need to appreciate what you have, rather than never moving past what you've lost.>

    Alex: (dead silence)

    Vattenfallet: <I hope my advice has proven helpful.>

    Alex: ...I suppose. (idly pets Vatt, who relaxes in response)

    (Within the deepest recesses of his mind, Alex briefly feels something resembling a pulsing, alien headache...)
