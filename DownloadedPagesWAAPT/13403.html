    <As for my identity, you can call me... Albert.>

    Sundancer: <That doesn't explain why you've disturbed my rest. Or what rest I can get here, at least.>

    Albert: <Well... I've come to try and help you out of your little... predicament.>

    -Sundancer lights up-

    Sundancer: <You mean you're going to free me?>

    -Albert gets a sad look on his face-

    Albert: <...No.>

    -Sundancer's expression becomes a peeved one again-

    Sundancer: <...Then to what predicament are you referring because I don't see any here.>

    -Albert's expression becomes nervous-

    Albert: <I mean, no, I can't free you yet. I'll need time for that.>

    <But in the meantime, I can help make it a bit easier, when I do come up with something.>

    -Sundancer's expression softens-

    Sundancer: <...How so?>

    Albert: <Well, could you try and pretend you're getting acclimated to things? He'll be less on guard if you do.>

    -Sundancer looks pensive for a bit-

    Sundancer: <...I suppose I could try. It's not like I have a better option at this point.>

    <And you apparently have some knowledge of Talonflame nobility if you chose to attempt to rescue me on sight without me having mentioned I'm a Le Bel.>

    Albert: <Er, well, yes, that's because... I happen to be a noble too!>

    -Sundancer gives an inquisitive look-

    Sundancer: <Really? Which house?>

    -Albert looks a bit more nervous-

    Albert: <The... Cains.>

    Sundancer: <The Cains...>

    <Can't say I've heard of them.>

    <But, I suppose that's mostly because we don't especially associate with Honchkrow.>

    -Albert looks a bit less nervous-

    Albert: <Next chance I get, I'll try and inform you beforehand.>

    Over the course of the night, Rex and Kim had a bit of an exchange with Eskay and Co. before leaving to find an inn to spend the night at. Of course, the nearest one was vaaaastly outside of their price range, so they ended up spending the night at the nearby Pokémon Center. It didn't have all of the usual comfort and amenities as an inn, but at least it was free.

    Early the following morning, Rex made his way into the main lobby to use the PC, with Kim still sleeping.

    Rex: I suppose I should switch up my team to let my new Pokémon get some experience.

    A few Pokéballs were placed into the machine and swapped out, new ones appearing into existence moments later.

    Aqua: <I don't remember you having a Drilbur before.>

    Rex: Well, she's new. I also got a Daruma- Ack!

    Surprised at the Vaporeon's sudden appearance, Rex turned around to see her sitting on the floor in front of him, smiling rather impishly.

    Aqua: <You should be less animated around your Pokémon, gramps. It'll draw less attention to your ability to talk with us.>

    Rex: I will when you stop sneaking up on m-

    Looking up, Rex noticed that a few nearby people were indeed looking at him, the boy pausing for a moment.

    Rex: Err, I mean... wh-who's a good Vaporeon?

    Aqua: Pore~

    Seeing nothing unusual about the current interaction, the people nodded to themselves before going back to their tasks, Rex sighing in relief.

    Aqua: <Being cute has its advantages, doesn't it?~>

    Rex: Why are you up and about so early, anyway, Aqua?

    Aqua: <Because, I had an idea on how you could cheer up grandma~>

    Rex: ...She's mad?

    Aqua: <Mad? Why would she be mad? You only stopped talking to one of your longest friends mid-phone call and never called her back for... let's see, how many weeks has it been?>

    Rex: ...S-so what was this idea of yours?
