    You Wouldn't Like Me When I'm Bored

    Terrera?

    -Sabaku finds himself sitting at a desert oasis, the night sky sparkling with stars. He looks up, and calls across the pool of water.-

    Sabaku: Is this what you meant?

    -A figure steps out from behind a palm tree. A familiar one, who's old armor glints in the moonlight. Sabaku stands up to meet his gaze.-

    Qaures: Yes, and because you didn't heed my words, the young woman from Paxlia has learned how this world truly works.

    Sabaku: That last one is a lie, and you know it.

    Qaures: Ha! Do not delude yourself. Strength is the only thing people like the Ghost Lord will understand.

    Sabaku: An you think that bloody conflict is the only means of demonstrating that?! All you do is sow the seeds for yet more conflict!

    -The older figure scoffs at his younger counterpart's words. The oasis melts away, and the two find themselves once again in the middle of a titanic sandstorm. This time, however, the winds are blowing against each other, with one wind supporting Sabaku and one supporting Qaures.-

    Qaures: A leader- nay, a king has every right to do anything with his kingdom as he pleases!

    -The sands shift, Qaures's wind pushing back Sabaku's.-

    Sabaku: You're wrong! That doesn't make you a king - it makes you a tyrant!

    -The sand rebalances.-

    Qaures: "Tyrant" is merely a label that the ignorant place upon those in power because they have none themselves!

    Sabaku: My, that sounds positively Ghost Lord of you.

    Qaures:    Don't you compare me to that upstart!   

    -From the sands, a massive, impossibly tall ziggurat erupts from the ground from under Qaures's feet. The older man stands tall at the top.-

    Qaures: He does not understand true power!

    Sabaku: And you do? Whether you admit it or not, you're just as deluded as he is, only with added vengeance for a perceived insult.

    Qaures: You're still a naive fool! You don't understand what a king must endure in his lifetime!

    Sabaku: ... Yes. I do now.

    -Sabaku plants a foot on the bottom step of the imposing pyramid.-

    Qaures: The people are meant to serve their king!

    Sabaku: A king serves the people!

    Qaures: A king is entitled to do as he pleases!

    Sabaku: A king is just as subject to the law as any commoner!

    -Sabaku continues to climb. Qaures becomes more vicious in his declarations.-

    Qaures: You let Alphonse die!

    Sabaku: His wounds were likely poisoned. Unless we were specifically expecting him to be poisoned, there was nothing we could do.

    Qaures: He died protecting you!

    Sabaku: And I won't let him die knowing that I backed down after he gave everything to see me live.

    -Sabaku is almost to the top of the pyramid.-

    Qaures: You don't know the first thing about being king!

    -Sabaku pulls himself to the top of the ziggurat's stairs.-

    Sabaku: A king's love...

    -Once again, the sandstorm collapses in on the two forms, consuming the giant structure in the process.-

    Terrera, Sabaku's Quarters

    Sabaku: ... Hrm.

    *Knock, knock*

    Tetsuho: Sir, we've received a message from Lord Kaius. You'll want to take a look at this.
