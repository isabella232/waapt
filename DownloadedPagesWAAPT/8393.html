    Ecruteak City

    After finding Deluge - who had eaten several wild Tauros by herself by the time they had gotten to her - Lake, Quin, Stratus and Ad Min had continued along Route 38 until they finally reached Ecruteak City, a town of antique architecture dominated by the almost Skyscraper sized Tin Tower, standing just to the north in sea of eternally autumnal trees.

    Deluge: <Wait, so they call the Tin Tower the Bell Tower now? That's a bit...stupid.>

    Quin: <Why?>

    Deluge: <Because now it's the Brass Tower and the Bell Tower! The names have nothing to do with each other! They had a theme going on before!>

    Lake: <If it helps, Mom. I still call it the Tin Tower.>

    While Lake, Quin and Deluge were discussing the importance of tower names, Ad Min flitted over to Stratus, who had been uncharacteristically gloomy as of late.

    Ad Min: Stratus? Are you alright?

    The Dragonite glanced at the virtual bird, but kept quiet.

    You've been awfully gloomy today. What's up?

    Stratus hesitated momentarily, then gave Ad Min a rather sad look.

    Stratus: <...I miss my bubble...>

    Ad Min: ...I see.

    Cocking his head quizzically, Ad Min wondered to himself if the fact that he was merely a simulated Pokémon who did not necessarily think the same way as an ordinary one was preventing him from being able to comprehend why Stratus seemed to be so sad that he no longer possessed a Bubble Shield.

    Because it wasn't making much sense to him.
