    A Bad Egg

    Mossdeep City. New Years Eve.

    Crowd: 5!
    4!
    3!
    2!
    1!
    A loud whopping cheer swarms the city as the night explodes with cracks and flares of multi-coloured light. Loud music bleeds out of every house and from various points of the crowd as the inhabitants of the island make merry and indulge in the good will of a fruitful new year. However, in the shadow on one of the far off neglected builds a figure looks on from the shadows.

    Mystery Man: Hmm. The lights are beautiful, though I will never understand why it's this particular day that gets people into a tussle with celebrations and hwhatnot. Hmm.

    The figure leans a shoulder on one of the metal pikes.

    Mystery Man: I never really saw much of a point to "New Years", or even put much thought into the concept of time at all. This time of year especially encapsulates what I find the most confusing about my own species. They set goals and lofty ideals and further kowtow to trying effortlessly to "discover meaning" within this world. A nothing more than an absurd prospect.

    He tuts as he watches the last firework explode into a flurry of colour.

    Mystery Man: People take way to much issue when confronted with the fact that their lives are essentially meaningless, that the universe cares little for them as a collective let alone as individuals. Unnecessary issue, might I add. The true fervour of life not comes from rejecting the absurdity of existence in the calming drug of hope and faith, nor does it come from the cowardly adherence to nihilism bemoaning the fact our lives have no inherent meaning. True joy comes from accepting the fact that we have no meaning, and spitting in the face of that. To create and forge our own meaning through our lives while knowing the absurdity in doing so.

    The man shakes his head and chuckles slightly.

    Mystery Man: But enough of that. I have no purpose here now, anyway.

    The man starts leaping his way down the building façade until he hits the ground at which point he makes a mad dash towards the sea, upon which there is a large Sharpedo waiting patiently in the shallow waters.

    The man quickly hops on to the shark Pokemon's back and grabs the top of the dorsal fin with his right hand.

    Mystery Man: Akheilos, we must move. Can't have the guards catch me once more. I must have broken a few of their rules or something.

    Akheilos: <Just because you can break the rules, doesn't mean you should sir. In fact, I propose that you should perform your duties where they see fit, rather than in acting in your own self interest. Such is not how we the moral should act.>

    Mystery Man: You know that we've never seen eye to eye on such matters of morality. Now if you please Akheilos. Your duty is to return from which we came from.

    Akheilos: <Using my own words against me? Tut, tut>

    The Sharpedo zips off into the moonlight, meanwhile a few of the guards and police are searching the area for a supposed thief that infiltrated the company building.
