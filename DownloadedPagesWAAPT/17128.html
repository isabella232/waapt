    Goldenrod

    Shingeko is currently standing on the edge of the roof of a tall city, looking down at the city. Without him knowing it, there was another figure behind him, slowly walking towards him while making absolutely no noise. Soon she was right behind the ninja.

    "Boo." said the figure as she poked the ninja on the back

    "GAH!"

    Shingeko jumped in surprise, losing his balance and beginning to fall. However he was soon pulled back onto the roof through his long scarf, falling on his rear as he had lost all balance.

    The figure giggled innocently "Hey! I managed to sneak up on a ninja! Does that mean I'm more ninja than you?"

    Shingeko panted a few times before standing up and turning around to see who was the newcomer. It was a teenage girl of brown hair and eyes, dressed in a black shirt and skirt.

    "Yeah. And I almost fell off the top of a I-don't-know-how-many stories building." said Shingeko, folding his arms with a tone of clear annoyance.

    "Would be quite the embarrassing end for a superhero huh? Falling off a building after being scared by a teenage girl." the girl smiled.

    Shingeko was taken aback by how casual she was being about the whole thing, he merely sighed and shook his head "... What are you even doing here?"

    "Well, I'm currently living in this building, so I think who should be asking that is me." she gave a smug smirk, before giggling and smiling cheerfully "Actually I already know. You're patrolling the city, searching for any villany aren't you?"

    "No points for guessing that." said Shingeko, in a more lighthearted tone.

    "So, you're the famous superhero I've heard about..." said the girl, looking at Shingeko's attire "Say, why aren't you wearing red?"

    "... Why should I?"

    "Because red is the color of heroes, silly." she giggled.

    "What? Blue isn't heroic enough for you?"

    "Just saying that maybe you could go for a costume change." she playfully winks.

    "Thanks for the concern, but I think blue fits me better."

    "If you say so." she then looked more serious as she once again looked at Shingeko's figure "So, this is a superhero huh... So cool..."

    Shingeko tilts his head slightly "First you criticize my costume, now you're saying I'm cool?"

    She wags her finger "Appearances aren't everything, you know! Blue or red, you're still a hero. The kind of people who puts themselves in danger so others can live in peace."

    "Oh, uh... Okay. Glad that you think of it like that."

    She puts her hands on her hips, looking puzzled "How could I think of it in any other way? You're living the dream of many people and I bet you're doing what many want to, but can never bring themselves to."

    "Yeah, I guess that's true. But you know, I can't do miracles. I'm not going to change the whole world myself. Everybody has to make an attempt to change things for the better instead of just waiting for it to happen." said Shingeko, in a far more serious tone.

    The girl looks slightly down as she thinks, gently tugging the collar of her shirt "Yeah, everybody has to do their part..." she looks back at the ninja and smiles happily "Well, I shouldn't be bothering now should I? While you're talking with me, someone could be getting robbed!"

    Shingeko was once again taken aback by the girl's words, but then nodded "Uh, right. I should probably go back to doing my job." he said as he looked back at the city

    She waved at him "Bye, Mr.Shingeko. Until we meet again!" she smiled warmly

    She then proceeded to leave the roof through whatever means she used to get there. By the time Shingeko turned around to say a proper goodbye, she was already gone.

    "... Well, that was new." he thought.
