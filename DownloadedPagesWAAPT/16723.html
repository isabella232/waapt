    Fairyland Picnic

    -a fairy runs over to the picnic; the source of the scream. He falls, and Joseph sees a large bite out of the midsection-

    -behind him, loping casually on his hind legs, is the Wolf. His face is matted, and three arrows stick from the wall of muscle that is his midsection. He is barely slowed down, running easily, loosely, directly towards Joseph-

    -the Queen, as always, doesn't give a damn. She doesn't even look up from her puzzle box. One of the more human looking fairies stands as the Wolf approaches, pulling out a sword that was a thin as a knife-

    Fairy: Something as animal as you is not welcome here. You fall short of even Pokemon status, and as such you must leave this place of joy at once. I can feel your magic thick around you, but mine is stronger.

    -the Wolf simply laughs; low, and dangerous. He stares directly at Joseph as he does, making the boy shiver-

    Wolf: It doesn't have to be stronger. My love in the red hood has a cold iron nail in his pocket.

    -the lie is enough to shock everyone into gasping. Even the Queen peers up for a moment, albeit much in the same way as a disinterested secretary. The mistake, of course, is that they look at Joseph, and not the Wolf-

    -a poor mistake. The Wolf lunges forward, dodging the sword, and opens his jaws. As becomes quickly apparent, the fairy is made of white chalk and glitter instead of flesh and blood, but dead is dead no matter what you're made of-

    -once the Wolf finishes with the fairy, he pounces, as easily and softly as silk, towards another diner at the table. Front claws dig into the long, princess gown, and teeth settled gently around a pale white throat-

    Elizabeth: Wha...what's going on? What's...happened?

    -drunk and hazy, she wriggles slowly. Cold fear grips Joseph, and he babbles-

    Joseph: Rattata said you'd find your way here. He said that the story called for it, and I should have known that you'd go after someone and I got myself attached and you felt it and...

    -the table isn't big enough. Joseph can smell the blood and fur and chalk, and he can see the animal lips pull away from Elizabeth's neck, and he can hear the gentle, loving baritone voice say:-

    Wolf: This is the story of my love. I followed you because I loved you, and I will keep following you because I love you. I have fought and killed and died for love, and I love you.

    -the voice doesn't seduce; it promises-

    Joseph: You don't love me. You love the hood, don't you?

    -the Wolf shakes his head-

    Wolf: Remember when we met before you came here? I wanted you then as I want you now. Love is not a perfect flower, you know. I have loved pure girls and boys, and corrupt girls and boys, but now I love you, Red Riding Hood.

    Joseph: My name is Joseph. Not Red Riding Hood.

    Wolf: Every Red Riding Hood had a name, love. I've done this before, and I confess I got jaded. Love is love, and blood is blood, and meat is meat, and candy-sweet faces are candy-sweet faces. But then I met you, and it all became new again.

    -Joseph stares, paralyzed. The Wolf barely even notices Elizabeth as he continues-

    Wolf: Let me get to know you, Red. Let me see your true heart, beneath your placid exterior. You are fair, and foul, and I love both sides. Walk into my embrace on your own feet, and make a choice. Or make another choice. Perhaps you'll get away.

    -claws dig into a dress, and Elizabeth drunkenly groans-

    Wolf: She won't. Make your choice.
