    Salvador watches the leaf stop in mid-air, before looking away slightly.

    Kostas: ...You're my son. The family protector and the guardian of my knowledge. Those are the responsibilities you have as the eldest child.

    Salvador: Yes papa.

    The get to the top of the hill. The pair stop, observing the cityscape before them.

    Kostas: Listen Salvador. I will share with you the pieces of knowledge that only I posses, but I am only telling you and you will listen and memorize it.

    The child blinks.

    Salvador: ...Only me? Not even...? Not even Odin?

    The man shakes his head.

    Kostas: Not even your brother can know about this, this is for you and you alone. You must never tell anyone this knowledge. You think you can promise me that?

    Salvador: Yes papa.

    The man knees down with a smile, and brings his son's hand to his cheek.

    Kostas: You are wonderful, Salvador. I know deep down that a boy like you would never disappoint his father.

    He places his hands on the child's shoulders.

    Kostas: You are such a handsome child.

    Salvador. You will be taking on an enormous burden for me. So I want to give you something in return, anything you want. All you have to do is ask, and I will give it to you.

    He pats his sons head.

    Kostas: You are such a good son, Salvador.

    Back to the now.

    Salvador: So... that's how it's done? I think I get it. May I have a try, s'il vous plait?
