    429472 PippingFool17th Nov 2014 04:59:36 PM from A BEAUTIFUL DUWANG Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I get a feeling so complicated...
    A Bad Egg

    Stormchaser

    Down one of the many corridors of the Stormchaser, the door to one of the many swelling rooms is adorned with burning incenses and what appear to be prayer cards and rosary beads. Inside, the sizeable bedroom and chapel has been converted into a temporary embalming morgue, complete with two mortuary slabs currently occupied by two hollowed corpses. On the various shelves in the bedroom that used to house religious paraphernalia, there are various viscera being pickled in a series of jars. On a table in the room's corner is an assortment of various types of embalming chemicals, tools, sweet incenses and oils and a strange assortment of coloured chalks and small sacred objects of sorts. The room looks more like some sort of eerie experimental lab than it does a bedroom.

    Salvador: (Twirling a syringe in his gloved fingers) Aah. It's been a good long while since I've been able to operate on bodies like this.

    He jabs the left arm of Lucius with the syringe and depletes it's contents.

    Salvador: To be honest with you Shaka, I was beginning to miss it.

    Shaka - who has been reading a book while Salvador works on the bodies - looks over at Salvador and shakes his head with a heavy sigh.

    Shaka: I'm beginning to wonder if this morbid and rather unsettling fascination and enjoyment derived from death is hereditary. I wouldn't consider this almost... cheery attitude to working on the eviscerated corpse of one of your contemporary's father exactly "normal".

    Salvador fills the syringe with more of the strong smelling liquids as Shaka laments.

    Salvador: Shaka, please. At least I've found a healthier outlet for such a thing - if what you say is true - than my father in his youth. I don't murder people, I just work on the bodies other people murdered~

    Shaka blinks as Salvador jabs Alice's right arm with the concoction.

    Salvador: There. That should do it.

    Salvador wipes his brow and takes a swig of a water bottle sitting on it's own table - while observing the levels of his various embalming chemicals.

    Salvador: Well that's done. I will say that this exercise has completely drained me of humectants and formaldehyde, I'll probably have to restock them soon. Hmm...

    Shaka: I'm pretty sure that if you gave a message to Tagg or Mr. Clavis, they would be able to supply with with all the potions you need.

    Salvador: Indeed.

    Salvador dusts his hands.

    Salvador: Well now. It's all fine and dandy that we have the fluids in the remaining tissue, but now we need to spread it out more easily. Shaka?

    Shaka: (Placing the book down) Of course.

    Shaka and Salvador stand beside either slab as they activate their psychic powers, and begin pulling the embalming liquids within the corpses of Lucius and Alice. As the liquids are pulled and spread throughout the corpses, the wrinkles of the dehydrated tissue are "ironed out". Making both look more like they did in life.

    Shaka: (While moving around the liquids) I must say, it's a real testament to your natural capabilities that you're able to pull this off, Salvador. As you said earlier, I am thankful that you perform these techniques on the dead rather than the living...

    Salvador: (Also pulling) People Puppets aren't my thing, honestly. Though - as father would say- sometimes dire situations would turn even saintly folk into sinners...

    Shaka: ...

    After a while, the fluids are evenly distributed and the bodies look much more full - or, as full as they could be at the moment with all their organs removed.

    Shaka: Good job.

    Salvador: Thanks

    Salvador takes another drink from his water and walks over to the table, picking up a bowl of oils, what appears to be a brush of some kind, and some of the chalk. He walks over to the bodies and begins chanting prayers in a dead language, marking the bodies with sacred symbols before brushing them with the oils

    Shaka: So. What's the next step?

    Salvador: (In between chants) Skin dyeing definitely. They were pasty-white in life, not something closer to my natural tone.

    Shaka: Ah, I see.

    Shaka returns to his bed and picks up his book again, turning to his dog-eared page while Salvador resumes his ritual.
