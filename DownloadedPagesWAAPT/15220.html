    Imperial Commissar

    Rasp Cavern

    -Gaia is sitting in front of the computer in her and the Auric Avenger's quasi-Batcave, looking over a virtual map of all known Illusion machine locations in Fall City, when a warp tile behind her activates. The Avenger himself steps through, looking rather battered and exhausted.-

    The Auric Avenger: Phew, talk about a long work day... I swear, for a place so close to the Ranger Union, Fall City has an absurdly high crime rate. Though sometimes I feel more like a babysitter than a vigilante...

    -Gaia doesn't respond, prompting him to slump into the chair next to her and lean over her shoulder.-

    The Auric Avenger: So, I heard there was a bit of a commotion yesterday.

    Gaia: Correct. There was a break-in at the city museum, perpetrated by two masked individuals. I was able to prevent the theft but those responsible managed to escape before I could apprehend them. The crime in itself was not particularly unusual, save for two outstanding factors. Firstly, the object they were attempting to steal was the Hechs Editor, an ancient writing tool with some admittedly unusual properties - but not an object of any significant function or value. Secondly, I was not the only individual at the scene who made an effort to intervene. The vigilante known as Shingeko was also present.

    -The Auric Avenger gives a whistle.-

    The Auric Avenger: Hoo boy... how did that go, then?

    Gaia: Poorly. Neither of us was expecting the other's presence, which meant that we merely obstructed each other's efforts and the resulting confusion allowed our quarry to escape. In addition, I found both his attitude and his handling of the situation to be highly unprofessional.

    The Auric Avenger: Look, I'll freely admit that the guy can be a bit of a loudmouth from what I've seen of him, but could you at least try to play nice with Shingeko? We are on the same team now, you know.

    -Gaia blinks beneath her mask.-

    Gaia: ...What?

    The Auric Avenger: Er... sorry, I might've forgotten to tell you about that. See, I ran into Shingeko a few days ago, and we kinda... well, long story short, we decided that we were better off cooperating rather than competing with each other.

    Gaia: ...I see no reason why we should validate the efforts of amateur imitators with less training and equipment than ourselves.

    The Auric Avenger: Hey, better we help them out than leave them to get picked off one by one. Besides, the two of us aren't going to take down Illusion and Neo-Galactic on our own. We could use a few extra hands around the place.

    -He rubs his neck and elects to change the subject, deciding that now is probably not the best time to mention that he also enlisted a sidekick.-

    The Auric Avenger: Speaking of amateurs, I ran into another wannabe hero last night. Seems like we started something of a trend.

    Gaia: Did you happen to invite them to join forces with us as well, by chance?

    The Auric Avenger: Nah, this guy really was an amateur. Had to save him from becoming some Neo-Galactic tough's literal whipping boy. Though he was an Auric, so I guess he's worth keeping an eye on, if only as a potential security risk.

    Gaia: That should be unnecessary. Our Aura Disruptors are supposed to prevent such situations from becoming an issue.

    The Auric Avenger: ...Wait, what Aura Disruptors?

    Gaia: ...You did read the owner's manual that came supplied with our costumes, correct?

    The Auric Avenger: Nobody actually reads those things!

    -Gaia sighs and facepalms.-

    Gaia: Well, I did. Both of our costumes have Aura Disruptors built into them courtesy of our suppliers. They act similarly to Aura Jammers, only on a much smaller scale. The extent of their reach is about one inch away from the fabric of our costumes. So long as they are active, we should be protected from identification by opposing Aurics or Psychics. Their effects are limited, however - for instance, if you are struck by an Aura Sphere, then your Aura Disruptor will provide no additional protection.

    The Auric Avenger: ...Okay, yeah, that really would've come in handy last night. How do I turn this thing on?

    Gaia: There should be a touch sensor somewhere on the side of your mask.

    -The Auric Avenger feels around the area described for a second before coming across said sensor and giving it a push.-

    The Auric Avenger: ...Yup, that seems to be working just fine. Only one minor problem.

    Gaia: And what would that be?

    The Auric Avenger: It disrupts my Aura Sense as well.

    Gaia: ...Ah. Yes, I can see how that would be a problem.

    The Auric Avenger: Yeah, we might wanna get that little flaw ironed out next time we drop Foxy a line. It could prove to be a pretty major inconvenience.

    Gaia: An inconvenience, yes, but hardly one that cannot be overcome. You have the training and equipment required to fulfil your duties as a vigilante even if your ability to read or manipulate Auras is compromised.

    The Auric Avenger: People expect the Auric Avenger to be, well, Auric! Aura's my big gimmick and everything, the one thing that makes me a literal superhero! What am I supposed to call myself if I don't actually use Aura, huh? The Normal Avenger? The Not-Quite-Badass-Enough-To-Be-Crobatman Avenger?

    Gaia: You're overreacting. Nobody is going to see you in action long enough to accurately testify to the full extent of your abilities.

    The Auric Avenger: I suppose... still, I'm turning the damn thing off for now. I don't care if it's called a disruptor or a jammer, it still gives me the creeps nonetheless...

    -He turns his Aura Disruptor off once again.-

    The Auric Avenger: Anyway, I suppose we'll be bunking here for the night?

    Gaia: It certainly seems that way.

    The Auric Avenger: Awesome! That gives me time to work on my new supersonic explodey gadget! I just need to get my hands on some of the materials...

    -He dashes off.-

    Foxglove: <..."Supersonic explodey gadget"?>

    Gaia: I suspect we are better off not knowing.
