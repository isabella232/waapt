    The Hero No One Knows

    Festival Grounds

    Jacob and Elityria are sitting and eating under the little tent arranged by Jacob's team, who all sit of to the side and watch, along with Joy, who keeps bouncing and shouting, <JOYJOY!> The two sit together happily, laughing and smiling, and generally having a good time.

    "So... you're not mad at me?" Jacob asked with a a hint of lingering remorse.

    Lity shook her head and laughed, "No, not any more. Wow this is good, who cooked this?" Argentum raised an arm, in response to which Lity just giggled more. "Wow, I never thought the little condescending Metang I used to know, who'd just rattle off probabilities and talk about how annoying we were would turn into an ace chef." Jacob looked around nervously.

    "Um.. so... I got you something." He pulled out a small little box and slid it across the table to Lity, who picked it up, put her ear to it and shook it.

    "Teehee~ What's in this, I suppose? People were telling me how you were asking for advice on what to do tonight...." She carefully, with the patience of an angered mankey, tore open the box to find a small gold band with a diamond on it. "Oh... oh my... Jakey you're not-" Jacob laughed a little.

    "No, I just know you like stuff like this. I'm not skipping straight to proposing to you." He smiled. Lity blushed and smiled as she put it on.

    "It's... lovely, Jacob. Thank you." She looked at him with a peaceful, blissful grin.

    "Oh it was no problem, really. I mean it was just a ring." Jacob looked down to the side awkwardly.

    "No dummy. I meant for coming back for me. For... not giving up." She was staring at him now, with regretful eyes. Jacob quietly finished eating and stood up, Lity followed suit.

    "So... we never really did get the Ferris Wheel ride." Jacob suggested. Lity laughed, grabbed his hand and tugged him along somewhere.

    "I love it! Let's go!" The two, laughing, ran off to the Ferris Wheel, got in one of the cars. The sit in silence on the way up, absorbing the breathtaking beauty of the nighttime Nimbasa scenery.

    "...I, uh... um, you look very pretty in that dress, Lity." Jacob said, awkwardly trying to make small talk. She giggled and snuggled in closer to him.

    "You're really bad at this, you know." She told him, giggling more.

    "Right... yeah... I can work on that though!" He admitted with a healthy dose of optimism. Lity just kept giggling. After a few more seconds of silence, as the car began nearing the apex of it's journey, Lity decided to stop just saying words and start talking.

    ".....I love you, Jacob. You know that, right?" They both still looked out at the night sky, the dazzling lights of the city a wonderful tapestry of both life and scenery.

    "Yes, I know that. And you know I feel the same." He replied, to which she shoved him playfully.

    "Come on! It's not the same if you don't say the words, Jakey. Just three, little words. Come on, I know you can." She teased. Jacob took in a shaky breath, worried about what he was about to say.

    You can do this, you know. You've already told her before. But not like this! The first time we did this, she died right before I could say that second word... If I mess this up, what's going to happen? What if you overdo it? What if she's creeped out by it? I... I don't want to lose her again!

    Of course, he was terrified of this. Why wouldn't he be? But of course, he also talked about this, and Discord had a solution to his conflict.

    If you don't even try, you'll never fail, that's true, and it's why you'd feel more safe not trying. But think about it. If you never risk getting told no, you never have the chance to be told yes.

    Please, just this once. Let me have something. He took in another deep breath, as poured all the feelings and thoughts, kept private and locked away in his heart into his declaration, "I love you Elityria."

    He heard a gasp, but that wasn't enough. No. Not nearly all the things he had to say, and yes, he had to say them, for not opening his heart to the one person who had wanted to have a place in it would be a betrayal of unforgivable magnitude, and so he went on.

    "I love you with everything I am. Nothing, not loss, death, war, no argument, not fate, not time, not life itself, nothing, even if the whole world turns against us for it, will ever change that fact."

    The words poured from his mouth as the Ferris Wheel continued to ascend, and he closed his eyes, hoping she didn't think he took it too far.

    Please Lity... Please, I... I... huh? He flinched, but slowly opened his eyes when he felt her hand go across his face.

    He saw her tearing up, surprised and concerned with this unexpected turn of events, he took her hans. "L-Lity? Are you alright?"

    She choked back a sob and nodded, as she tried to squeak out, "J-Jakey. H-Have you seen the movie From Space With Love? I... I heard that the main male character w-was good at his role. D-Do you if this is true?" She stammered. Jacob sighed at the memory, looked outside and saw within a few moments, the Ferris Wheel would reach the top.

    Of course you saw the movie. But... you don't care, do you Lity? You... you still.... Oh my Arceus, thank you, thankyouthankyouthankyou! I... I've... I will never treasure any gift I will ever be given more than this. Thank you, THANK YOU! At last, at long last, I have something worth living for.

    "I could prove it to you." He said with a small grin. Lity looked at him with big, round hopeful eyes. Well, what could go wrong? If I don't try, I'll never know what I could have gained.

    "Please." Then as the Ferris Wheel reached the top of it's cycle, the two leaned in close to each other and

    Discord: <STOP RUINING THEIR MOMENT BY ABUSING META-POWERS!> -Shoves Argentum out of the way of the text-

    as they pulled away, both had full, genuine smiles, and turned to see the city of Nimbasa at the perfect climax of the Ferris Wheel's circle.

    "Hey, you didn't die before we could kiss this time." Jacob tried to casually point out, tears streaming down his face.

    "R-Right. I-I'm so... happy, that we finally did this." Lity was crying too, so she burried her head into Jacob's shoulder. He hugged her closer to him.

    "Best. Day. Ever." They said in unison, and held on to each other as they slowly began their ride back down, both of them crying that this time around, both of them could enjoy it together. The bond they had, that even death itself could not break, finally sealed.
