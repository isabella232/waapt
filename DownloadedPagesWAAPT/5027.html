    125671 Luke92431st Dec 2011 02:20:40 PM Get RP Mod

    National Park - Johto

    It's a beautiful morning in the National park, and although the weather is chilly, that has done little to disdain visitors from Goldenrod, Violet and Ecruteak. Even a few Bug Catchers from as far away as Azalea Town have come to visit, in hopes of participating in the days' Bug Catching contest.

    Some people, however, are relaxing by the fountain in the centre of the park, some simply content to throw seeds or breadcrumbs to the local Pokémon, be they Pidgey, Spearow, or even the odd Murkrow that's strayed from Goldenrod nearby, in hopes of facing less stiff competition in seeking morsels of food.

    Hardly noticeable in the crowd of bird Pokémon, however, are a particularly distinguished looking Pidgeotto, and a young Pidgey, who sticks close to the older bird's side, a slightly uncomfortable look on her face.

    Pidgey: <Dad?>

    Pidgeotto: <What is it, sweetheart?>

    Pidgey: <I don't like being a silly old Pidgey, daddy! I look so dull like this!>

    The Pidgey chirps in annoyance, ruffling her feathers and generally looking rather irritated, causing the Pidgeotto to let out a small sigh.

    Pidgeotto: <I know you're not quite so keen on this, little one. But it's very important that we spend time in these forms.>

    Pidgey: <But why? Being a Pidgey is so boring! I can't even breath fire like this!>

    Pidgeotto: <There's a very good reason for that though. Look around you...>

    The larger bird gestures with his wings at the fellow bird Pokémon surrounding them. The Pidgey follows his wing, catching site of a few Pidgey sharing breadcrumbs nearby, a pair of Tailow bathing in the fountain, and a few Murkrow sitting on a bench nearby, cawing nosily to each other.

    <All these Pokémon aren't like us, sweetie. Me and you...we're special.>

    The Pidgey tilts her head, not sure what her father means.

    Pidgey: <Special?>

    The Pidgeotto nods.

    Pidgeotto: <Yes. In our true forms, we are much, much more powerful than the vast majority of wild Pokémon. Not even the very strongest of trained Pokémon could hope to match us in terms of sheer strength. Well...most of them, anyway.>

    The older bird briefly reminisces about one of the few defeats he had suffered a few months back. Granted, he had been holding back, but he wasn't too shocked that those two particular Pokémon had managed to triumph against him.

    <But with that power, there comes the risk of growing blind to those who are not on your level, sweetie. When you don't have to worry about certain needs, it's very easy to forget that others do.>

    Pidgey: <And why would forgetting those things be bad?>

    Pidgeotto: <Because it's our duty to care. All the people and Pokémon in this region are our responsibility. We have to make sure that they're kept safe, and that they need not suffer needlessly. But...if we do know what it is like to be a lesser Pokémon like them, we would eventually grow to consider the problems they face everyday as unimportant. And we would grow blind to their suffering. And we if we wouldn't know if they were suffering, how would we ever be able to help them?>

    The Pidgey blinks, tilting her head slightly.

    Pidgey: <I don't get it...>

    The Pidgeotto simply smiles.

    Pidgeotto: <...It's fine, sweetie. Perhaps it's too early for you to be worrying about these sorts of issues just yet.>

    Pidgey: <Does that mean I don't have to be a silly Pidgey anymore?>

    Pidgeotto: <I'm afraid not, my young hen. Just because you don't understand this now, it doesn't mean you won't later.>

    The younger bird hangs her head, disappointed.

    Pidgey: <Aww...>

    Her father smiles, chuckling slightly.

    Pidgeotto: <Cheer up! There are advantages to this form. We don't have to hide in public for a start.>

    Pidgey: <Oh, yeah! Hiding is so boring! And we can let the humans feed us yummy breadcrumbs!>

    Pidgeotto: <See? It's not so bad, is it? Now, how about we go and see if we can find any->

    <SQWARK!>

    <"OH NO! HE'S HERE!">

    <"FLY AWAY!!!">

    Pidgeotto: <What on Earth-?>

    The Pidgeotto looks over to see most of the bird Pokémon in the park fleeing in sheer terror at the site of a large and fierce looking Staraptor, the huge avian sporting several scars on it's body, obviously from prior scuffles.

    The Staraptor approaches a pair of Pidgey that either hadn't noticed him yet or were unaware of his reputation. Upon catching site of the bird, he shoots them both a death-glare. Scared half to death, the Pidgey fly into the air, cooing wildly in fright.

    Staraptor: <THAT'S RIGHT, CLEAR OFF! ALL OF YOU! THIS IS MY PARK!>

    His dominance assured, the Staraptor begins to nom on a piece of bread that the Pidgey had been sharing moments before, the area now void of other birds.

    Apart from a certain Pidgeotto and Pidgey, who, while trying to put a brave face on things, was slightly Intimidated by the newcomer.

    Pidgey: <W-What a meanie! He j-just scared all the other birds away, and now he's taken their f-food!>

    She looks up her father, a mixture of anger and food on her face. <Go on, Dad! Go over there and roast him with the colourful thing you can do!>

    Hearing the Pidgey nearby, the Staraptor looks up at the two. Seeing this, the Pidgey shrieks in fright, then hides behind her father, shivering in fear.

    <Eek! don't let him get me, Dad! He might eat me!>

    The Pidgeotto pats his daughter with a wing comfortingly.

    Pidgeotto: <Don't worry, sweetie, I'll handle this. But find somewhere safe to hide while I do, okay?>

    The Pidgey nods, still shivering slightly.

    Pidgey: <Okay...be careful, Dad!>

    She then flies away, disappearing into the branches of one of the trees surrounding the park. His daughter now safe, the Pidgeotto bravely approaches the Staraptor, who has now moved on to eating the various seeds and breadcumbs scattered around the park.

    Pidgeotto: <Good morning. May I inquire about something?>

    Staraptor: <What?>

    The Staraptor continues to peck at the food, ignoring the other birds presence entirely.

    Pidgeotto: <I've noticed that you seem to have made use of your natural intimidation to frighten all of the other Pokémon away from this public area, which could hardly be considered to be good manners.>

    Staraptor: <Well, this is my park. I scared 'em away because they're not welcome.>

    The Pidgeotto frowns at this revelation.

    Pidgeotto: <You claim that this park, a public park I may add, belongs to you, and you alone?>

    Staraptor: <That's the idea.>

    The bird finally acknowledges the Pidgeotto with a glance.

    <You got a problem with that?>

    Pidgeotto: <I do have issues with it, yes.>

    Strapator: <Well, here's what you can do...>

    The Starpator gets right in the Pidgeotto's face, giving him a glare that would scar a less brave (Or stupid) Pokémon for life.

    Staraptor: <This is MY park. Clear off or I'll peck you eyes out. Understand?>

    The Pidgeotto says nothing for a few seconds, seemingly unfazed by the bird of prey's threat.

    Pidgeotto: <...Yes. I fully understand.>

    Staraptor: <...Good.>

    The Staraptor goes back to eating the breadcrumbs...

    ...only to be struck a few seconds later by a Wing Attack from the other Flying Type, the sheer force of the attack sending the Staraptor flying several feet. Before he can strike the ground however, he flaps his wings, righting himself in the air.

    Spotting the Pidgeotto, he glares at him with pure malice.

    Staraptor: <...Idiot. You said you understood!>

    The bird of prey rockets back towards the Pidgeotto, rises into the air, and swoops down at unavoidable speed

    His opponent, however, has no intent on dodging the strike. With only a few seconds to react, the bird focuses on one though: protecting his only chick from harm. Concentrating, the bird uses his desire to defend her to form a golden barrier around himself.

    The Staraptor, realising that he's about to crash headlong into a protect barrier, attempts to break off the attack, but it's already far too late. The bird crashes headlong into the shield, bouncing off harmlessly and rolling a few feet along the floor. The attack successfully deflected, the Pidgeotto drops the shield.

    Pidgeotto: <But I do understand. I understand that you have selfishly claimed this park as your own, knowing full well that is was built as a safe haven for Pokémon and people alike!>

    His pride now on the line, the Staraptor foolishly charges headlong at his opponent again, trying to get as close as possible so that he wouldn't be able to guard against any of his strikes.

    But the Pidgeotto has different ideas. Moving at lightning speed, the bird seemingly splits into two identical copies of himself, which in turn, split into two copies of themselves, the Staraptor eventually finding himself surrounded by Pidgeottos, all indistinguishable from each other.

    Staraptor: <Double Team? Youre resorting to hatchling tricks to stop me now?>

    The bird strikes the nearest copy with a Wing attack, the limb passing through as the clone fades from existence. The Pidgeotto smirks at this.

    Pidgeotto: <All moves, no matter how basic, have their uses.>

    The Staraptor strikes the clone that just spoke, only for it to fade away as well. He continues to strike at the clones, all of them fading from existence, until there's only one Pidgeotto left. Losing his cool, the bird or prey eyes him, annoyed.

    Staraptor: <Darn it, what's the point you're trying to make here?>

    Pidgeotto: <My point? My point is that just because you may be stronger or smarter than others, it does not give you the right exert yourself on others in order to get what you want.>

    The Staraptor blinks, not quite believing the drivel the Pidgeotto is coming out with.

    Staraptor: <Are you crazy? You obviously have no clue how the world works! The Strong survive. But the weak? The weak exist to either serve the strong...or die!>

    Intending to finish this interloper once and for all, the bird of prey screeches, then dives at the Pidgeotto, aiming his talons at his neck, hoping not only to make an example out of him for anymon else that may dare to stand up to him, but score a decent meal as well.

    Meanwhile, in the branches of a nearby tree, several bird Pokémon watch in horror as the Staraptor charges at the Pidgeotto, knowing from experience that he was aiming to kill. Random!Starly: <OH NO!>

    Random!Tailow: <He's a goner...>

    Random!Murkrow: <Caw, that poor, brave fool...>

    A certain Pidgey, however, ignores all this as she watches with utter horror as the Staraptor comes closer and closer to ending her father's life.

    Pidgey: <DAD!!!>

    "CLANG!"

    The killing blow, however, never lands.

    To his surprise, the Staraptor's attack is blocked by the Pidgeotto's wing, it's feathers having seemingly transfigured themselves into a lightweight material resembling steel.

    Pidgeotto: <You see, Staraptor, it's you that doesn't understand. The weak do not exist to serve the strong...>

    The bird lowers his wing, then spins around in mid-air, aiming his other metallic wing at his foe.

    <They exist...TO PROTECT THEM!>

    Before the Staraptor can react, he is slammed with the Steel Wing, knocking the air out of him and sending him falling downwards towards the fountain, landing with a loud "Splash!" in the water, soaking his feathers through and rendering unable to fly.

    Not that he would be doing any flying any time soon, as the bird's eyes had been replaced with swirls, signifying that he had fainted.

    Glad that the conflict was over, the Pidgeotto flew down and perched on the rim of the fountain, preening a few feathers that had been ruffled during the battle, seemingly oblivious to the large crowd of birds emerging from the trees and gathering around him.

    That was, until a certain Pidgey glomped him.

    Pidgey: <DAD! I was so scared! That nasty old Staraptor nearly killed you!>

    The Pidgey hugs her father, burying his head in his feathers. frowning, the Pidgeotto warps his wings around her, giving her a hug.

    Pidgeotto: <I'm sorry, sweetie. I honestly didn't mean to worry you like that...But I got him, didn't I?>

    Pidgey: <...But...But what if you hadn't?>

    She stares up at him, frightened at the very thought of losing her father.

    Pidgeotto: <...Well...>

    Smirking, the bird lowers his head to whisper into her ear.

    <I would take more than that to kill a phoenix.>

    He then gives her a sly wink. His daughter takes a few seconds to process this information, then smiles and winks back.

    Meanwhile, a random Murkrow hops over to the Staraptor and pecks him lightly with his beak, then hops back, cringing at the thought if being attacked in retaliation...

    ...but no such retribution came.

    Murkrow: <Hey...he did it! He actually beat him!>

    Shocked, the gather birds cheer, a chorus of joyus chirps erupting from the crowd. Several random birds push hop forward, offering the Pidgeotto and his daughter various breadcrumbs and seeds.

    Random!Tailow: <Thank you so much! That guy was such a jerk!>

    Random!Starvia: <Is there anything we can do to repay you?>

    The Pidgeotto simply smiles, shaking his head politely.

    Pidgeotto: <No thank you, helping you all out is reward in itself.>

    His daughter, however, gazes at the breadcrumbs and seeds hungrily.

    Pidgey: <Aww, can I have some Dad? Please?>

    Her father thinks for a few seconds, then nods.

    Pidgeotto: <Alright, but don't have too many.>

    Pidgey: <Okay!>

    The Pidgey chirps happily, then hops down and begins pecking at the bread, the Murkrow and Tailow watching in interest.

    Random!Tailow: <Wow, your Dad is so cool!>

    Random!Murkrow: <Yeah, he's pretty awesome! Does he have a name?>

    The Pidgey shakes her head.

    Pidgey: <He doesn't, but I do!>

    Random!Murkrow: <Oh...>

    Random!Tailow: <What is it?>

    She smiles, then puffs her chest out proudly.

    Pidgey: <I'm Nix! What's your names?>
