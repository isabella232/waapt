    -Pent sets down the stone and places a hand upon it-

    Okay.

    -He hums slightly and the crystal in the bardstone pulses with a deep green light, a second later the glyph and the crystals within the wall pulse as well. The hum is magnified before it falls silent, and after a second a new sound picks up: A voice. The voice is scratchy and gruff, belonging to a man hardened by manual labor-

    This is a recording of the vision of Our Lady by myself, Tresan, leader of the people of this isle. Ahem.

    I saw bronze. And I heard a rhythm of wardrums.

    And the bronze opened, like the dilating of an iris, and I saw into eternity.

    Then, all of a sudden, the bronze iris closed. The beating in my head ceased. And I found myself in the throneroom of Our Lady.

    Our Lady turned to me, and said

    -A second voice picks up, a younger female's voice. Her dictation is very deliberate, as though reading slowly from a sheet of paper or having the words whispered in her ear-

    Tresan of the obscured father, son of Doulis Nox.
    You are not ready.
    There was a flash of light, and when it passed Our Lady held the light within her hands. She nodded solemnly to herself.

    Two.
    I blinked, and when my vision returned I was not staring at the throne room of Our Lady, but at a vast landscape.

    A ruby-red fire burnt on an emerald isle in a shimmering sapphire sea. And Our Lady spoke again.

    Lead your people here Tresan. Lead your people beyond the fog and until your dying day you will know sanctuary from strife and ravage.
    And the vision faded. I awoke with to the drumming in my head and the lingering thought of that far-flung place.

    -The female's voice resonates again, this time less cautious and meek-

    When my father led his people to this isle, it was as he had seen in his vision. Still seas, a vast green forest and a grand bonfire burning upon the shore. There he found a single stone marker, engraved with a last message from Our Lady: Welcome home.
    I was the first child born upon this island, in the tradition of our family I was named Tetra. It is now as I prepare to enter adulthood myself that we commit this vision to the stone, so that some day my own children may hear of their grandfather's vision.

    -The light in the room fades, and Pent takes a step back-

    That was the link... Final link in the puzzle...

    -He smiles slightly for a moment-

    Ominous sure, but it solves my question coming here...
