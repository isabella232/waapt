    NI HQ

    As Byran walks into the base, he feels a prickling at the back of his neck, a dark sense of dread clouding around him. As he looked forward, he saw that the lobbys lights were dimmed, the only ones on were the wall lights, which gave the whole area an eerie glow. He tried shrugging the feeling of discomfort off, and thought :Gaah, this feels unsettling, is the boss trying to give the place a gothic noir feel or something?

    His eyes then widened as, in front of him, a figure seemed to loom, growing larger and larger as it approached him, its shadow filling up the wall, but then breathed a sigh of relief when he saw that it was just Wesker, smiling, and wearing a cloak, which gave an oddly demonic visage, silhouetted against the wall.

    Why hello there, my good man, Wesker spoke softly, So good of you to come early. Now, I have some work Id like for you to get done in the office, so follow me, please. He turned around, swishing his cloak, the demonic silhouette growing against the wall before vanishing around the corner again as he passed.

    Byran followed him, shaking his head as he did.Ill humor him for now...but ooh boy, is the information in his computer looking more and more accessible now..

    Byrans office

    The floor, oddly enough, was covered in newspaper, or what was either that or a huge white sheet. Byran tilted his head at that, puzzled, but shrugged. Wesker closed the door behind him, and waved him inside, gently locking it as quiet as possible, so that Byran didnt hear. He then stowed the key in his cloak. This required gently nudging the cloak out of the way, which, for a flash of a second, revealed a raincoat underneath.

    Byran cautiously sat down in his office chair, which happened to be the only chair present in the room. There was a stack of papers present on his table, which he began to read through.

    Wesker looked around the room, and moved towards the sound system, after which he began to shuffle through the CD collection, eagerly. In a couple of seconds, he came across a particular CD with a band on the cover, which he showed to his underling.Do you like Huey Lewis and the Scraftys?

    Hmm? Byran looked up from the papers, looking rather distracted, and off handedly said Oh! Yes, theyre all right, and went back to reading through the papers.

    Wesker looked a bit disappointed at this answer, walking through the room to the bathroom as he said Well, their early work was bit too..New Wave, for my taste, but when Pokeathalon came out in 83, he said, his voice a bit more upbeat and echoey as he walked into the bathroom, I think they really came into their own, commercially and artistically.

    As he started washing his hands, he kept talking The whole album has a clear, crisp sound, and a new sheen of..consummate professionalism, that really gives the songs, he added, after wiping his hands and taking off his cloak, leaving the uppermost layer being the raincoat underneath, a big boost. He then walked out of the room, grabbing his axe as he went out, smoothly sliding into the room, Byran still focused on the work in front of him, head still down.

    Hes been compared to Elvis Costello, Wesker kept talking, elicting a very low, too quiet to hear groan, but I think Huey has a far more bitter, he set the axe down behind the bookshelf for a bit hiding it from view, sense of humor, he finished, grinning.

    Arceus..he just keeps talking. I mean, really, I have nothing against this precious band, but aaarg, if he doesnt stop going on and on about them I might just end up burning my CDs of em. He then looked around a bit, frowning.Um, sir? Why is there so much newspaper all around the office?He then looked up at Wesker, who was wringing his hands, and looked even more puzzled.And why are you in a raincoat, sir?

    Oh, didnt you hear, lad? They said it was going to rain mighty heavily today, so I was getting it all ready in case of muddy feet, my dear underling. Byran made an aaah face, looking satisfied, and then going back to reading the papers, most of the way through them now.

    Wesker then walked back to the sound system and put a CD in, obviously a Huey Lewis and the News CD.In 1987, Huey released,  he gestured to the system, leaning against it, this."

    Byran groaned again, Great, now hes playing one of those cheesy songs! while Wesker took no notice.Four, their most accomplished album. I think, their undisputed masterpiece is Hip to Be Square, doing a little dance to the song, which incidentally was the same one the sound system was playing at the moment, a song so catchy, most people probably,  he kept talking, as he headed on over to the bookshelf, and picked up the axe, probably dont listen to the lyrics, but they should.

    Byran nodded, just thinking Oh man, am I looking forward to getting this mission over and done with. Aaah, heading back to Fiore, meeting up with Ashleen and Kelly... Man, just chilling on Fiores beaches, being back home, but most of all,, being away from this nuthouse. All of that seems so close now..

    Because its not just about the pleasures of conformity, and the importance of friends, its also a personal statement about the band itself! But, I personally? Due to life experiences, I can see a different perspective of it! So, to me, its a bit of a cautionary tale, about a rat that tries to join a group of cats to steal their food and get them locked up, but gets in far deeper than he bargained for..

    Byrans head slowly moved up, feeling a bit unnerved.Wait..no..he cant possibly be talking about.. Moral of the story? Never try to one up a cat..because they can be very, very vicious.

    Eyes wide, totally convinced, Byran shakingly, against all judgement, turned his head, just in time to see snarling Wesker heave the axe above him, and strike, while his last words appeared in his mind.Everyone..Im so, so very sorry..I failed you all..good luck, Marie..

    Unheard to everyone, the quiet sound of a stiletto blade singing through the air whistled about. The blade buried itself in Bryans head, and the male slumped over,his head falling back on to the desk, staining the paper with the blood now pouring out of him, shaking one or twice, before finally stopping all movement. In the doorway stood Alys. Were you having a fun moment there, Wesker?

    Looking nonplussed, Wesker looked from the now lifeless Byran to Alys, and glaring, spitting out Chienne at the spider lady, who simply rolled her eyes. I see weve watched similar films, Wesker. You could pick a better model next time than a yuppie from Castelia. She walked away, heels clacking.

    Wesker merely sighed, and called after her Hey, do not diss a classic! He then began clearing up, grumbling to himself Well, guess I and the boss have something in common, then.
