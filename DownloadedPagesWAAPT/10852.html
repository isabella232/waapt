    Terrera, the Citadel

    -Sabaku rubs his forehead as he reads the letter.-

    Sabaku: Well... at least from Lucanus, I know that he'll stay true to his word. And if he needs open borders to protect his allies... of course it would be Illusio. He doesn't have an open border with them.

    Shiko: I'm sure the Merchant's Guild would greatly appreciate the open trade route...

    Sabaku: Yes... I hate pandering to them, but aside from them, that would put two potential borders on friendly terms, and one on threatened ground. Cragspur and Yaksha might take offense to "allying" with others, but Warriors from Pugilis would be very handy against both...

    -Sabaku takes out a piece of fine vellum and begins writing.-

    Dear Lord Lucanus Cain,

    To be perfectly honest, I'm very relieved that your letter was a request for trade and not a declaration of war. Your terms seem fair, and you've proven your word to be trustworthy before. I shall acquies to your proposal for an open border agreement, and will consider your troops free for movement upon Terreran sand, so long as they show respect to the locals as they would their fellow countrymen.

    However, such a display of friendship may irk our mutual neighbor Chrysalia, as well as Cragspur and Yaksha to the east. Therefore, I would also like to extend a pact of mutual defense, as the warriors of Pugilis would be a great deterrent against assaults from Terrera's eastern border, while my own would help against Chrysalia, Ignis, and Violight.

    And... thank you for your encouraging words. I know the old lord Takuma put his trust in you for a reason, and I am willing to trust you as well.

    Sincerely, Lord Sabaku of Terrera

    Sabaku: Here. Deliver this to Lucanus. I'm sure he will be most pleased.
