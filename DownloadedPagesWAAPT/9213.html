    Ecruteak - February 26

    The sun was starting to set.

    Not that it mattered at the time. Eskay was at home, inside. The only window in the room was to the east, so the sunset wasn't visible.

    That room used to be his mom's bedroom. Now it was mostly unused. The last time it had been used had been when he, Psyche, Trip, and Straw had spent a few days here, while everyone else was in PMD; he slept in here while the others took the other rooms. Otherwise, he only entered the room on special occasions.

    Today was one of them.

    The first thing he looks at whenever he comes in here is a picture. A family portrait.◊ It was him, Alli, and their mom, standing out by the beach in Cianwood. Behind them, the sun was setting. It was back when Alli had short hair; that was one of the recurring thoughts that Eskay thought of when he looked at it each time. However, a different thought was a bit more prominent.

    It was the last picture they had before she had died.

    Today marks six years, Eskay thought to himself. Six years since she died...

    He wipes some dust off of the frame. The date it was taken was fading, but as of now, it was still visible. 

    February 25, 2007

    One day before.

    Eskay lets out a sigh, and puts the picture back on the desk. He heads out to the roof shortly afterwards.

    The sun had already set the rest of the way before he got out here, but there was still that faint glow from just below the horizon. It wasn't worth noting, since the trees were obscuring it, but there was still just enough light to see his surroundings.

    He looks over the Bell Tower.

    "Skye was so happy after finally getting to go up there... I wonder if something happened up there that she didn't mention..."

    A few possibilities go through his mind.

    ...Did they meet- Nah, people would have noticed...

    Perhaps it was- ...No, that's not it...

    ...Maybe- Why would I even consider that one... >///>

    Eventually, he just decides it was nothing more than the apparent.

    "I helped her live a lifelong dream... Anyone would be happy about that."

    He looks over at the Burned Tower.

    "And then, Psyche... She was terrifying, when she realized I'd manipulated people. Sure, she forgave me, but still... I don't know if she'd have dumped me or mind-crushed me or what, but I know it was te- "

    He stops as he sees a white figure in the tower. At least, he thought he saw one. He blinked, and it went away right after.

    "Did something just..."

    He tries to recall the glimpse of the mysterious white figure. A few things were clear in his mind: it was a woman with an impossibly-pale... everything. Like a ghost.

    That was all he could remember. He wanted to say she looked... familiar...

    Was that... n-no, it couldn't have been...

    All that came to mind was his mother.

    ...I'm just imagining things... Just a puff of smoke from a Koffing exploding or something... I'm just misinterpreting it...

    There was one thing that couldn't have been misinterpreted, though. It took the form of a woman.

    Eskay shakes his head, and goes back inside to go to bed.
    Key word "mostly".
