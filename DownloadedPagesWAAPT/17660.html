    This post here is the start of a long series of spagposts which have been a collaborative effort involving me, Eskay, Tagg, Tangent, Umbra and Every and given Herbert's blessing. So without further ado, enjoy.

    -There is a ethereal forest. This forest is neither here nor there, there is no wind blowing through the trees, the air is sour and static, there are no leaves on the ground, there is not even the usual forest sounds of small creatures chattering in the tree branches or munching on the debris on the forest floor. One would say the the forest is dead.

    However, the forest does not look dead - not in the slightest. The trees tower over the underlay, the leaves are bright, fresh and plump, the smaller plants are just as lively and the rows of flowers that adorn the clearings are as beautiful as ever. The forest must be alive then?

    But no. This forest is not alive. Nor is it dead. This forest seems to exist in between both life and death, making it the perfect place to discuss matters of both. And in one of the clearings in this forest stands Yveltal, along with Xerneas and Zygarde, for precisely such a discussion. Xerneas is sitting down on one of his tatami mats that he brought along, pouring tea into a few cups for both himself and his current guests.-

    Xerneas: So, siblings, how may I be of assistance today?

    Yveltal: <Well, I wanted something to be done about that little issue I pointed out a few months ago.>

    Xerneas: Ah yes, _that_ one. I do not take issue with your modest proposal sister - since the affair was neither Just nor Heroic, but alas, it is Zygarde's authority that I act on in matters like this

    -Xerneas takes a sip of his tea and looks over to Zygarde.-

    Xerneas: But now that we are all here, we can finally come to an agreement on these matters. Yes?

    Zygarde: <As it happens, I've been in a good mood the past few days. Some Noibat came to visit down in Terminus Cave. An idea which you two might consider more often. So yes, I am willing to hear you both out.>

    Xerneas: Apologies. I tend to get caught up in my meditations and tea cultivations. Regardless, I think sister is in a better position that I am to discuss the issue at hand. It affects her realm more directly than mine.

    -He pours another cup.-

    Yveltal: <Zygarde, I know you and I are both big fans of the concept of karma. And the event in question was decidedly un-karmic. That bastard got away scot-free and I want to do something about it!>

    Xerneas: I suggest something a little tit for tat.

    Zygarde: <Many bastards get away scot-free. But in this case, you are correct, a rebalancing of the scales is necessary. At the same time, though, I must hear out your brother's request.>

    Xerneas: Well, in turn of sister Yveltal getting her pound of flesh, I require mine. In other words, we make an equal trade. I trade the one from my realm that has caused much injustice for the two in hers to achieve parity. Fair, yes?

    Yveltal: <Wait, two? I'm iffy enough on trading one, is there some other way? I just want to get my talons on that smug bastard.>

    Xerneas: The two arrived as a packaged deal. Should it not only be fair that they return as one? Would be quite cruel to favour one over the other, yes?

    Zygarde: <Yveltal, your brother is correct. But your concerns are noted, of course. As such, I will not force you to submit. Xerneas, if you wish for an uneven deal, you will have to convince Yveltal of its merit, and more importantly, you will have to convince me. You may gather anyone you like for this purpose, but I will not allow the unbalancing of the scales without due cause.>

    Xerneas: I have my mind on a few. I will make sure to humour the two of you

    -Xerneas's eyes flash briefly as he places the freshly poured cup to the coaster on the other side of the table.-
