    Rebecca: You give up?

    The bluenette whistled, Skulk adopting a less menacing pose and making his way back to her side, the girl patting his head.

    Rebecca: You realize that means the Mega Stone is mine, right?

    Mega Lucario!Kim: <...>

    Rex: I know. But, I'm not used to fighting with Kim just yet. Even with... whatever this is...

    Mega Evolution. He'd heard that from the Professor.

    Rex: I doubt we can beat you when you probably know how it works better than we do.

    Kim looked a bit irate with his decision, though she couldn't deny what he was saying was true. But then...

    Rebecca: ...Hmm.

    The smirk returned to her lips, the girl nodding at his decision.

    Rebecca: You're right. Even with your transformation, you still couldn't beat Skulk. Not with a recently evolved Lucario that's not even used to moving about.

    Recalling Skulk to his Dusk Ball, Rebecca gave a shrug of her shoulders.

    Rebecca: Well, you tried, at least.

    Turning to walk off, the girl began to make her way to the top of the cave.

    Rex: Wait!

    Pausing in place, the girl glanced backwards, a curious expression on her face.

    Rex: Just because I give up now doesn't mean this is over. I said I'd get that stone back, and I will!

    Rebecca: Hmm? You're awfully bold, you know that? I don't exactly have incentive to challenge you again.

    Rex: ...

    Turning to face him, Rebecca stared at him in silence for several moments, as though she was peering into his soul. Then, finally...

    Rebecca: Here.

    Her hand shot out and a small object flew towards the two, Kim darting forward to catch it in a paw. As she looked down at the stone, she was surprised to note that the stone was exactly the same of that in her pendant.

    Rebecca: If you can master that Mega Stone, I'll let you have another shot.

    There was a glint in her eyes as she spoke, as though she were aware of some kind of joke that he wasn't.

    Rebecca: I suppose I should give you a chance to prove yourself with a bit of preparation time.

    Rex took a step over to Kim, taking the stone from her palm and glancing up at Rebecca with a frown.

    Rex: How do I know you'll keep your word?

    Rebecca: You don't.

    That was blunt.

    Rex: W-well... how will you know if I master it or not?

    Rebecca: You don't need to worry about that. Once I find an interesting mark, I always keep a close eye on it.

    Smiling, Rebecca gave a teasing wave and turned to make her way back up the cavern's exit, her footsteps fading within moments.
