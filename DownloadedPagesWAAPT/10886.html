    -she coughs once-

    Isolde.

    Isolde: -blinks and turns to her- <What is it?>

    I need... to hunt.

    Isolde: <Hunt? Humans don't hunt anymore!>

    No... but...

    -she stops for a moment, her fingers fumbling at the string around her neck-

    You said... you don't... talk to anyone?

    -Isolde drifts a bit closer to the floor, looking very, very sad-

    Isolde: <No... no one talks to me. No one comes to see me. You are the first in a very, very long time, Crewe.>

    -Crewe looks a little uncomfortable-

    I'm sorry. But then... I can do... this.

    -she pulls out the moon stone attached to the end of the string and grips it in one hand. There's a very faint pulse of light from it, and her form wavers as she seems to fade out of existence. A runty Sneasel appears in her place-

    <There. Now I can... hunt...>

    -Isolde's eyes widen, and she floats very quickly over to Crewe, getting uncomfortably close-

    Isolde: <No... it can't be...>

    -Crewe inches back from her, scrambling up against the wall-

    <What...?>

    Isolde: <You look... you look just like her! It can't be?>

    -Crewe furrows her brown, frowning-

    <What can't be? Who do I look like?>

    Isolde: <Kori, of course! You look just like Kori! I can't believe it!>

    <... Oh. Um...>

    Isolde: <You must be her descendant! That makes so much sense!>

    <That... that seems kind of unlikely...>

    Isolde: <I remember Kori's face as perfectly as I remember my Amhlaidh's, and you match her entirely. She must have been your ancestor!>

    <Uh... well...>

    Isolde: <Oh, this is such glorious news! I have a piece of her! But... why did you appear human? And why did you look so very much like my Amhlaidh?>

    <I can explain... but... first, I need food...>

    Isolde: <Oh! Yes, food! I had completely forgotten that you need to eat! Come with me, there's a berry bush just outside you can eat from.>

    -Crewe nods and stumbles gratefully out of the cave. Isolde follows, extremely closely. Crewe shivers as she glances around, quickly spotting a Rawst and an Aspear berry bush. She starts picking and eating berries ravenously, every so often glancing down the mountain or back at Isolde. The Froslass is staring at her intently, unblinkingly. After the first few times Crewe sees this, she decides to stop looking at Isolde. Eventually, she trains her focus on the berries she's eating. Which doesn't help all that much, as the first time she takes notice of what kind of berries they are, her paws start shaking-
