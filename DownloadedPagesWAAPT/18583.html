    Mad Dad

    Floating Fortress, Bridge

    Lazarus is back at his command post, Glendower at his side.

    Lazarus: So, you had a report on the Darkrai research.

    <Glendower: Aye, milord. Specifically, looking into ways of harnessing the subject's powers without actually attempting to capture the subject.>

    Lazarus: Quite. No risk, no reward, certainly, but as the failures of many a would be world conqueror and team leader show, attempting to subdue deities only ends in failure.

    <Glendower: With that in mind, I found a fascinating tale from the oral histories of a tribe of folk who once lived in and around the Entree in Unova. The story is known as "The Darkrai Who Shed His Coat." Summarizing, there was a Darkrai who wanted to woo a mortal Pokemon, but kept scaring her off because of the nightmares. So Arceus told him to go deep into the woods and shed his coat, and he would no longer be scary. Odd story, yes, but further analysis hints that it is likely an origin story for an artifact.>

    Lazarus: You mean to say there is an artifact that could give me the powers of a Darkrai?

    <Glendower: Or close to it. The wrinkle to this, milord, is that the set of tales this is taken from are supposedly tales of another world, one that is solely occupied by Pokemon.>

    Lazarus: Hmm. Father heard rumors of the J-team visiting such worlds, while I was still in his head. They have the designation PMD. Never did find out what that stands for. So, to sum up, this artifact is in another world entirely?

    <Glendower: In essence, yes, milord.>

    Lazarus: Drat. Even worse, I have no Pokeform, and thus unable to enter such realms directly.

    <Glendower: Unfortunate indeed, milord.>

    Lazarus: Quite.

    He thinks for a while.

    Lazarus: Start looking into mad scientists with a specialty in extra dimensional work. At the very least, a functioning dimension gate under our control would be a massive asset.

    <Glendower: Aye, milord. And once we have access? What about actually going into the other world?>

    Lazarus: I'll have to come up with an agent good enough, that I can trust. I can't afford to lose you, so you're out of the running.

    <Glendower: A shame, though thank you for the compliment, milord.>

    Lazarus: I'll think it over, get back to you. Good work, Glendower.

    <Glendower: Aye, milord.>
