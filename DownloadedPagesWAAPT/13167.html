    Deziree: Eep!

    -Deziree leaps to the side as she twists to look at the smallish woman beside her. Emphemria laughs as Deziree catches her breath.-

    Emphemria: What, never had a conversation with the dead in the middle of a lucid dream?

    Deziree: No!

    Emphemria: Well... you have now, at least. Now...

    -An ornate table and accompanying chairs rise from the sand. Emphemria sits in one, taking a cup of tea from nowhere and sipping before motioning to the other chair.-

    Emphemria: Sabaku might like to sit cross legged, but I prefer chairs myself.

    -Deziree takes a seat, a little amazed at the display of malleable reality.-

    Deziree: So... uh... who are you?

    Emphemria: Oh, a thousand pardons, I haven't introduced myself yet! My name is Emphemria Morton, daughter of Tycho and Elaine Morton. And wife of Sabaku the Wanderer.

    -Emphemria smiles as Deziree's light up with recognition.-

    Deziree: Oooh! You're Dune's great-great-etcetera grandmother!

    Emphemria: Indeed I am. Now, I hear you and he are becoming... close. I'd like to take you under my wing and make sure you're the right fit for him.

    Deziree: Wait, you're going to test me?

    Emphemria: And train you.

    Deziree: Why?!

    Emphemria: Because of what happened the last time one of our line married. I do not want a repeat performance of that debacle.

    -Deziree unconsciously rubs her shoulder, remembering the incident back in Gateon.-

    Deziree: Alright. I'll give you that point. Where do we start?

    Dream Terrera, Some Time Later

    Emphemria: What was your first dance like?

    Deziree: It was painful.

    -Emphemria raises an eyebrow. Deziree blushes.-

    Deziree: Neither, uh, neither of us are very good dancers.

    Emphemria: Well, I suppose that makes sense. Apparently they don't push the finer arts in school as much as they used to.

    -She let's a smirk creep past the rim of her teacup.-

    Emphemria: And I imagine having legs is still new for you.

    -Deziree's face drains of color.-

    Deziree: You... you know? How?!

    Emphemria: I heard it through the grapevine. A handy skill for any Kalosian woman.

    Deziree: And... and we're still talking? You're... not just going to cut me off now?

    -The older woman looks almost hurt by the question.-

    Emphemria: My dear, I am a hopeless romantic. If what you feel goes beyond the species barrier, which you yourself crossed by your own hands, then who am I to say otherwise?

    -The light from Deziree's face could almost be measured in lumens.-

    Emphemria: But!

    - Record Scratch -

    Emphemria: I still want to make sure my great-great-great... you know what let's just call him my greatest grandson. I still want him to feel proper love. His parents may be beyond my lessons, but I'm hoping that you are not.
