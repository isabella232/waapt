    Luke: "Well, even if most of the Dinosaurs are gone now, there are still quite a few Pokémon that might as well be Dinosaurs. Meganium, Tropius, Aggron..."

    Not recognizing any of these names, the Shieldon stared at Luke in confusion.

    "Oh. You don't know what any of those are, do you? Don't worry, you'll meet them all in time. But anyway, are you happier now that you know that a lot of things are still the same?"

    She paused, gazing off into the middle distance, before lowering her head, sighing sadly.

    Shieldon: <My herd's still gone, though...>

    Frowning, Luke walked over to the little Dinosaur and sat down next to her, patting her gently.

    Luke: "What was your herd like?"

    She turned to look at Luke, staring up at him wistfully.

    Shieldon: <...They'd go to the ends of the Earth to protect each other. Just like any of my kind would. They kept me safe from predators like Sharpteeth, Flyers or even Quickrippers.>

    Luke didn't know what a "Quickripper" was, nut judging by the name, it didn't seem like the sort of thing you would want to meet at night in a dark ally.

    Luke: "And were you friends with them?"

    Shieldon: <They weren't just my friends. They were my family, too. My Mom and Dad, my Brothers and Sisters, my Cousins...>

    She sniffed sadly, seemingly on the verge of tears.

    <And now they're all gone...and I'm all alone.>

    Luke: "But...you're not alone!"

    She whipped her head back up at Luke, blinking.

    Shieldon: <...What are you talking about?>

    Luke: "Let me tell you something about what humans do. Lots of humans - myself included - work together with Pokémon. They call us Pokémon Trainers."

    Shieldon: <...Okay...>

    Luke: "And Pokémon trainers catch a whole bunch of Pokémon, and they all work together to make each other stronger so we can do amazing things. In a sense...I'm the leader of my own herd. A herd made out of lots of different Pokémon, but still a herd!"

    A little unsure, she responded with the Shieldon equivalent of a shrug.

    Shieldon: <I guess so?>

    Luke: "I know we can't possibly replace your old herd, but...would you like to join my one? I promise, me and my Pokémon will do everything we can to keep you safe. And not only that, we'll help you grow bigger and stronger, too. You want to be a Bastiodon, don't you?"

    He had her there. Ever since the first time she had been defended from a huge Sharptooth by the Bastidon in her herd - who had formed an immovable wall around her and the other Shieldon to keep her safe - she had always wanted to be a Bastiodon so that she could help protect the her herd rather than cowering in fear behind them.

    Shieldon: <Yes. I do.>

    Luke: "Well, I promise I'll do my best to make you into one. We all will. I just need your permission."

    Lowering himself down to her level, Luke extended a hand out to the Shieldon.

    "Will you join my herd?"

    She had to take a few moments to consider this offer. He was right, this wasn't her herd. Not even close. And nothing would ever replace them. But at the same time...he was offering her everything that she wanted in this strange new world. And could she really ask for more than that?

    After a few seconds more, she hesitantly raised her left fore-leg, and held it against Luke's palm in an effort to mimic the gesture.

    Shieldon: <Alright...I'll join you.>

    The Wild Shieldon joined the Team!

    Would you like to give a nickname to the Shieldon you just caught?

    Luke: "Wonderful. Now we need to give you a name."

    She blinked, tilting her head quizzically.

    Shieldon: <A name? But I have a name! It's Shieldon!>

    Luke: "But that's the name of your species! Sure, some people don't give their Pokémon nicknames, but..."

    He frowned.

    "I've always found that strange. It's like calling a human like me "human." How would we tell each other apart! Didn't you ever have trouble telling the members of your herd apart?"

    The little Dinosaur shook her head.

    Shieldon: <No. But we have lots of ways of telling each other apart that you probably don't.>

    Luke: "Yeah, that's the thing. So, do you mind if I give you a nickname? You don't need one, it just makes it easier for me to tell you apart from any other Shieldon or Bastiodon we may meet in the future."

    Shieldon: <...Alright. Do you have anything in mind?>

    Luke grinned sheepishly.

    Luke: "Actually, I've I decided on your name before I even revived you. But you get the final say, of course."

    Shieldon: <And what would that name be?>

    Luke: "Well...I was thinking of calling you Aegis.>

    Shieldon: <...Aegis...>

    Aegis. Aegis...she had never thought of having a name, but Aegis sounded like a decent one. It wasn't too long, was easy to remember. She could see herself getting used to it.

    Having decided she liked it, she gave Luke a nod.

    Shieldon Aegis: <Alright. I guess I'm Aegis now, then.>

    Luke: "Wonderful!"

    Forgetting that the newly christened Aegis weighed fifty-seven Kilograms, Luke attempted to pick her up and give her a hug, but...

    "HURRRRRRGH! HURRRRRRRRRRRRRRRGH!"

    Aegis: <What are you-?>

    Luke: "Trying to hug you! Gah!"

    He gave up, panting.

    "Tell you what, I'll just..."

    Without bothering to try and pick her up, he wrapped his arms around the Shieldon and gave her a hug.

    "Okay, there we go. Welcome to my herd, Aegis."

    Aegis: <...Thank you, Luke. But why have you got your arms wrapped around me?>

    Luke: "It's a hug. It's how a lot of humans and Pokémon with arms display affection. Do...you want me to stop?"

    Aegis: <No, no. You're very warm. And I'm cold-blooded, so...>

    Luke: "Yeah, I get you."

    Imperial Commissar

    Pugilis - Around 600 years ago

    -A young and fresh-faced Warrior is standing in his throne room with an Alakazam stood by his side, shivering slightly.-

    Lucanus: I must say, this place is rather drafty. Isn't it, Revan?

    Revan: ~Did you expect anything else? This is a castle, not a palace. The locals say that fifty good men would be all it takes to hold this place against an army. From my experience, though, fifty good men are harder to acquire than any army.~

    Lucanus: Oh, always the cynic, Revan. Look around you! I now rule over a kingdom of such men. Fine warriors, hardened by the harsh upbringing of their native land, ready to live and die by their honour! Such warriors are the kind of which bards sing about!

    Revan: ~Really? I thought lands such as these would be rather difficult to sing about. After all, I've never heard any songs about the hero's six children dying in the winter due to a poor harvest.~

    Lucanus: Don't be ridiculous! Such things would never happen in my lands!

    -He pauses for a second.-

    Lucanus: Would they not?

    -Revan never manages to make his response, because at that moment he is interrupted by the doors swinging open and another warrior walking in.-

    Warrior: Still playing at castles, are we?

    Lucanus: Ah, Takuma! I was hoping to seek your counsel regarding the strength of our armies.

    Warrior Takuma: Why do you need my counsel, Cain? You're the Warlord now, not me. I'm getting too old to lead armies and wage wars. All I ask of you is that you deliver to me the Ghost Lord's head on a pike. He and his forces have plagued this kingdom for too long. Aside from that, Pugilis is yours to do with as you please. Keep her people safe, and you are free to rule as you choose.

    Lucanus: Of course. I am a knight of the realm, and I will fulfil my oath to you. Though I find it rather peculiar that you would list the well-being of the common peasantry as the one condition for my rule.

    Takuma: A kingdom is nothing without her people, Lucanus. Remember that.

    -Takuma hands Lucanus a rolled-up scroll.-

    Takuma: Now, if you are done requesting my assistance, there is a matter that you as Warlord must attend to.

    Lucanus: What is it? Am I required to host a tourney?

    Takuma: In a manner of speaking. The Warlord of Illusio has requested fourteen of our finest Warriors to host a staged fight in her kingdom. If we refuse, she will cease to offer us her printing services. The arrogance of it... what does she think we are? Wild dogs to tear each other apart for her amusement?

    -He gives a derisive snort.-

    Takuma: Nevertheless, as Warlord, you will be expected to attend the proceedings.

    Lucanus: Oh, yes. I imagine there will be a feast and such festivities to accompany the event.

    Revan: ~Well, that's a good way to gain public favour. Gorge yourself at a feast when your people can barely feed themselves.~

    Takuma: There's not going to be a feast. And if there is, you certainly aren't going to be attending it. Here in Pugilis, only the strong rise to rule. And what better place is there to prove your strength than in the arena?

    Lucanus: Erm... well, naturally. I have won my fair share of tourneys. No doubt we will be able to impress the Warlord of Illusio with our strength and skill. Perhaps I could take her hand at the ball afterwards and regale her with tales of my-

    Takuma: Ha! You're making me laugh, kid. I'd recommend not pulling that kind of thing on Aiura if you want to keep both your hands. Now, I do believe that is everything.

    -Takuma turns and starts to walk out of the throne room.-

    Lucanus: Shouldn't you bow? After all, I am your Warlord.

    Takuma: Kid, I ruled this realm for over thirty years. Once you've been sitting on that chair for that long, then you can start expecting me to bow.

    -And with that, Takuma departs, leaving Lucanus to start reading the scroll he was delivered.-
