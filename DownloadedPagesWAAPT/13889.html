    347214 ChaoticMarin2nd Feb 2014 10:55:21 PM from Nintendoland Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: 700 wives and 300 concubines
    Chiptune DJ

    Tower of Mastery - Balcony

    Valorie was facing off against Korrina again; this time it was personal. She watched in amazement as Korrina's lucario mega evolved before her very eyes and she in turn attempted to activate her own mega stone.

    Nothing happened.

    Korrina hesitated, just sort of decidedly giving Valorie a free pass to try again. There wasn't much fun in taking advantage of the moment of weakness. Yet, as Valorie tried and tried again she was met with no progress. It only took three total tries before Korrina raised a hand to stop her.

    ...A feeling of dread welt up inside Valorie.

    Tower of Mastery

    Back down at the bottom of the tower of mastery Korrina explained in no uncertain terms that the only thing she could think of that would prevent a mega evolution like that would be the lack of a strong enough bond between the pokemon and it's trainer.

    Valorie lowered her gaze to hide her shame.

    "...But how can I fix that?" she asked, unwilling to simply let the issue go.

    "Well... I can't tell you exactly!" Korrina admitted. "Every pokemon is different, so you've just gotta roll with their personalities to see what they like and what they respect. Don't feel bad though; lots of people can't mega evolve!" she replied. Valorie hesitated a moment to reflect on how lame this was before Korrina abruptly continued. "...Though, you have been training this pokemon for some time right? Maybe there's something really specific holding you back." she offered with a shrug.

    Valorie furrowed her brows. "...Alright. I guess I just have to try. Do you know where I can find some roller skates?" she said, quickly changing the subject.

    Korrina's eyes widened briefly in surprise. "Sure! Let's get you set up~" she offered eagerly.

    Route 11

    ...Not all that long later, Valorie stood upon route 11. In all the rush, Gurkinn had forgotten to make sure she gave her mega necklace back. A pang of guilt was felt, but she decidedly wasn't that guilty.

    She had a destiny to fulfill, after all. Releasing Charizard, she began staring into the other pokemon's eyes. The pseudo dragon stared back at her for a moment before snorting, a trail of smoke escaping from her nostrils. Charizard shook her head, averting her gaze.

    Well this was a problem; how was Valorie supposed to figure out what was wrong?!

    "Uh... hey. I can tell this is kind of important; want me to translate?" Shu's ghostly voice spoke up.

    . . . Well that was convenient! "...Yeah, actually; that'd be nice." Valorie responded. The ghost pokemon appeared forth before her to address Charizard, who grunt-roared out something.

    "Hehe... I'm not repeating /that/." Shu remarked light-heartedly, only for Charizard to continue on, seemingly saying something much longer this time.

    "Oh." Shu remarked in mild surprise, turning to face Valorie. "...Charizard is ashamed of you." Shu began hesitantly. Somehow the realization that her pokemon was about to criticize her hit Valorie like a truck, filling her stomach with a sort of dread.

    "She says she wants a strong trainer that isn't afraid to seek out her goals and doesn't need a hug to get her up in the morning. She thinks you're just avoiding the gyms because you don't think you can handle the elite four, and... She says she doesn't know you anymore." Shu explained in a matter-o-fact tone.

    ...Well fuck you too, Charizard! Valorie had never been insulted by a pokemon before, and admittedly she could not contain her rage and indignation. She shot Charizard a harsh glare, only for the pseudo dragon to reach out and shoved her hard to give her a healthy dose of fear to balance off that anger. Charizard lowered her head and growled at Valorie, staring her down.

    The two were having a staring contest, one that Valorie was quickly losing.

    Narrowing her eyes to slits, Valorie grimaced and ripped her gaze away from Charizard, turning her back on the other to hide her misery. That... had been a mistake.

    Charizard's expression filled with rage and her tail erupted into a brilliant blue color. She refused to stand idly by while her trainer continued this self-destructive behavior.

    She had no choice; she'd need to teach Valorie strength the same way she'd been taught, a lesson taught by none other than Valorie herself. It was a dark day when she was forced to this... but the pseudo dragon stepped forward, a guttural roar building up inside her as she prepared herself to cross this threshold.

    ...Valorie would just need to forgive her one day.
    I dropped out of school to become a pokemon master, but then they pulled me right back in to get my doctorate!
