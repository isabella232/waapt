    A ship bound for Hoenn

    -Savannah and Zelner have been on this ship for a couple hours. Savannah has been looking down at Willard, who's been asleep on her lap, while Zelner has been lying on his back and looking out at the ocean. Savannah shifts uncomfortably and glances at Zelner, as she already has several times so far on this trip. She finally works up the courage to say something.-

    Savannah: Um...

    -Zelner sits up.-

    Zelner: Hm? What is it?

    -Savannah looks down and blushes.-

    Savannah: Y-you haven't said m-much at all th-this entire trip.

    -Zelner smiles and looks back at the water.-

    Zelner: Well... neither have you.

    Savannah: B-but I-I'm...

    -Her voice trails off.-

    Zelner: It's okay to speak your mind with me, you know? I don't get upset very easily, if that's a concern.

    Savannah: I kn-know... it's just...

    Zelner: Well... It makes sense. You don't know me very well at all.

    -He looks at Savannah again.-

    Zelner: There's probably not a good way to fit this into a conversation, but it's been on my mind a lot today... I apologize for the sudden change in topic.

    -He looks out at the water.-

    Zelner: In truth, I used to be a lot like you are now. Probably even a bit worse. I couldn't ever say much of anything to anybody. And, well... I'm not sure what changed, but...

    -He rests his arms on the side of the ship.-

    Zelner: I just... grew confident. I suppose that's one way of putting it. I decided I'd say what was on my mind, regardless of what the people around me would think. Whether or not that's entirely positive depends on the person. That change wasn't simple, or quick. But over time, it happened. And it made me...

    -He looks back at Savannah.-

    Zelner: ...it made me more me, if that makes any sense.

    -He sighs.-

    Zelner: ...I'm sorry, I'm certain that could have been phrased more eloquently.

    -Savannah looks down again.-

    Savannah: N-no, that was f-fine.

    Zelner: I never did answer your question...

    -He looks out at the water again.-

    Zelner: I'm... content. I'm just the sort of person who's happiest when he has plenty of time to think.

    -He lies on his back again and looks back at Savannah.-

    Zelner: And what about you?

    -Savannah pauses for a moment.-

    Savannah: I-I'm a bit b-bored, in t-truth...

    -Zelner looks back at the water.-

    Zelner: Sorry if I'm asking too many questions, but didn't you pack a few games just before we left? Why haven't you played those?

    -Savannah blushes, smiles nervously, and looks back at Willard.-

    Savannah: I w-was afraid they'd g-get wet...

    -Zelner smiles. The ship probably arrives near Petalburg soon after.-
    "Sing with me a song of birthrights and love."
