    Bard: <My trainer has decided that this would be an ideal time and place to demonstrate his acting talents.>

    -Inside the gathering, Minstrel is stood atop Troubadour's back, bundled up in a thick black cloak and waving his cane like a conductor's baton. Poet is crouched beneath Troubadour, out of sight.-

    Minstrel: Welcome, welcome, one and all! Tired of your tedious tasks and toil? Eager for some extraordinary event to enlighten your evening? Then come to have your monotony ministered to by a peformance from Minstrel, master of the arts!

    Heckler: What's this weirdo up to? Is he trying to sell us something?

    -He cups his hand around his ear as though straining to hear.-

    Minstrel: Oh? What was that? "But Minstrel, you dashing devil!" I hear you cry. "How do you intend to put on a performance when you can only passably portray one protagonist?"

    Heckler: Hey, I didn't say anything like-

    Minstrel: Why, your lack of faith wounds me most deeply, dear audience, yet your doubts are definitely dire. Fear not, however! It is the ability of the actor to take on an assembly of roles, and though an amateur may only attempt alacritous alternation, I can switch between my multitude of personages as quickly and easily in the time it takes for you to blink. Behold!

    -He jumps down from Troubadour's back at the same time as Poet uses Ancient Power to create a large wall of boulders, blocking him from view. When she drops it again after a few moments, there is a timid-looking girl whose long blonde hair and flowing dress blow gently in the breeze surrounding the rocky outcropping she's stood upon.-

    Princess: I can be the designated d-damsel in distress... holding out for a h-hero until the end of the night...

    -She suddenly stands up straight, draws a dirk from beneath her dress and cuts off a chunk of her hair, tearing off her gown to reveal a boiled leather cuirass beneath it.-

    Adventurer: Or I can be the brave and determined warrior, setting out on my quest to become a true knight!

    -Poet shields her with another barricade, this time using Grass Knot to create a row of dark and imposing vines. They soon collapse to reveal a hideous, misshapen creature with a hunchback and a face burnt beyond recognition, purple mist clinging to its ankles.-

    Monster:    No matter how malformed, how inhuman...   

    -The purple mist is built up into a Shadow Ball that shields the creature, then collapsed, fading to unveil a figure shrouded in white and green that could, at first glance, pass for a Gardevoir.-

    Gardevoir: No creature on earth is beyond my acumen!

    -The Gardevoir raises its arms and suddenly the surrounding area is engulfed by a flash of light, followed by a puff of smoke. As it clears, Minstrel can once again be seen sat atop Troubadour, having now shed his thick black cloak.-

    Minstrel: Yes, my friends, all of these fascinating feats of impersonation and more are well within my ability. So if you should ever suddenly be seized by the urge to see similar sights, remember the moniker of Minstrel, acclaimed actor and confident Coordinator. Adieu!
