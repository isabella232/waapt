    -Lucanus is sitting in his war room, handing a rolled-up scroll of parchment to a nervous looking messenger. A slender Warrior of indeterminate gender is standing by his side, visor down.-

    Lucanus: I don't care how you get past their defences. All that matters to me is that it is done. Terrera's current state of lockdown leaves us at a severe tactical disadvantage, since we are effectively cut off from Eastern Ransei, and I feel that Sabaku needs to be... persuaded to open his borders once more.

    Messenger: U-understood, sir.

    -The messenger gives a hasty bow, then runs off. Lucanus gives a sigh and turns to the Warrior by his side.-

    Lucanus: I do hope that Sabaku understands that my actions are not driven by a desire to conquer. We have not recieved our regular food deliveries from Illusio for some time, and as such we will need the trade routes that Terrera provides if we wish to avoid another famine.

    Warrior: I'm sure he will see reason, my lord. Sabaku is wise enough to understand that he does not want Pugilis as his enemy.

    Lucanus: I suppose. Still, I feel that I could have worded my letter more eloquently. What do you think, Ayame?

    Warrior Ayame: I can't really comment on- oh. Oh dear.

    Lucanus: Oh dear indeed.

    -Before Ayame can move, Lucanus has her bound with several summoned Aura chains.-

    Lucanus: I find it hard to believe that you thought I wouldn't recognise your voice behind that visor. How long have you been working against me?

    Ayame: U-um... well, I don't think I can-

    Lucanus: Never mind. That information is irrelevant. What matters is that you are here now before me. How very ironic... a spymaster performing espionage against the very nation she is supposed to protect from such acts.

    -Lucanus starts pacing up and down the room, not looking at Ayame.-

    Lucanus: You are aware how I will have to punish you, correct? The punishment for spying against Spectra or her allies is death.

    Ayame: ...Yes, I know that.

    Lucanus: And you would be willing to risk this punishment in order to undermine my leadership?

    -Ayame's only response is a long period of silence. Lucanus sighs, then dispels the Aura chains binding her.-

    Lucanus: Go.

    Ayame: ...What?

    Lucanus: You heard me. Run. If you are quick enough you should be able to slip past the guards before they raise the alarm. I will not execute an old friend simply for choosing the opposite side, but if I see you around here again I will be forced to mete out the appropriate justice. Now, what are you waiting for?

    -Ayame hesitates for a moment, seemingly in disbelief, then darts out of the room and off of the balcony. Lucanus looks around the now-empty room.-

    Lucanus: ...I'm going soft, aren't I, Revan?

    -He gives a chuckle devoid of any sort of amusement, then starts pacing up and down the room again.-
