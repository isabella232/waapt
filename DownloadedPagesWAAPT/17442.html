    Aaaand here we go. Note that this will be written in a somewhat different style to my usual fare.

    "That", Petalburg

    For thirty minutes or so, the curtain remains inert. The Magma grunts, who number somewhere from 100 to 200 individuals, are the main audience, standing around and chatting amongst themselves about topics with varying levels of connection to Team Magma. Accompanying them are 20 to 30 ordinary citizens, Tori among them, whose general reaction to this elaborate setup appears to be intrigue blended with a certain amount of distrust for the hooded figures who nearly destroyed their world several years ago. Occasionally, an extra red hood or two can be see flittering around in the mechanisms of the curtain, presumably giving the rather cheap technology a quick once-over before... whatever comes next.

    The first sign that events are finally starting to unfold is one that cannot be ignored - except, perhaps, by the totally deaf. Without warning...

    "SILENCE!"

    ...a voice roars out from behind the curtain. Every single Magma grunt immediately falls quiet and snaps to attention, followed quickly by normal citizens who had been seconds away from abject panic when they heard the sound. It's hard to blame either side for their response - the voice is terrifying, pitched down and distorted to almost inhuman extremes and shouted through some sort of primitive amplifier that gives it an ear-bleeding clipped quality.

    Seconds later, there is another, far quieter sound. It is the distinctive whoosh of countless Pokeballs being opened backstage.

    Then the pulleys start to turn, the curtain swings open, and the flames erupt.

    To claim that the sky itself caught fire is an old cliché, but anyone who stumbled across the stage at this exact moment would be hard-pressed to think of a better description for what is taking place. Nothing is visible but a churning wall of white-hot flame that consumes the entire stage. Through the flame, vague hints of humanoid figures moving into position can be made out, but the brightness of the fire makes details indiscernible. Whether a sane health and safety commission would even think of allowing such a performance is a mystery for the ages, not that anyone exposed to those few seconds of blinding luminescence could think of anything except how much they missed having eyes that weren't full of whiteness.

    Eventually, the flames sputter out as quickly as they came. The viewers, still clearing the static out of their eyes, don't notice a contingent of Fire-type Pokémon being recalled by their trainers, some of them coughing up little jets of steam and looking slightly ill. What they should notice is the man, clothed from head to toe in searing red, who made his way onto the stage behind the wall of fire that conveniently concealed his movements, accompanied by an array of red banners in the background. The banners depict a huge variety of intricately drawn scenes in a style resembling ancient [[Greek]] pottery, but they all contain one symbol - a large orange M in a pseudo-handwriting chickenscratch font, contained within a perfect circle.

    "Greetings, my fellows!"

    His voice is the same voice that bellowed out the order at the beginning of this whole affair. However, the volume is no longer deafening, and his tone is significantly calmer, with an accent that can't be properly made out through the brickwalled distortion.

    And I shall end it there. It may seem like an abrupt ending, but that's just you assuming this is over; I'll do more stuff once Tori responds to this, since Hephaestus will coincidentally pick her for an audience participation segment.

    Please don't use the term "life hacks" unless you happen to be a Reality Warper.
