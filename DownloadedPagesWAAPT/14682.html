    Anistar

    Kaen: "Once, there was a Songbird who lived in the woods. The Songbird was all that was beautiful and bright and good. And all who heard the Songbird were happy, happier than ever before. No one could hear the Songbird's tune and harbor ill feelings in their hearts. And the woods prospered, and the Songbird sang with joy and laughter.

    But then, one day, something arrived in the woods. Something old. Something dark. Something hungry. A shadow, it slipped under the trees, and over the rocks, keeping to the darkness, avoiding the dappled sunlight. Finally, the shadow reached a point where it could not advance without leaving cover. And so it took on a new form, a form familiar to it as breathing is to us. The shadow became a wolf. And so the Wolf came to the woods. Soon, the Wolf came upon the Songbird's tree, where the Songbird was singing with laughter and joy. And the shadow that was the Wolf could not bear to hear such beauty. The Wolf lashed out, killing many of the Songbird's friends. Blood stained the ground, and the Songbird cried, but still the Songbird sang with joy, even as the tears of grief trickled down its cheeks. And so the Wolf left for the night. But the next day the shadow that was the Wolf returned. And again its dark heart lashed out, in blood and pain and death. But still the Songbird sang, song unbroken. And the days past in such a way, until at last the Songbird's friends were all dead. The Songbird's tune was faint, failing, slowing, coming to an end. And still the shadow that was the Wolf came. And at last the Songbird broke. In rage and grief and pain, the Songbird sang a new song. A song of shadow and blood and fire. And the woods burned with a righteous fury like the wrath of old gods. And not even the shadow that was the Wolf could withstand such an inferno, and its form burned away, leaving it again as a weak, tiny, hateful dark thing, which slipped away down into the earth through cracks in the rocks. And the Songbird sang a dirge, one last terrible note, before flying silently into the fire, to pass from the woods for good.

    But still the shadow exists, and still it walks the land dark and hateful. Few things can harm such an old and evil thing. A dagger forged of metal not of this world, given in love, wielded by one pure of heart. A fire lit by a song sung in the lost innocence of one once joyous. There may be more ways to injure the shadow that was the Wolf, but if there are, they have been lost to time and darkness. Now, I bid you goodbye. Speak not of this story for at least three days hence, lest you attract the attention of something far worse."

    The red haired sweeper shuffles away, whistling to himself.
