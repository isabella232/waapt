    465099 Herbert40k17th Aug 2015 08:18:50 AM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
    Imperial Commissar
    Thanks to Moon for spagging this with me.

    Sootopolis City - Yesterday

    Shun, forced to assume that Joseph has spaced out on account of him not responding to questions for several minutes, decides to sidle quietly away from the conversation.

    Sootopolis Pokemon Center

    Shun is sitting in his room, reading through the notes he took on the Blaise family back in Lilycove whilst Gregson is curled up and sleeping contentedly on the bed.

    Shun: ...None of these dates overlap with the day of the abduction. The closest I can find is a week beforehand, when the family were staying in the Lilycove Hotel for a "business meeting". However, there is no mention of who they were meeting with...

    Outside, the sound of a familiar voice is faintly audible through the door.

    Sakura: Stick close to me for today, okay? You got some nasty burns on your chest and I want to make sure you won't get in trouble...

    Shun hears Sakura from the other side of his door, raises an eyebrow curiously, then puts the files down on his desk and sticks his head outside.

    Shun: Is something wrong?

    Upon looking, he sees Sakura accompanied by a Greninja with some bandages on his chest.

    Sakura: Oh, hi Shun. It's nothing to worry about.

    Shun looks the Greninja's rather familiar-looking burn injuries up and down, eyebrow still raised.

    Shun: ...Hrm.

    He turns back to Sakura.

    Shun: ...Accidents happen, I suppose. At the very least he appears to be up and walking with little difficulty.

    Sakura: Yes, he's pretty much healed, probably will be taking the bandages out soon as it wasn't that bad. We just had some rough days.

    Shun: I can understand. Rough days are hardly uncommon in my line of work. Some of the more recent transgressions of the law I've been called upon to deal with have been... taxing.

    Sakura: You do deal with crime, I honestly would expect any day where something happens to be on the rough side.

    Shun: Yes, but I don't typically expect any day where something happens to involve one costumed oddball hijacking a rocket in order to win the affections of another costumed oddball. That particular set of circumstances was not covered on the training course. Nor, for instance, do I usually have to form an uneasy alliance with a hotelier of questionable morality to secure information on persons of even more questionable morality.

    He gives a long, exhausted exhale.

    Shun: It's been a long couple of weeks.

    Sakura: That sounds like a lot of stress for a little kid like you.

    She giggles a bit, though she seemed to feel a bit sorry for him. Shun scowls.

    Shun: I'm not a "little kid". I'm an officer of the International Police and more than qualified to deal with the demands of the job.

    He stifles a yawn.

    Shun: Even if it does get exhausting at times...

    Sakura giggles again

    Sakura: Aren't you just precious. Shun, I'm not saying that you're not any of that, but as capable as you are, someone of your age should not be dealing with the kind of stuff you do, you can't really deny that.

    Shun tilts his head.

    Shun: And you haven't dealt with similar experiences? You're a known associate of the J-Team and you're only a few years older than I am.

    Sakura: I never said it was easy for me. I've dealt with a lot and it was definitely very stressful, to say the least.

    She sighs.

    Shun: ...Would you be comfortable with elaborating?

    Sakura stops to think for a bit and sighs again.

    Sakura: Not long ago my sister's boyfriend got kidnapped by Cipher and the one responsible basically blackmailed her into doing his bidding while I was trying to help her save him. Not only that, but I've been held at gunpoint before and almost died during said quest.

    Shun opens his mouth to reply, then remembers how his previous attempt at consoling a traumatised victim went and closes it again.

    Sakura: I've had other rough times too, but nothing still was as bad as when I was indirectly responsible for the death of my own parents.

    She sighs again.

    Sakura: Believe me, my life was not easy. I'm very much hoping I can live a life with no concerns in the future, or at least no concerns of that level.

    This time, Shun can't hold back the first thought that comes to his mind.

    Shun: How did that...?

    Sakura: That what?

    Shun looks down at his feet.

    Shun: ...Your parents. Assuming you're willing to say... how did...?

    Sakura: I was playing with my sister's Sandshrew while we were going through a cave, I wanted to see what he could do and he showed. He made the whole place shake and the cave was a bit unstable so... You can probably guess.

    Shun: I... I'm sorry. I wasn't... I didn't mean to make you...

    He appears to be having difficulty keeping his own emotions in check. Sakura lets out a small smile and then walks up to him, pulling him into a hug.

    Sakura: Don't worry about me, everything is just fine now. Plus, I guess at least I got to see you when you're not trying so hard to act professional and serious.

    Shun doesn't resist Sakura's hug, but manages to keep himself together.

    Shun: No, I... I'm alright. It would be callous of me to lose my composure now, when your loss was far worse than my own...

    Sakura breaks the hug and steps back a bit.

    Sakura: Shun, you don't need to be so polite and professional around me like that, especially when we're just having a casual conversation. Plus, hearing someone that's like, four years younger than me have a better vocabulary than me is pretty embarrassing

    She giggled again.

    Shun: ...I was only trying to maintain a manner of speech that showed due deference to the seriousness of the subject matter. Was it not appropriate?

    Sakura giggles considerably more this time.

    Sakura: I've moved on, Shun, this all happened a long time ago and I don't want to let the past keep dragging me down. I'm more concerned with the now and the future right now.

    Shun nods.

    Shun: I understand. Still, as you offered to help with my own personal investigation, so too shall I offer to help with yours. Should you require assistance in bringing the kidnappers who threatened you and your sister to justice, I am more than willing to provide it.

    Sakura: Eeeeh... The whole thing about the person who tried to shoot me dead is a bit complicated and I'd rather not talk much about that one. The one who blackmailed my sister though... I'm not really hunting him down and honestly I think going after him is probably only going to result in more pain for everybody involved.

    Shun looks almost offended by this proposition.

    Shun: ...So you're content with allowing them to escape justice, free to commit further crimes without consequence? If you would rather not be personally involved, then I can sympathise, but in the eyes of the law such a case is still very much open.

    Sakura can't help but smile.

    Sakura: You really remind me of someone with that attitude...

    The smile soon turns into a grimace.

    Sakura: And considering how he ended up that's probably not a good thing.

    Shun folds his arms.

    Shun: I know my job has its risks. I learnt that on the day my parents were murdered. If we were all unwilling to speak out against injustice for fear of retribution, then there would be nobody left to oppose it. All that is necessary for the triumph of evil is for good people to do nothing.

    Sakura: You're right. People have to act more in order for criminals to be stopped instead of just allowing it to happen. That's why I want to help you with your case.

    Shun: And in turn, you'll allow me to help with yours?

    Sakura: I don't think I have any right to stop you, especially since I wasn't even the main victim. If you want more details on that story you'll probably have to ask my sister and her boyfriend about it.

    Shun: If I happen to meet them, I will.

    He bows his head.

    Shun: Thank you for confiding in me. I... there's not many people I can talk so openly with.

    Sakura: They travel with me so you could probably find them really easily. It's really to miss Duke, he's like, two meters tall and built like a truck.

    She giggles.

    Shun: ...I'll keep that in mind.

    Sakura: Anyway, are you really that closed off to other people? As much as I like that you're willing to be open with me, I can't help but imagine you must be quite lonely.

    Shun frowns.

    Shun: ...My job doesn't leave me with a lot of time for socialising. That, and I grew up in a relatively small town. The only company I had was my brother, and... we were never on the best of terms.

    Sakura: How sad... Me and my sister have our issues but we're on very good terms with each other. I also grew up in a small town and I wasn't one of the more social girls out there. The whole accident with my parents forced me to change that though.

    Shun: I see... I suppose that's another way in which we're alike.

    Sakura: I guess, but it seems like we went on some rather different routes when it comes to what we decided to with our lives.

    Shun almost smiles.

    Shun: ...You could say that. Now then, I assume you have something you need to be getting to, so I won't hold you up for any longer.

    Sakura: Oh, right...

    She turns to face the Greninja behind her.

    Sakura: I almost forgot you were there, let's go get something to eat.

    She giggles as the Pokémon just rolled his eyes.

    Sakura: I'll see you later, Shun.

    She waves and then begins heading downstairs alongside her Greninja. Shun watches them go, his expression creased thoughtfully, then heads back into his room.
