    Several minutes later, the dance winded down, Rex taking the opportunity to slip away from the group.

    "Ugh...I hate dancing," he mumbled. "I thought I was going to die of embarrassment..."

    "Hey, where are you going?" someone called, and Rex cringed, fearing that it was the woman again. However, he turned to see that it was one of the female dancers who'd called out to him. A male dancer accompanied her over, both of them stopping in front of him. "You can't leave yet. Not until you show us that dance again~"

    "It was one of a kind," the male said.

    "Never seen anything like that before," the girl agreed.

    Rex felt his eye twitch, resisting the urge to let out Rascal and show them a real flaming dance. "Mrrggh..."

    "Hey hey," the male said, noticing his anger. "We're only teasing. You did pretty good, to be honest."

    "Yeah. Most visitors freeze on the spot when Kala grabs them up like that," the girl said with a smile.

    "...Thanks, I guess," Rex sighed, letting go of his anger. "So, what kind of festival is this, anyway?"

    "You've never heard of the Festival of Flames?" the girl asked.

    "It's one of the various rituals we celebrate on Shamouti Island," the male added. "This particular festival is meant to honor Moltres, bringer of Spring."

    "Moltres?" Rex asked. "Is it here?"

    "Well, yes and no," the girl giggled. "Moltres roosts on Fire Island around this time of year. ...By the way, I noticed that you were a trainer." Her eyes narrowed suddenly, her tone becoming menacing. "You weren't planning on    CATCHING    it, were you?"

    Rex put up his hands as if trying to ward the girl off. "N-no...I wouldn't dream of it."

    "Good!" she chirped, reverting back to her normal self. "It'd be really bad if you did."

    '...Note to self: watch out for island girls.'
