    248035 luke92429th Apr 2013 08:33:14 AM Get RP Mod

    Chargestone Cave - Lake & Treeo

    The odd pair of Alligator and Gecko made their way through the cave, keeping an eye out for any sign of Dream Rifts or Lyrui's Pokémon.

    Treeo: <Nope, nothing so far.>

    Lake: <Let's keep moving.>

    The Wood Gecko nodded, then followed Lake as they entered a tunnel and headed deeper into the cave.

    <Say Treeo, I'm curious...>

    Treeo: <Oh?>

    His attention perked, Treeo turned his head to the Feraligatr as they walked onward.

    <What about?>

    Lake: <You know how you're a Grass type? We'll, I'm kinda' wondering, how much of you is a plant? Are you a lizard that looks like a plant? A plant that looks like a lizard, or something else?>

    Treeo: <Ooh, there's a very interesting answer to that, actually!>

    Lake: <Oh yeah?>

    Treeo: <Yep! You see, there are actually two sorts of Grass type Pokémon. The first sort of Pokémon that are actually plants!>

    Lake: <You mean Pokémon like Sunflora or Lilligant?>

    Lake nodded.

    <Yeah, I figured as much. I guess you're the other type, then?>

    Treeo: <That's right! You see, long, long ago, my earliest ancestors, as well as Pokémon like Venusaur, Tropius, Meganium and others, were normal Pokémon that weren't Grass types at all. But these Pokémon eventually figured out that plants could help them survive in the wild.

    Like, for example, they would use certain types of flowers to spray Poisonpowder or Stun Spore on predators that tried to eat them.>

    Lake: <Hmm. Makes sense.>

    Treeo: <They started to make more and more use of these plants, and after thousands of years of evolution - the slow sort, not the fast sort - eventually these Pokémon started being born with these plants growing on them in a sort of symbiotic relationship.>

    Lake: <So they became a part of them, then.>

    Treeo: <Oh yeah. As time went on, these plants became more and more integrated into their bodies, until it got to the point where it had gotten impossible to tell where the plants ended and the Pokémon began.

    Which brings us back to myself and your original question. The answer to that being that I am both Lizard and plant at the same time. Our systems are so well integrated with each other that we've become a single entity.>

    Lake: <Amazing. That's evolution for you, eh?>

    Treeo: <Oh yeah, evolution is amazing like that.>

    Lake: <Shame us Water types aren't so interesting. My ancestors were like "Hey, let's live in the water!", and then we spent so long living in water that we got really good at manipulating it.>

    Hearing this, Treeo chuckled slightly.

    Treeo: <So are you saying you'd rather be a Grass Type?>

    Lake: <Eh? Oh, well, I guess it must have it's perks. Like that whole Photosynthesis thing. Hungry? Just lay in the sun and do nothing, and you'll be full of energy in no-time!>

    Treeo: <Hey, it's not that simple! While I do have Photosynthesis, it only makes up about half of my food intake! And it's not much good when it's nighttime or the sun's not out!>

    Lake: <Hmm. How about you? Would you rather be a Water type?>

    Treeo: <Erm...>

    Rubbing his chin, Treeo pondered on this for a few seconds.

    <Being able to swim really well does sound fun. And I wouldn't have my Fire-type weakness anymore. That's always a plus. And I'll admit...I wouldn't mind being able to use Surf the way you can.>

    Lake: <Heheh. I've noticed that's a real crowd-pleaser lately. Anyway, we should be keeping an eye out for those kids.>

    Treeo: <Yeah, you're right. Let's shut up and keep our eyes open.>

    And thus, they continued their searching.
