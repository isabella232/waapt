    As Luke gave the issue more thought, Unite fidgeted uncomfortably.

    Unite: <Buddy, I'm just as curious as you about these things, but you've gotta' remember we're on the clock here!>

    Luke: Ah! Right, right! Did you see any way downwards to where the gun might be?

    The bird shook his head.

    Unite: <Not yet, but we haven't looked at the back yet. Let's got take a peek.>

    And the two left the unexplained gel orbs where they had found them...

    Bridge  1:31 to Cannon Fire

    But they wouldn't remain that way for much longer. Via the ships' security system, Sinclair had been watching and listening as the two intruders searched for the way to the gun control, occasionally pausing to comment on something that had taken their interest.

    Quite a peculiar pair, too. The human  who Sinclair could only assume was the Braviary's trainer as they were only indigenous to a Region halfway across the world  seemed to parse his speech as if he were fluent in Monese.

    This took him back. In the days long passed, when humans had yet to make many of their technological advancements  as well as possessing a tendency to go to war at a drop of a hat  making bonds with Pokémon had been much harder before the breakthrough of Pokéballs.

    With fewer companions, the humans had been able to forge such deep bonds with the Pokémon they did have they came to understand their language naturally.

    It was sad then, now that humans relied on Pokémon more than ever, they had forgotten this special time, content to make do with having only a vague understanding of how their Pokémon really felt.

    What a shame it was that his master Torque was trying to conquer the world for the sake of power more than anything else. Perhaps he could have sparked the catalyst for change.

    The robotic spider shook his head to wrangle back his wandering mind. As intriguing as the human was, something had to be done about him and his Braviary before he could sabotage the Wave Motion Cannon and halt Torque's plan.

    He had seen them examining his newest  and perhaps greatest  creation, the F.P.A.C.S. While it had tested favorably enough in practice scenarios to authorize mass production, it was still untested in the field.

    As he brought up the F.P.A.C.S control panel on his computer screen, he chuckled to himself as he sent the dormant units their instructions. As they had been clueless to their true purpose, it was time for the human the Braviary to find out first-hand.

    Factory Floor  1:30 to Cannon Fire

    The computerized orbs sprung to life, glowing softly as they silently emitted electromagnetic waves into the gel surrounding them. At once, the jelly-like substance began to shift, growing in size as they took on a new form, producing a rather sickly sounding squelching as they did so.

    Hearing something going on behind them, Luke and Unite stopped, glancing at each other.

    Luke: Are you making those noises?

    Stopping on the spot, he gave Luke a look of disgust.

    Unite: <Yeah, because Braviary are well known for their disgusting squelching. It's coming from over...>

    He turned around to point a wing back where they came, only for him to freeze, his pupils dilating in shock.

    <...There.>

    Luke quickly spun around, only for him to gaze wide-eyed at what awaited.

    Luke: ...Huh...Never would have guessed.

    430040 Luke992421st Nov 2014 02:43:29 PM from England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: Noddin' my head like yeah

    1:29 to Cannon Fire

    Where there were once four globs of gel with spherical computers entrapped within, now stood four gelatin forms. The mechanical orbs were now suspended in the mid-section of their gel bodies, pulsing lights blinking to indicate they were now active.

    All four of them had assumed shapes close to that of existing Pokémon. The major difference that gave off a distinct feeling of the uncanny being that rather than fur, feathers, scales, flesh, or anything natural. They were nothing but a single colour of gel.

    Two of these false Pokémon  colored in a metal grey gel  had taken the form of a pair of Skarmory, the what would be metal birds hovering in the air menacingly. The other two  in a shade of lime green  were a towering pair of Haxarous. Still intimidating even if made from what was essentially jelly.

    It wasn't a surprise that Luke and Unite were shaken. Especially as the mockeries of Pokémon assumed aggressive stances.

    Luke: Oh Arceus. Oh Arceus, I wasn't prepared for this!

    Unite: <And you think I was?!>

    Luke: Okay, let's not panic. They're Pokémon. They're made out of jelly, but they're still Pokémon! Unite, Arie-

    Luke was cut off by the false Skarmory screeching loudly. Except in a mockery of a real Skarmory's cry, in a way that sounded like they were gargling water at the same time, the two opened their beaks wide and fired a burst of metallic energy at the human.

    The False Skarmory used Flash Cannon!

    ACK!

    Unite: <I don't think so!>

    Jumping in front of his trainer, Unite raised his wings around himself. This action spontaneously formed a deep blue barrier around the bird, the bolts of energy exploding against it harmlessly.

    Unite used Protect!

    Luke had been knocked off his feet, but was otherwise fine. He paused to wipe his brow.

    Luke: Thank Arceus I taught Protect to all of my Pokémon!

    Flapping his wings, Unite flew up as he glared at the group of Pokémon. With a gurgling roar, the Haxorus charged at the bird.

    Unite: <You want some, Jello-shots? Then come and get it!>

    All of a sudden, his wings flashed white as they seemingly transformed into metal...

    Unite used Steel Wing!

    And with a loud screech, he charged the dragons in kind. One of them reared back it's head, then swung a sharp axe at him...

    The False Haxorus used Dual Chop!

    but the Braviary raised a wing a parried the attack with a wing with a loud clang. To his surprise, the Haxorus' hatchet-like tusk hardening upon impact. Anticipating the second swing, the Braviary raised his other wing to block again.

    With all of his strength, he pushed back, managing to tip the Dragon off balance, giving him the chance to strike.

    <I think I'll cut you down to size!>

    With a slash of his wing, the Braviary struck the Haxorus across the neck, and  to his surprise  slicing clean through. The Dragon's head falling cleanly off from the vertical slice, spraying small flecks of gel everywhere.

    Unite hadn't expected this. He also didn't expect one of the Skarmory to come out of nowhere and slash him across the back in one quick motion.

    The False Skarmory used Aerial Ace!

    <YOW!>

    As the now headless Haxorus collapsed to the floor, Unite spun around to retaliate, only for the other Haxorus to slam him with his thick tail while his back was turned.

    The False Haxorus used Dragon Tail!

    <BWARK!!!>

    The Braviary soared involuntary into the air, soaring up high.

    Luke: Oh no!

    Wanting to try and help as best he could, his trainer rushed after him as he began to come down, holding his arms outstretched.

    I've got you! I'VE GOT YOU!

    Unite: <No you don't!>

    Luke: Yes I do!

    '''Unite: <YOU DON'T!>

    Luke: I DO! I D-ARGH!!!

    The bird crashed into him with a thud, knocking them both to the floor, dazed.

    ...Got you.
