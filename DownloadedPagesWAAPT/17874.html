    Meteor Falls

    While Lyuri is resting, she and Duke hear something approaching.

    "<Is somebody here?>"

    It turns out to be a Deino, walking around and looking all over the place, with Lyuri and Duke being in plain sight.

    "<I thought I had heard someone just now...>" he said.

    "Deino are blind right?" asked Duke

    "Well duh. We're right in front of him and he didn't see us, what do you think?." said Lyuri

    "<Hey, I might not be able to see like you two, but I'm not deaf!>" said the Deino, now looking straight at them

    "Uh... Sorry?" said Duke, in a slightly sheepish way.

    "<It's okay. What are you anyway? You smell like a Pokémon, but I don't know which Pokémon. And the other one is a human. It's not always that one shows up here. Though I think last time they did they beat up my mom and dad.>" said the Deino, very casually.

    "Uh, well, I'm a human as well... Right now." said Duke

    "And are your parents ok?" asked Lyuri, raising an eyebrow.

    "<Really? But you don't smell like a human...>"

    The Deino then walks closer to Duke and begins smelling him.

    "<Weird... By the way, mom and dad are fine. They're just sore and grumpy.>"

    "Uh... Good to know." said Lyuri

    "<Yeah, yeah. Anyway, I'll be going now. Be careful not to fall here, it's pretty easy to do that.>"

    The Deino then begins walking away, while Duke and Lyuri just look to each other.

    "I wonder how difficult it must be, being unable to see." said Duke

    "Not easy. It's probably even worse when you don't have a crutch or something to help you see where you're going. Otherwise..."

    They then look back at the Deino and notice that he was quite literally going to walk off a cliff. Seeing that, Duke quickly rushed over to the Deino just as he began to fall off and manages to grab him before he'd end up hitting the ground head-first.

    "<... Oh. I went the wrong way.>" said the Deino, casually as he noticed that he was being held in the air.

    "Y-You sure did..." said Duke, sighing in relief as his body was almost halfway hanging out.
