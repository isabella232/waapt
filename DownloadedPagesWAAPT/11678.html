    Holon City, Museum

    -Revan floats into Lucanus' new exhibit, phasing through the crowds as he goes. A passing glimpse around the exhibit tells him that it doesn't paint Lucanus in a particularly flattening light, though he skims over the details.-

    Revan: <Now this is hardly fair. Yeah, he was a terrible Warlord, but he died in battle against someone who is almost universally agreed to have been even worse. Surely they could've cut him some slack.>

    -He takes a moment to examine the text on a nearby monitor that forms part of one of the displays.-

    Until recently, Lucanus' survival and unexpected betrayal went unnoted by history. Were it not for the detailed Time Flower records that Shokaki kept, which detail his journey through Ransei as a merchant, we would still be oblivious to it today. Touch the screen to hear Shokaki's second recording.

    Revan: <...Okay, now they're obviously just going out of their way to insult him.>

    -Nevertheless, Revan passes a hand through the screen, and the text is replaced with a recording of an aged Sho. He speaks with an expression and tone worthy of the subject.-

    Sho: Soon after the Ghost Lord's conquest of Pugilis and capture of her Warlord, Lucanus, the Ghost Lord saw fit to place Lucanus back on the throne of Pugilis.

    Revan: <Wait a second. The Ghost Lord executed Lucanus after the battle ended... didn't he?>

    Sho: However, the Lucanus that returned was not the benevolent man that was once so popular. The man that sat on the throne of Pugilis was a tyrant. Oppression was rampant and the people of Pugilis now feared and hated the man they once respected and loved.

    Revan: <That can't be right... Lucanus would never->

    Sho: A short time after Lucanus' placement, Lady Aiura, Warlady of Illusio, announced she was pregnant with Lucanus' child. It was an open secret the two had been in love, but the pain had been dealt. Many problems that plagued Pugilis in that time only increased under the fist of the changed Lucanus. Riots were common after his public defeat at the hands of Celzvet, Warlord of Cragspur, and poverty and living conditions became worse. It was a few weeks later that Lucanus defeated Celzvet and his army, capturing Celzvet in the bargain. Celzvet was taken to Spectra and died at the hands of Ghost Lord and Lucanus.

    Revan: <No...>

    Sho: Towards the end of the Ghost Lord's reign, Lucanus seemed to have a change of heart and fought with the Warlords, Warriors, and myself as we assisted the Lady Amaterasu in defeating the Ghost Lord. In the time that passed as I moved up in the world, I saw Lucanus' name and feats start to fade from the minds and memories of the people of the world. No doubt he wanted to be forgotten.

    -Sho's tone chages, becoming cold and bitter.-

    Sho: However, I had been holding a grudge against Lucanus ever since he ordered one of my partners maimed.

    -Sho's expression turns to one of outright hatred, his tone has gotten even colder and is full of venom.-

    Sho: This recording is my revenge against that bastard. A unbiased account of Lucanus' actions during the war, so that the world may yet know of his deeds, is a greater blow to Lucanus than any lies or slander I could spread. I have dedicated several time flowers to document Lucanus' deeds, both the good and the bad. So that he may be remembered as he truly was.

    Revan: <...>

    Herbert: So... been catching up on what you missed after your death, have you?

    -Revan turns around to see Herbert approaching behind him, looking haggard and downtrodden.-

    Revan: <...You were there. Is this... did Lucanus really...?>

    Herbert: Yup. It's all true.

    Revan: <But... Umbra never mentioned...>

    Herbert: Somehow I doubt he wanted to dig the subject up.

    -He waves a hand around the exhibit.-

    Herbert: This is it. This is the lineage that I was so proud of, once upon a time. I always used to wonder why I could never learn anything about Lucanus... no matter how hard I searched, I couldn't even find his name, let alone something about who he was. Now, though... I know why. He wanted to be forgotten, so that his descendants wouldn't be remembered like this. And he failed. This whole exhibit makes that last point kind of obvious.

    -He looks over at Revan and sighs before he continues, now seemingly talking to himself rather than the Dusknoir.-

    Herbert: I still don't know what to think about Lucanus. He was responsible for everything this exhibit accuses him of and more, and what he did to Celzvet was worse than simply killing him. But... when you look at what drove him to do all of this, it becomes a lot more understandable. Not excusable, mind, but you can start to see where he snapped. And to be honest? I met Shokaki, and he was a dick. He had his own reasons to hate Lucanus. Reasonable ones, perhaps, but these recordings were made with an agenda. I guess that in the end, you're supposed to decide for yourself. Was he a bad guy to begin with, or just an unlucky individual who couldn't handle the pressure of everything he was asked to deal with?

    Revan: <...What are you going on about?>

    Herbert: ...I don't know, actually. Just venting some thoughts that have been running around my head for a while.

    -He shakes his head.-

    Herbert: It doesn't matter anyway. The point I was trying to make before I got distracted by whatever that was is that you've let your entire life - and unlife - revolve around Lucanus. To the point where you spent six hundred years in a tomb because you'd thought he'd died. Even now you're still just blindly following one of his descendants. Now that you know about all of this... that Lucanus wasn't quite the man you thought he was... perhaps you can finally move on?

    Revan: <...>

    -There would be a moment of silence, if the two were not currently standing in the middle of a crowded museum exhibit.-

    Revan: <...I think... I need some time to think about things...>

    Herbert: Naturally. I won't bother you for any longer, then. Bye for now, Revan.

    -The two go their separate ways, Herbert returning to the streets of Holon City.-
