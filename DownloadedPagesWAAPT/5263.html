    Fortree Store

    <Oh? WhatsEep!> Skywalker, surprised to find himself out of his Pokeball, turns first to Wolf. Upon seeing his former Trainer, however, he takes several steps backwards, towards the wall, and draws in on himself.

    Crewe stands a few feet away from him, biting her lip. She takes a step forward and extends her arm towards him, but he ducks away, wings coming up to half-cover his face. Crewe takes another step, her hand inches from his muzzle. When he doesnt move, she softly lays her palm on his nose. Skywalker makes a soft whimpering sound, but otherwise remains still.

    "Shh Shh Its okay" she murmurs, stroking his muzzle. Slowly, she lays her other hand on the side of his face. She pets him with both hands, wordlessly, moving from his nose to his forehead. She continues along his neck, the Salamence slowly relaxing, his wings retracting ever so slightly.

    By the time Crewe reaches halfway down his neck, shes crying. She stops abruptly and flings her arms around Skywalker, burying her face in his scales. The Salamence, alarmed, makes several soft crooning sounds. He slowly brings his head down over her back and rests his cheek between her shoulder blades. His wings extend again to cover both of them.

    They stand like that for a few moments, until Crewe takes a step back, untangling herself from the Salamence. He looks at her with wide eyes, whimpering softly. She takes his head in her hands again, meeting his gaze, and trying to see straight through the tears welling up once more in her eyes.

    "Oh, Skywalker..." she whispers hoarsely, "Why do I always hurt you?"

    He whimpers again. Crewe leans so their foreheads meet, closing her eyes. Skywalker croons softly, nudging her.

    "Why am I such a terrible Trainer?" she asks, not really expecting an answer. And she doesnt get one. The two stand still for a few more moments, neither speaking or even moving. Once again, Crewe is the one to break contact, taking a step back and letting her hands fall to her sides.

    "Im sorry," she says, this time a little louder. Her voice rasps in her throat, but shes able to keep it from breaking.

    Skywalker takes a step forward and nuzzles her shoulder softly, then loops his neck around her and encircles her in his wings. She hugs him again, taking several deep breaths.

    "You forgive me too easily," she says wryly. "You always have."

    One final time, she steps away from him, but leaves a hand on his muzzle.

    "I hope youre happy with Wolf," she murmurs. "Hes a better Trainer than I can ever hope to be You deserve each other."

    Crewe turns and walks towards the window. Skywalker takes a half-step after her, whimpering. She glances back over her shoulder and smiles at him, then at Wolf.

    "Thank you," she tells him. "For taking them in. And tell them I'm sorry, will you?"
