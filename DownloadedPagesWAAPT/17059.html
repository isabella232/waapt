    Salvador is kneeling in front of a small silver statue of Yveltal. His usual thick robes are missing, and his back looks like it has been torn or shredded - the Delcatty-'o-Nine-Tails laying limp on the floor is the most likely source of the sounds. He places one of his hands on his bleeding back and begins tracing red down the image of the Deity of Death.

    Salvador: May peace be upon those who have died by knives and hate
    May peace be upon those whom have answered you call before their just time
    May Her gentle embrace heal them of their wounds of the flesh
    May they suffer no more in Her eyes
    Blessed be Yveltal, Lady of the Martyrs.
    Holy in Her name are the sinners, the mental and the departed
    Shall they forever find peace in her domain
    Amen
    Standing behind Salvador is the elderly Shaka, who's hands are clasped together.

    Shaka: So the news has reached your ears.

    Salvador: Of course it has.

    Salvador's voice almost seems lifeless

    Shaka: You are troubled. You have experienced murder thrice over in your young life, yet you still seem on edge.

    Salvador: These ones were more personal, you know that...

    Shaka furrows (one of his) brows.

    Shaka: Salvador, I know that's not it.

    Salvador: ...

    Salvador stands up and reaches over for a pot of brine water. Pouring it over his back.

    Salvador: What does this mean for me? The timeline I come from is a mistake, one that - by any means - should not exist. Alexandre's father is dead, as is Lillianna's, evidently I - by extension - should not exist either.

    Shaka: Salvador...

    Salvador drops the clay pot, allowing it to smash into shards all over the bloodstained floor.

    Salvador: ...

    Shaka: ...

    Salvador: ...We head out to Almia as soon as possible. We head to Pueltown and we will find Lex. Bring him back here. He's going to need all the consolation he can get.

    Shaka: I understand young master. But for now you should rest, the blood loss is getting to your head.

    Salvador: I know...

    Salvador flops down on his bed.

    Salvador: I know...

    And drifts off into slumber.
