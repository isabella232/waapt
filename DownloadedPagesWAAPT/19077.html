    Farmland

    Champloo: <We start at the beginning - where all baked goods come from. There are many minor sources, but the big three are thus - grain, rice, and potatoes. Study them - find what looks best. Nature does not lie when it shows you something that looks good aside from flowers but we're not getting into that right now. Remember these qualities, because never a good cake was made with bad ingredients.>

    Fuuma: <So... Which of those three are good for what?>

    Champloo: <An excellent question. Grain is the main staple of western diets - however, do note that cultures unused to the gluten in wheat may have developed sensitivity to it. The earthy taste of grain-made bread, especially rye, can add a bite to savory dishes - like ham and mustard. Wheat is the baseline grain - use it as a starting point. If another type of bread is called for, then do not hesitate to use that instead - however, do not worry if you stick with the tried and true golden wheat.>

    -Champloo picks up a bag of rice.-

    Champloo: <Rice is difficult to turn into what most people consider bread. Rather than grind it and turn it into flour, rice is best used by soaking it in savory or sweet sauces, where the kernels will absorb the flavor and become packable into onigiri or other treats. Just don't try making donuts out of them.>

    -Champloo then holds up a potato.-

    Champloo: <Ah, the potato. The most underused of the carbohydrates in terms of baking. Potato bread is some of the most soft, moist, and healthy alternatives to wheat available. Do note, however, that you must always be watchful of the temperature while baking - they burn far easier than grains and rice. Potato flour is also not nearly as common as wheat flour, and only certain batches of potatoes are made for that use. A sad fact of life, though I will admit that a baked potato is certainly with merit, even if it is diverted from this scrumptious alternative.>

    -Champloo places the potato back in the box it came from, gently patting it for a moment as if it were a baby Lillipup.-

    Champloo: <After we're done here, we can move onto the practical experience!>

    Seishirou: <Then let's get to that then!>

    Champloo: <Hehe, I remember when I was that eager. Next lesson - following instructions!>
