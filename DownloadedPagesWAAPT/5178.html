    Twinleaf Town (In the further future, Sinnoh)

    Not sure if he should bow in return, Rex simply gave a little wave of his gloved hand, smiling at her somewhat shyly. "Erm...my name is Rex...Rex Hunter...from Johto." He shoved his hands into his pockets, ignoring the amused snorts of his Ponyta.

    "I see," she nodded. "Your clothing does have that Johtoan look to it. Mareep wool, isn't it?"

    He looked down at his new clothes, inspecting them thoroughly for the first time. Sure enough, on a little tag on the inside of his jacket, there was an image of a smiling Mareep on top of a pile of wool. "Huh...I guess so."

    "Hmm..." Dawn's gaze switched between the trainer and his Pokemon for a few more precious moments. "In any case, I welcome you to Sinnoh on behalf of Twinleaf Town. I'd love to give you a tour but...!" She blinked, as if suddenly becoming aware of something. "Oh no! It's almost time!" She gave him an apologetic look. "E-excuse me; the professor will be upset if I linger." Without a further explanation, she dashed off into the woods, leaving Rex staring blankly at her retreating backside.

    "...Professor?" he asked, folding his arms curiously. "Could she mean Prof. Rowan? ...Come to think of it, I do recall hearing that his lab is in Sandgem Town."

    <Is that our next destination?> Ponyta asked, coming to his side.

    "Sure. The only other place near here is...ah..." He opened his Pokegear, inspecting a map of Sinnoh carefully. "Lake Verity. And I have no intention of going near the lake of a deity that can suck out my emotions."

    <A wise idea.>

    Route 201, Sinnoh (Future)

    "This is your DJ Ben, bringing you the Pokemon Music Channel! Today, we're going to play a gentle tune, hailing all the way from another region. So, please, sit back and relax as we bring you Sinnoh Sound ."

    Riding on Ponyta wasn't as awkward as he thought it'd be. The equine Pokemon was comfortable enough to let him mount her, and even kept at a reasonable pace in order to prevent him from falling...though she had started to break into a small run once a mischievous Growlithe had tried to outpace her. Better still, he felt no discomfort from her flames, the idea that she trusted him making him smile a little. Now, he just needed to work on making her more comfortable around him and his tea-

    <I'm handsome!>

    Rex blinked, looking down at Ponyta. "Uhh...was that you?"

    The Ponyta snorted again and shook her head. <Try again.> She pointed ahead of them with a hoof, the boy looking in that direction and staring in surprise. "Shahinne?"

    Near a small crowd of Nidoran♀, a little Starly bounced around excitedly, chirping and flapping its wings excitedly as it continued to chirp. <Do you like nuts?> The Nidos simply stared at it in confusion, one of them scratching gently at it to shoo it away. It cooed and fluttered away from them, landing a bit closer to Rex, though its gaze was still on the Nidoran.

    "Oh...it's a male Starly."

    <Remind you of someone you know?>

    "You might say that." Rex watched the Starly for a few more moments, reaching for his belt absently. "Hmm..." He gently gripped a Pokeball, observing the tiny bird for a few moments. "I wonder if I should..."

    Suddenly, the bird turned to him, soaring over to land on his head. Rex blinked at the sudden movement, wincing a little as the bird pecked him a few times on the head. "Ow..."

    <Silly trainer!> the Starly cooed happily, leaping from his head and fluttering off in the direction of Lake Verity. The Nidoran, meanwhile, wandered off into the bushes, unsure what to make of the whole encounter.

    <Odd thing,> Ponyta murmured, shaking her head.

    "..." Shrugging, Rex let go of the Pokeball in his hand, turning back to Ponyta. "Well, then...shall we proceed?" Soon enough, they had resumed their pace, heading further in the direction of Sandgem Town.

    Sandgem Town, Sinnoh (Future)

    "Another small town," Rex noted as Ponyta's steps began to slow, the two looking about the place curiously. A few houses were scattered about here and there, as well as a Poke Mart. Of most immediate concern was the Pokemon Center, where Rex would be visiting next, and a large building with a blue rooftop sitting next to it. "This building must be the Professor's Laboratory."

    Heading over to it, Rex gave a gentle knock, the door opening as a familiar blue-haired girl stepped into view. "Greetings, and welcome to Prof- ... ... ...oh, hello, Rex! I didn't know you were on the way here too."

    "Rex?" asked a deep, powerful voice from inside. "The protege of Elm's?"

    "Well, he did say he was from Johto," Dawn replied. "Hmm...come in." She stepped back, making room for the boy to enter. Deciding it'd be counterproductive to have a Pokemon out, Rex recalled Ponyta and stepped inside, noting the warmth of the building as he entered.

    Standing nearby, grasping a handful of notes, was Prof. Rowan, the man nodding politely to Rex. He couldn't tell due to the stoic look on the man's face, but he thought he was smiling. "Greetings. It's good to meet you in person. I see you've improved in health since the Orange Islands."

    "Haha...yes sir..."

    Dawn, sensing that the two had some things to talk about, politely excused herself, heading off into the back. Rex's gaze drifted in her direction questioningly, prompting Rowan to speak up. "She's an apprentice researcher, much like you."

    "I see..." He hadn't expected someone with such a regal appearance to be doing things like research and field work. He supposed it was true about judging books by their covers.

    "On the topic of research work, I hear that you've been supplying Prof. Elm much data with all of your recent captures, though he's stated that your own personal notes have been somewhat...brief, lately."

    Rex looked a bit sheepish at that, flushing in embarrassment. "Haha...well, there were some...setbacks. B-but that's all taken care of now."

    Rowan raised a brow, but said no more. "Very well. Might I inquire as to why you've come to my laboratory?"

    "I was simply making a visit, sir," Rex admitted, unconsciously shifting into a more polite manner of speaking. Something about the man simply commanded respect. "I thought that I should introduce myself to the professor of the region and a respected colleague of Professors Oak and Elm."

    "I see." He mulled that over before setting down the papers into his hand, reaching for a notepad from his pocket and scribbling something down. "Well, since you've stopped by, I suppose it would do no harm to offer you my personal number. In the event that you need my assistance, simply feel free to call."

    Rex got Rowan's Number!

    "Thank you, sir. I appreciate your help."

    "It is no trouble." The man nodded, stepping back over to his desk. In mid-step, he paused, recalling something as he turned back to the youth. "Oh, and Rex?"

    "Yes, sir?"

    "I understand that you've gotten involved with that group of Trainers...the J-Team, was it?"

    "...yes, sir?"

    Rowan's brows furrowed, his eyes piercing Rex's soul with their stern, no-nonsense look. "I trust you will be respectful of Sinnoh and your surroundings on this trip?"

    Rex felt his spine turn to jelly under the powerful stare, feeling not only a strong compulsion to agree to the Professor's words, but also to complete the regional Pokedex for Sinnoh.

    "Y-yes, sir..."

    "... ... ... ... ...Good." Rowan nodded satisfied, turning back to his desk. "That is all."

    Needing no further excuse, Rex dashed out of the lab, wanting to put as much space between him and    the stare    as possible.
