    Forest

    Rattata: ...you said you saw a Wolf.

    Joseph: I did. It was really weird. He always seemed to speak in cryptic bullshit and had a huge crush on me, or something.

    -the Rattata stares, aghast-

    Rattata: ...this is bad. This is very very very very very very bad!

    Joseph: Calm down, Rattata. It's not like this is the most provocative Red Riding Hood thing I could be wearing right now.

    Wolf: Technically correct...

    -Joseph turns and flails-

    OHARCEUSTAPDANCINGCHRISTWHEREDYOUCOMEFROM?!

    -the Wolf seems unconcerned, and slinks out of the bushes. He purrs, in a voice that sounds of velvet and chocolate:-

    Wolf: Presentation and attitude are everything. And I happen to like yours quite a lot, Red.

    -as he leaves the bushes fully, he stands at full height, and looks down at Joseph hungrily. The coat feels a lot smaller than it seemed at first-

    -Rattata pulls at the Inverness cape frantically-

    Rattata: Don't talk to him! You're already in the story, but it can't end if you're not charmed by him!

    -he tries to whisper so the Wolf can't hear. Pointless-

    Wolf: Oh...you've found yourself a new Pokemon companion?

    Joseph: ...what do you mean by new? I have my old ones, they're just not on me.

    Wolf: I was talking about the other one, Red.

    Joseph: ...Munn? He's not a Pokemon...

    -the Wolf raises his eyebrows-

    He seems to follow you like one. What a shame...there's so much more I can offer you...

    -Joseph tries to not be charmed by the soothing voice, or the soft, catlike movements. He succeeds-

    I'd rather him to you! He's more trustworthy, at least.

    -the Wolf chuckles, very amused-

    Wolf: Is he, now? But that's besides the point. The point is, you're right. He has your best interests at heart, and mine are, of course, selfish. He sees things like that, in black and white. He likes to claim otherwise, but he does. You're much the same way, you know. You think that what you're going to do is the right decision, because it's worked so far.

    -he circles around Joseph, brushing his tail very lightly against Joseph's hip. Joseph shudders, involuntarily-

    Wolf: Do you know, Red, that there have been over a thousand years of your tale, and I've never met one quite like you? Things are working differently, now. You're not visiting your grandmother's house, and I'm not trying to get there ahead of you. I just want you to make a choice. You either follow what your Rattata wants you to be, or what your boy wants you to be...or you'll be who you want to be. It's entirely your decision, and I mean that.

    -Joseph nods. He can tell that the Wolf does-

    Wolf: Do you see where the path curves ahead of us, Red?

    Joseph: That's not a curve, it's a fork. The other one's hard to see.

    -the Wolf chuckles, delighted-

    Clever morsel. Most people only see one path. The one with sunshine, and the ones with flowers and cute little Pokemon to talk to. It's the one that most Red Riding Hoods would love to take, and I'm sure that includes you. But...there's another one that leads you to the shadows, and while the sunny path leads to fun, the shadowed path leads to satisfaction. Do you know what I mean?

    -Joseph glares, but nods again-

    Wolf: I'm taking the shadow path. I hope I see you there. I'd so enjoy walking that path with you by my side, rather than waiting at the end like a Mareep only good for food.

    -the Wolf lopes off. Joseph stands in silence, and anger, and fear, for a while-
    A tooth for an eye!
