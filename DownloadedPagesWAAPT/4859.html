    121459 Crewe19th Dec 2011 06:41:33 PM from Gravity Falls, Oregon Get RP Mod
    Li'l Punk

    Somewhere in the wilderness of not-Hoenn

    After a day of traveling in what is fortunately not a blizzard, Morana taps Biton on the shoulder, Martin coming up behind her.

    <Martin and I want to head back to base camp. We need to eat, and theres nothing to hunt around here.>

    Biton glances back at his siblings, then looks ahead, then sighs.

    <All right. Are you heading into town?>

    The female Sneasel nods.

    <Ill meet you guys at camp. See you later!> With a wave, she turns and runs, soon disappearing from view. Biton turns to Martin.

    <Lets head back, then,> he says, turning around as well. The smaller male tags along behind as the two start walking, looking around and occasionally picking up a handful of snow and holding it up to the sunlight.

    After a while of walking, the two arrive at a small cave in the side of a bank. Its tucked back behind several trees, with hanging moss and the branches of a willow growing above it covering half the entrance. The two push inside, sniffing for signs of Morana or unwelcome visitors. Finding neither, they settle down.

    Biton drops onto one of three nests of sticks and moss, leaning against the wall. Martin walks into a corner and shoves a rock out of the way, revealing a shelf covered in items that have no place in a cave in the middle of nowhere: a few T Ms, a pair of sunglasses, a beat-up old Pokeball (which is carefully placed in an indentation towards the back of the shelf), some Potions, half of a broken green glass bottle, and a poffin. Martin paws through the supplies (keeping well away from the Pokeball), then turns back to his brother.

    <Nothings missing,> he announces, then picks up the bottle and sits down in his own nest, turning it over in his paws. Biton grunts his acknowledgement, all but falling asleep.

    After a short while, the two Sneasel are startled alert by the thud of someone knocking a rock against the wall of the bank outside. They prick up their ears and listen carefully, as the knocker repeats a pattern three times: Thud. Thudthudthud. Thud. Thud.

    <Its Morana!> Martin says, hopping up and placing the bottle back on the shelf.

    Sure enough, the female Sneasel pushes her way through the curtain covering the entrance and drops a small leather satchel on the ground, grinning.

    <I found some great stuff. First, some lunch.> She pulls out two poffins and Oran berries and tosses them to her brothers, then settles down with food of her own. The three eat in silence for a while, then Martin comes over and stares at the bag curiously.

    <Cmon Morana, whatve you got?> he asks, reaching out towards it. She swats his paw away, drawing the bag towards her.

    <Hey! I stole this stuff, I get to show you whats in it!> she says, giving him a sharp look. <Now, first>

    She pulls out a small golden coin hanging from a thin chain. Biton crawls closer and he and Martin ooh and aah over it for a few moments, before Martin takes it and holds it up to the sunlight. Morana snatches it back and lays it aside.

    <Wait until Im done, then play with them,> she snaps, swatting his head. Martin playing swings back, but she ducks and shoves him over.

    <Okay, next!> She pulls out a small bell attached to a bright red ribbon. Shaking her paw slightly, she sets it ringing, filling the small cave with a soft chiming. The three visibly relax, smiling. She stops ringing it and sets it down, causing Biton to blink and Martin to eye it curiously.

    <Just a few more things> Morana says, rummaging through the bag. She pulls out an oddly shaped, dark stone with an indigo glow in the center. <I dunno what this does, but its cool.>

    Her brothers nod in agreement. Martin reaches out to touch it, but she swats at him again and sets it down, then pulls out a small compass.

    <I dunno what this is for either, but I saw a human staring at it and looking at a map,> she says, also setting it down. This causes Bitons ears to prick up, and he looks down at the compass, while Martin goes back to eyeing the stone and coin.

    <Just two more besides the bag,> Morana says. <This one we all know.>

    She holds up a TM. <Im pretty sure Sneasel can learn it, too,> she says, looking proud of herself.

    <Is it one of the special ones?> Martin asks, tilting his head.

    <You mean the unbreakable ones? I dunno, I didnt see the Trainer use it.> Morana shrugs, dropping the TM. <Okay, last thing. This one is really cool.>

    She reaches into the bag once more, and pulls out an apple core?

    <Um>

    <Morana, are you—>

    <Quiet, I havent explained yet!> she snaps, glaring at them. <Anyway, Id already snagged the food, but I was hungry and wanted something to eat before I got here. So I pulled this out of a trainers bag. But when I started eating>

    She takes a bite, and Martins and Bitons expressions change from dubious to incredulous as she chews part of the apple, a part that apparently had never been bitten off, going by the appearance of the core in their sisters hand. Morana smirks, swallowing.

    <Yeah. Its real. This thing never disappears, no matter how much you eat. Its awesome. Try it!> She passes the core to her brothers, and both take a bite that doesnt seem to take away from the core.

    <Take care of it,> she says sternly. Martin nods, and starts gathering up the items to place on the shelf. He then takes the coin and stone and sits on his nest, examining them. Morana tosses the satchel to Biton.

    <Here. I know how hard it is to maintain Hammerspace; just fitting all that stuff in this thing was exhausting. Keep them in here.>

    With that, she goes over to her own nest, curling up.

    Biton holds up the bag, then goes over to his nest. Reaching behind his feather, he pulls out of nowhere a piece of eggshell, and gently places it in the bag. He repeats this, pulling piece after piece out of nowhere, until theres none left. Then, smiling and (very carefully) hugging the bag to himself, he curls up and falls asleep.

    The day passes lazily, with the three Sneasel lounging around and resting, recovering energy from long days of traveling in ever outward reaching spirals. As the sun begins to set, Martin and Morana venture out of the cave to look for berries and maybe the odd bird Pokemon. After a short while, Martin returns to the cave to tidy up the shelf, while Biton carefully hangs the satchel around himself and walks outside

     To be hit square in the face with a snowball by a grinning Morana. Yelping, he stumbles back, and is hit by another.

    <Cmon! You can do better than that!> Morana taunts, readying another.

    Letting out a playful growl, Biton charges her, scooping up a snowball of his own and throwing it. The two Sneasel scurry back and forth, lobbing snow at each and laughing. A few bird Pokemon emerge from their nests and sing as the sun sinks. Martin appears in the entrance to the cave, watching his siblings play and holding up the coin and watching as it catches the last rays of the setting sun.

    Victory! Honor! Destiny! Mutton!
