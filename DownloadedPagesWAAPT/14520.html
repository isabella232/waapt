    Humilau City

    -After several weeks spent relaxing in Unova, Herbert is sitting on the beach, looking out over the ocean and occasionally sifting handfuls of sand through his fingers. Sarah walks up and sits beside him.-

    Sarah: Something on your mind?

    Herbert: Well... Virbank aside, this is probably the one place in Unova that holds the most memories for me. Not all of them good ones, though...

    -Sarah thinks back to their original visit.-

    Sarah: ...You're talking about Edge, right?

    -Herbert gives a small sigh.-

    Herbert: Yeah. He's probably still buried around here somewhere... sure, he came back as a ghost and all, but I don't think I'm ever going to forget my first kill.

    -Sarah sighs and looks down at the sand.-

    Sarah: You never can... It's not a fun thing to remember, but it's burned into your mind no matter what. Burned into our minds...

    -Herbert looks back up at Sarah and manages a small smile.-

    Herbert: ...It's not all bad, though. I wouldn't have come here if it was. Me and Carol had a long talk here after the fight with Edge, and I... well, I almost got around to telling her some things that I probably should've told her a long time ago.

    Sarah: Well, even if you didn't... it still turned out okay, didn't it? I mean, now you've got Samantha.

    Herbert: I guess so... I probably should've let her know where I was going before running off to Unova for several weeks, though. I really haven't been a great boyfriend... but she doesn't seem to mind, for whatever reason.

    Sarah: ...

    -She hesitates-

    Sarah: ...We should probably get back, then. As much fun as this was, we still have things to do there. And I can't drag you away from your girlfriend forever.

    Herbert: Hey, I've dragged you away from your girlfriend too. We're both as guilty as each other. I'm just glad that Samantha's not the type to get the wrong id- no, scratch that, she's totally the type to get the wrong idea.

    -He looks back out over the waterfront.-

    Herbert: Still, you're right. I can't just run away from my problems and expect everything to have sorted itself out once I get back... I burnt a lot of bridges before leaving Kalos. And even if I can't rebuild all of them, at least I can make sure I have my affairs in order before I make myself scarce.

    Sarah: We'll have to do our best to repair what few bridges we can, then. And I'll help you do that even if nobody else will.

    -Herbert gives a wide smile.-

    Herbert: ...Caroline's a lucky girl, you know.

    -Sarah can't help but smirk-

    Sarah: You say that as if I wouldn't do the same for anyone.

    Herbert: Exactly. You're too nice for your own good. Arceus knows that it must take a lot of patience to put up with people like me.

    Sarah: Oh, you're not that hard to deal with. You just have a bad tendency of getting into situations with other hard-to-deal-with people.

    Herbert: Case in point. Too nice.

    -He stretches his arms for a moment, then stands up.-

    Herbert: So, uh... is that Beheeyem of yours still hanging around? Only I kind of left my entire team back in Kalos. Maul included.

    ~blip~

    Brecht: <Does this answer your question?>

    Sarah: Yes, it does. To Lumiose?

    Herbert: ...To Lumiose, I suppose.

    Lumiose City — Herbert's Room

    -Brecht blips the two back here. Bishop is sitting on the bed with his arms folded, looking virtually unfazed by the sudden appearance of several teleporting interlopers.-

    Bishop: <...You're back. I admit that I was beginning to wonder if you were ever going to return.>

    Herbert: The thought of just staying in Unova crossed my mind, yeah... but I've got unfinished business here. Plus, there's no way I could just leave you guys behind.

    -He turns to Sarah.-

    Herbert: So, uh... I'm going to get going now. It's been a long time since I was last in Lumiose, I should probably try and get my bearings again.

    -He heads out with Bishop following behind.-

    Lumiose Cafe

    A certain ex-Cipher admin was just calmly sitting on a table inside one of the cafes. He had some books on the table and was taking some notes as he drank some coffee. He was wearing his normal outfit and had a bag near him.

    "Well, the stay at Team Flare has been unproductive to say the least, at least I managed to get some files regarding that suit... I think it's name was Essentia. That seems like an interesting toy." he thought as he kept taking notes.

    He then noticed that there was someone near him, a rather small someone in fact. She was just looking at what he was doing with curiosity, but once he turned around to look at her, she just waved. Before writing a message.

    "Hi! Are you a writer? It's not often that I see someone doing stuff like this here."

    Allen just raised a curious eyebrow at the girl, who just started writing again

    "My name is Aline and I'm mute. Nice to meet you!" she wrote, with a smile on her face

    "... Oh." said Allen, looking around for anyone who might look like Aline's parents and finding no-one "... I suppose you could say that I am a writer in a way."

    Aline then sits on the other seat and starts writing again "Nice~ Can I read it?"

    "No."

    Aline just made a disappointed expression while Allen pretty much pretended she wasn't there and resumed what he was doing. He did notice that the girl seemed to be extremely bored, likely why she decided to even approach him.

    "... Shouldn't you be with your parents?" he asked, without even looking directly at her

    "Mama and Papa let me go around Lumiose on my own, I know the city as well as the back of my hand!"

    "You look a bit young to be wandering a big city alone, you could easily get kidnapped this way."

    "I have my Pokémon to take care of me, I'll be fine!"

    There's just silence from then on as Allen keeps doing his own thing, eventually Aline gets up and goes to ask for a soda. However, as she goes back to the table she trips and the soda goes flying, landing on the table and drenching all of Allen's files in the process. He just sits there, staring at the disaster.

    He facepalms "I suppose this could be considered a very small display of karmic retribution." he thought, sighing.

    Aline gets up immediatelly and smiles awkwardly at Allen before getting something to clean the table. She then sits again while looking very sheepish while Allen just examined just how badly she had drenched his files.
