    FUAM, Sear Dorm

    Munn is hanging out in the lounge with Harry.

    "So, tell me, how does this VR thing work?"

    Harry: "So, interested after all, huh? Alright, let me see..."

    He leans back in his chair.

    Harry: "The sport has to do with, as you might guess, virtual reality simulations. The machine involved is hooked up to you, interprets your brainwaves, gives you feedback, etc. Inside the simulation, you compete with the other team in a scenario, which can vary greatly. Each player can take in one of their Pokemon with them. As the name suggests, there is quite a bit of fighting - and not just between Pokemon. Luckily, the system has an insane amount of safety precautions, including the fact that in it, you feel pain analytically, as opposed to physically, so you can't die of sheer shock."

    "It's a team sport, right?"

    Harry: "Right. A team has no upward limit on number of players, but must have a minimum of six. The number of players you can send in is equal to the size of the smaller team. For example, in a game between a team of seven and a team of ten, both sides send in seven players. As long as you can find six members of a minimum skill level and a faculty sponsor, you can form your own team."

    "Huh. So, then, what are the teams like?"

    Harry: "There are five major teams of note. First off, the three house teams, Sears, Pours, and Sages. All three hold their own quite well. Then there's the other two of the Big Five. There's the Black Hats."

    "Ooh, sounds nasty."

    Harry: "Yeah, they are. They push the rules to their limits, using as many nasty and underhanded tricks they can get away with. Real pleasant people. And finally, the Powers. All of them, to one extent or another, have the ability to use the four powers. They're all either Psychics, Aurics, or Obscurics."

    Munn whistles.

    "Tough crowd."

    Harry: "Yeah. The simulation also takes that into account, giving them as much control over their powers as they do in reality."

    Harry sighs.

    "What's up?"

    Harry: "Well, we have a scrimmage against the Sages coming up, but Valdez, our resident hothead, is out with a broken arm. We only have six with him, so now we no longer qualify. Plus, there's no one else in our house who meets the minimum skill requirement."

    "Huh... how do they determine your skill?"

    Harry: "There's a little test they have you take. Why?"

    "Cuz' I'm interested. I think I may just take this... test."

    And there you are, Uil! How it works, plus set up for something fun once I get back!
