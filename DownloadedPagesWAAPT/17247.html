    -after Thanksgiving, Honore is laying on the couch, staring at the ceiling. Alys sits on the chair, across from her-

    Alys: How have you been, then?

    Honore: I've been perfectly well, thank you.

    -it's shocking how much venom could be placed in one syllable. Alys is unperturbed-

    Alys: I see you've stopped dying your hair, Honore.

    Honore: ...I figured that I didn't need hair dye to look beautiful. I figured that I should embrace my middle age.

    Alys: I see! An admirable position to take...

    -she exhales smoke-

    Alys: That is, if you believed a word of it.

    -Honore opens her mouth, but Alys interrupts-

    Alys: In all of my twenty-eight years, Honore, have you ever once lied to me and got away with it? Can you name one time?

    Honore: Yes. That one time with Gerard-

    Alys: -when you said at dinner that you were over the moon with love, and that you planned to marry him one day? Yes, and you also lied to me the next day, when you said that Michel just drove you back from the party because Gerard's car broke down. Then you lied, right after that, when you said that you needed to go to the bathroom to brush your hair.

    -she grins-

    I saw the Listerine on the counter, Honore. You spilled a bit.

    -Honore blanches-

    Alys: It is true that people do age gracefully, and that people can be very secure in older age. You are not one of them. You desperately cling to beauty you lost a decade ago, holding the image of the rich socialite when all you really want to do is go back to the days when all anyone expected from you was your parent's money and your mouth.

    -Honore practically snarls. Alys chuckles-

    Alys: Now, now. You're thinking you're a good mother, yes? You've done good things for your children, yes? I suppose you have, but they still don't tell you things, do they?

    Honore: They-

    Alys: Consider little Mimi. You bragged about her IQ score the last I saw you. Said it was higher than what mine was at her age. And yet, thanks to you, she's wasting her time being a coordinator rather than doing something meaningful.

    Honore: Shut-

    Alys: And Noelle? Do you know what she wrote to me one day, Honore? She wrote to me, saying that she was a lesbian. She told me, and not you, Honore. Think about that.

    -Honore is silent for a good minute before hissing-

    Honore: ...you talked to her, didn't you?

    Alys: Honore, dear, I have no agenda. I had no reason to. She came to me on her own. Don't bother doing your little X-symbol, sister. There's nothing you can change.

    -she exhales smoke again, exiting the house. Honore simply looks up at the ceiling, miserably-
