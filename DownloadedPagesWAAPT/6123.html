    153057 Luke92419th Apr 2012 05:00:37 PM Get RP Mod
    After taking a few particualry nasty hits from Lake, Crowley had adopted the battle strategy of staying in the air where Lake couldn't get to him, the Murkrow now cawing tauntingly at the ground-bound Crcoconaw as he tried to shoot him down with hos Water Gun attack, but without much success.

    Lake: <COME DOWN AND FIGHT, YOU COWARD!>

    The Murkrow smirks tauntingly, flapping out of the way of another stream of water fired up at him.

    Crowley: <I don't need to fight you! We both know I could win against you any day of the week!>

    Lake: <THAT'S IT, SHUT UP!>

    The Croconaw jumps up, firing another Water Gun at the Murkrow. With less time to react, Crowley can't move fast enough to avoid being soaked by the torrent of water, knocking the bird to the ground.

    Before the Murkrow can take flight again, he is suddenly pinned to the ground by Lake's foot, the Croconaw snarling at him viscously as he does so.

    It was at that point that Crowley began to lose his cool.

    Crowley: <Hey, come on! Let's not get too hasty here...>

    Glaring at the bird, Lake lowers his head stopping it inches away from the end of Crowley's beak.

    Lake: <GIVE ME ONE GOD REASON WHY I SHOULDN'T EAT YOU.>

    Crowley: <...Um...>

    "LAKE!"

    Both Pokemon stop, glancing over at their trainer as he arrives along with treads, the human looking rather angry to see the two at each other's throats, almost literally.

    "What on earth are you doing?! Let Crowley go!"

    Lake: <But he->

    "I don't care what he did, you let him go! NOW."

    Luke shoots Lake a harsh glare, informing the Croconaw that there would be consequence if he didn't let the Murkrow go. Although intimidated, he hesitates for a few seconds, then steps off the Murkrow, Crowley quickly flapping up and perching on a high windowsill out of reach.

    Knowing that he wouldn't be able to reach him for the time being, Luke dismisses the bird for now, then walks over to the Croconaw, a cross look on his face.

    "Would you like to explain to me why you were attacking Crowley?"

    Lake glances down at floor for a split-second, before glancing back up at his trainer, a hurt look on his face.

    Lake: <He...he was saying awful things about me!>

    "...Awful things? What sort of awful things?"

    Lake: <He said that I was too soppy and that I wasn't capable of leading the team and that he was going to replace me, and...and...>

    He stops, shutting his eyes tight to try and avoid crying, but it doesn't work. With a howl, Lake buries his head in his paws, sniffing loudly as totally-not-crocodile tears streak from his eyes.

    <THEY HURT, LUKE! SO I WANTED...I wanted to hurt him too...>

    It's at that point he breaks down completely, dropping to all fours as he cries loudly. Saying nothing, Luke glances up at Crowley, who looks away awkwardly, trying to avoid eye-contact. Rightly assuming he was trying to hide something, Luke pulled out the Murkrow's Pokéball and recalled him, before glancing down at Treads, who seemed rather sad to see Lake crying.

    "...Is Lake telling the truth, Treads?"

    The Phanpy nods, knowing that Luke was only able to understand what Lake said. Because of this, Luke occasionally had to get his other Pokémon to verify whether he was telling the truth or not. That didn't happen often, though, and this was no exception.

    "...Okay..."

    The human walks over to the Croconaw, bends down...then gives him a hug, trying to make him feel better. Appreciating the affection, Lake hugs back, burying his head in his shoulder.

    Lake: <It's not fair...why are you blaming me? It was his fault...>

    "Well, you're right. Crowley had no excuse to say those things to you. But...just because he was didn't give you the excuse to attack him."

    Lake: <But...he was saying awful things about me...>

    "I know, I know. It sucks when peoplemons are mean to you for no reason...but attacking them back never helps. It only makes you as bad as them."

    Crying a bit less now, Lake raises his head to look his trainer in the eye, his face still wet with tears.

    Lake: <...Then what am I supposed to do?>

    Luke pauses to pull a handkerchief out of his bag offering it to Lake. The Croconaw accepts it, using it to wipe the tears from his face.

    "Well, the best thing you can do is to simply ignore them. Fighting is never the answer."

    Lake: <But what he said about me wasn't true...>

    "Then prove them wrong and be the bigger person...um, Pokémon, and walk away. Trying to get back at them will only make things worse, won't it?"

    "...I guess..."

    Luke smiles, then pats Lake gently on the head.

    "There you go. If he's wrong, he's wrong. You don't need to prove that to him because somebody else eventually will. Besides, there are just some fights that you just can't win, no matter how hard you try."

    Lake: <I...I guess so...>

    Lake sniffs, giving Luke a sad look.

    <...I'm sorry...>

    "It's okay. Just promise me you'll apologize to Crowley once we've got him treated at the Pokémon Center?"

    Lake stops, seemingly hesistant.

    "He'll have to apologize to you too, if that helps."

    Lake: <...Okay, I will.>

    "...Good boy."

    Luke gives the Croconaw another smile, then stands up.

    "Okay, let's go and get Crowley treated. Thankfully, it doesn't seem you beat him up too badly..."

    He turns then walks away, Treads beginning to follow him closely, with Lake quickly catching up.

    Lake: <Only because I couldn't get to him in the air...>

    "Oh?"

    Lake: <Oh! Not that I really would have eaten him! I was just really mad...>

    "...I know."

    Somewhere in the Orange Islands - Present Day

    Lake: <Zzz...Huh? Oh!>

    The Feraligatr wakes up with a start, having just had a dream where one of his moments from when he was a Croconaw that he wasn't very proud of was replayed in rather vivid detail.

    So...it was more like a Nightmare. Apart from the end. That wasn't so bad.

    And...he did learn an important lesson that day. Sure, Crowley had continued to be an outright jerk towards him a few more times after that, and while he had gotten riled up once or twice, he eventually took Luke's advice to heart and started ignoring him. Although, he couldn't help but think his evolution into a Feraligatr, and the maturity he had developed from it, had helped with that.

    Hmm...perhaps it wasn't so bad to be reminded, then.

    Lake pulls himself up, making a mental note to say thanks to Luke the next time he met him, then heads off to see if he can find Bozaka, and perhaps something to eat for breakfast, too.

    Just as long as it wasn't a Murkrow.
