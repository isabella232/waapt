    And Now...Story Hour with Alys Boucher

    Alys sits in a big, cushy blue armchair. Her legs are crossed elegantly, and she sips a steaming hot cup of tea with perfect posture. She smiles.

    Alys: Well, hello there! I didn't see you come in.

    Her voice, in contrast to her usual syrupy menace, is gentle and kindly. Of course, considering that it's still a seven-feet-in-heels spider woman, the voice doesn't help much.

    Alys: You're just in time for one of my stories. You see, I've recently started writing little fairy tales and morality plays for myself, like the stories I used to read as a little girl. And it would be nice to share them with you. Would you like that?

    Silence.

    Alys: You would, good! Today, I shall tell the story of...

    The Tale of Cruel Kirsten

    -a camera pans in to a picturesque village that could have come from any point in the past few centuries-

    Alys: Once upon a time, there was a young girl named Kirsten that lived in a village full of good little boys and girls.

    -a young Joseph sits cross-legged on the ground, humming and drawing on the ground in chalk-

    Alys: Kirsten wasn't a very good girl, though. She was a mean, nasty little girl who liked nothing more than to cause as much pain and suffering as she could.

    -a young Kirsten, with both eyes, dashes over to Joseph and scuffs her heavy boots all over his drawing-

    Joseph: Ah! My drawing has been spoilt! Why must you torment me so, Kirsten?

    Kirsten: Hahahaha! You are as naive as you are poor and effeminate! If you cower at the sight of me so, you should seek your solace in the arms of your friend Munn, eh?

    -her hand darts out to grab his chalk and snaps it in two, before running away laughing. Joseph cries and runs off-

    Alys: She didn't just do that, of course. She was also quite a good violinist, and when she was at home she practiced as much as she could. Of course, this led to some arguments with her parents.

    -Kirsten sits on her bed, practicing scales. There is a knock-

    Mother: Kirsten! Dinner is served!

    Kirsten: Oh, soak your head in the well, mother! My practicing is more important than eating your shameful slop!

    -she goes back to practicing with a huff-

    Alys: But one target for her cruelties was a dog named Juliet.

    -Juliet is on her hands and knees, wearing a pair of dog ears along with a plain brown dress. She appears to be very confused, but doesn't say anything-

    Alys: Every day, Kirsten would run up to Juliet and give her a big, strong kick to the side, like this.

    -Kirsten runs up and punts Juliet in the stomach, causing her to yelp and roll over-

    Kirsten: Ha! Idiot mutt, you should avoid that after every day I do it!

    Alys: After a while, of course, Juliet did remember. And one day, after Kirsten had finished her scales and was ready to thrash the dog, she decided to give her punishment.

    Kirsten: Well, hullo, stupid creature! Are you not scared of me yet?

    -she comes up ready to kick...before Juliet snarls and pounces-

    Kirsten: AAACK!

    Alys: As punishment for the years of cruel treatment Kirsten gave to Juliet and the other kids, Juliet took each delicate, gentle hand of Kirsten's and bit them clean off!

    -Kirsten screeches in pain, staring down at her now-useless arms-

    Alys: Kirsten never troubled Juliet or the other children again, only staying in her bed lamenting her situation.

    -Kirsten looks out the window, distraught-

    Kirsten: Oh, woe! That cursed mutt has deprived me of the tools that allowed me to play my beautiful music! Now, I am useless, and cannot possibly face the world!

    Alys: She was fed only foul-tasting mush by her parents as punishment for the trouble she caused, and saved the choice meals for themselves...and Juliet, who scavenged the trash.

    -Juliet chews on a ham hock, satisfied.

    Alys: And so, the moral of the story is to be kind to all, or your hands will be bitten off and you will waste away in your room for the rest of your life. Good night!

    A tooth for an eye!
