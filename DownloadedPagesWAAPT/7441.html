    A Bad Egg

    Sandelford Warren

    It had been a week since the Sandelfords escaped the wretched clutches of Woundwort and his Efrafan croonies and crossed the trechorus passes of Mt Moon and really, the inhabitants counldn't be happier.

    After clearing Efrafa, the journey became less trechorus for the hlessis. After reaching the other side of Mt. Moon, Skree turned to them, thanked them for their care and flew off without another word. Some unspoken deal between Skree and the nidos happened that day, and they knew that when winter settles in, he'll be by their side again.

    The way back to Sandelford was relitivley safe, at least in compasrision to Efrafa. Travelling through Cerulean at night, and avoiding the trainer who set up camp at the base of the hill, the posse made it back to their home with all 11 splinters from Efrafa. There was some mating amoungst the Nidos, but the main effort was making the does feel comfortable with their surroundings.

    It took around 5 days for the does and Blackavar to fully assimilate with Sandelfordian culture, but the peace and freedom they had in this warren paid off, and a majority of them started showing signs of improved mental, social and pyshical health.

    One morning, Hazel-rah was talking to Nildro-hain at siflay when suddenly, a Rattata approached the two. Surprising Nildro-hain.

    Nildro-hain: <....Hazel-rah? What is this Rattata doing here?>

    Ratatta: <'scuse me, bu' I 'ave ev'ry right to be 'ere as you do missy.>

    Hazel-Rah: <He's an old friend Nildro-hain. We befriended him when we first arrived here.>

    Nildro-hain was rather confused by this. Yet, the more she thought about it, the more it made sense. After all, they did befriend a Fearow to help them escape from Efrafa, so why not a Rattata here?

    Hazel-Rah: <What is the matter friend?>

    Ratatta: <Aye, news travels 'round swiftly 'ere. Second 'and gossip from th' Spearows say tha' some group o' vicious nidos be heddin' here quick. They said the moster leadin' 'em be redder than a raer Gyarados he'd be.>

    Hazel and Nildro-hain's eyes widened. While they were expecting the Generel to persue them, they were hoping that the General would give up and let them go. They never thought he would persue them all the way to their home.

    Hazel-rah: <Nildro-hain, round up the others and gather them in the Honeycomb* immediatly. We hold on until we have claw our way out.

    Nildro-hain: <Yes Hazel-rah> -bounds off-

    Hazel-rah: -Turning back to the Ratatta- <Thank you

    Rattata: <Aye, friends 'elp friends. You save ma life, I 'elp save yours.> -Scampers off-

    Hazel-rah watches his acquantaince scamper off, before bounding away to discuss matters with his warren.
