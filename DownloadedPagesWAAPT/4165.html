    On the third and final island near Shamouti...

    The girl and her team trudge up a mountain, nearly halfway there. Well, most of them trudge; the girl strides ahead as excited and energetic as ever, and Ariel keeps pace with her, occasionally even moving ahead to scout for obstacles blocking their way. This, along with the long days on nearly continuous hiking interspersed with a few short ferry rides, and worrying about their Trainer, has the rest of the team feel rather irritated.

    <Can't wait till this is finally over and we can go back to hunting non-mountain dwelling Legendaries.> Nathaniel is the first to begin muttering, but Campion and Espen are quick to join in.

    <I know we've hiked before, but this is excessive...>

    <Even I'm tired. I can't believe Ariel's still going, I did just as much work as he did and I couldn't push around another boulder if my life depended on it. Well, maybe my life, but not much else.>

    Ezekiel tries to placate them. <Well, at least when we reach the top we can rest. Then we can go home. Or somewhere else. Anyone know our next plans?>

    They all shake their heads.

    <What if we don't find Moltres, though?> Nathaniel asks, glancing up quickly at the mountain peak. <She'll be crushed! She's so set on finding these Birds, I've never seen her like this! Arceus help her if her hopes are dashed, again.>

    <Arceus help us, too,> Campion murmurs. <Chances are, she'll go into a funk, or she'll flip. And if she flips, we're stuck in the crossfire.>

    Lockspur finally decides to step in. <Stop complaining, guys! Sure, we're all tired (Except Ariel, and who knows what's gotten into him) but it's not like we've never done this before! Once we reach the top, we'll meet Moltres, and she'll be happier than she's ever been. And we will meet Moltres. We've gotten close twice already. Third times the charm, right? Everything'll work out. And the important thing is that we make sure we do our jobs and keep our responsiblities to our Trainer. So c'mon. We're already halfway there.>

    This speech is greeted with assenting grumbles from the rest of the team, and they pick up the pace a bit more to keep Ariel and their Trainer in sight.
