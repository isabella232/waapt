    Ignis, 600 years ago.

    Gaston and Charlemange are waiting currently waiting off the boat while their supplies and belongings get unpacked. Gaston brings out a fan for which to cool himself off with as he takes in the fiery surroundings of Ignis. After a few minutes of waiting, he notices a man in rather rich attire approaches him.

    ???: Greetings sir. -Bows- You must be Gaston de Callone from the faraway Kalos region?

    Gaston: That I am, and who are you my good sir?

    ??? -Bows again- I am Miyazaki, advisory to Lord Ryuku and for your visit, your guide.

    Gaston: Very well. Are we going to the palace or whatever form of royal lodging is in this region now?

    Miyazaki: Indeed. Follow me.

    Gaston: As I followed through the streets, I took in the sights the nation had laid out for me. All along the port and in the main streets of the capital were artisans of all kinds. Woodworkers, weavers, blacksmiths, jewelers, each kind of artisan working with their chosen partners to create goods for trade. I also noticed many other kinds of shops along the way, small food outlets, taverns, knick-nack shops, services like doctors and army training. It was wonderful. However, I couldn't help but to feel that something was off. They way people looked at me, looked at my guide, was off-putting. I felt like they were afraid that something terrible might happen while we were in their presence. I initially shook this off as some sort of... cultural misunderstanding. Though later, I would find out what it truly meant.

    Miyazaki: Sir. We have reached the Atakashi residence.

    Gaston: ''As we entered through the gates I took in the fine sight of the palace before me. A Pagoda with roofs made out of the finest burgundy tiles held placed inbetween pylons and columns of Mahogany wood. The walls of the structure were a brilliant chinese red color and in the middle, the insignia of the Atakashi clan could be seen for all to know. The beauty and exotic splendor, I must say, captivated me. It captivated Charlemange too, albeit, in a different way."

    Gaston gently kicks his Raticate softly.

    Gaston: Charlemange, stop chewing the gate pylons. We don't want to make a scene.

    The Raticate stops chewing on the mahogany pylons and retracts his teeth from the beam, scuttling back behind Gaston and making a squeak of dissatisfaction.

    Miyazaki: -Walks back towards Gaston- The lord will see you shortly. Please, come inside. It would be rude of us not to offer you hospitality.

    Gaston: I followed the adviser. After taking off my shoes and entering the palace, it seemed much more... humble than I expected. Not too much fancy furniture or elaborate decorations, though, there existed some. This would be one of the many cultural curiosities I would encounter on my journey here. As I knelt by the kotatsu table as instructed by Miyazaki, I waited intently for my fated encounter with the Fire Lord. However, nothing is ever really as simple as they appear...
