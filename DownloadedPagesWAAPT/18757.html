    Mossdeep City

    -An unforecast storm rolls in over Mossdeep City. Most people have gone inside for shelter by this point, but Anima, dressed in a hooded black raincoat, is walking through the streets alone, carrying a small object covered by a towel to keep it dry.-

    -The wind is blowing the rain into the office in Roman Villiard's home, where he's been working as he waits for a call. He doesn't pay the storm much mind until a particularly strong gale pushes a pile of papers off his desk and knocks his antique sword down from the wall where it hung. He stands up and slams the windows shut in frustration, then throws a Pokéball to send out his Weavile.-

    Villiard: Help me get the windows in the rest of the house?

    -The Weavile nods, and the two of them split up to close the rest of the windows before anything else can be knocked over. When the Weavile climbs up onto a counter to reach a window in the kitchen, it's quickly and quietly knocked out by a Drain Punch from a Mienshao entering the room. Anima follows closely behind.-

    Anima: Good job.

    -The Mienshao nods as Anima sets the device down on the table and takes the towel off. She pulls out her phone, dials a number, and holds it to her ear.-

    Anima: I'm all set. Give me fifteen minutes.

    -Not a second later, the power goes out across all of Mossdeep City. Judging by the beep coming through Anima's phone now, phone service is down, too.-

    Anima: How many mons do you think I'll need? No... might as well have everyone out. Just in case.

    -She releases all the other mons she has with her from their balls and presses a few buttons on the machine. It begins to whir as she walks upstairs.-

    -Villiard is back in his office when the lights go out, and he takes out his anger by slamming his fist against his desk. He grabs his cell phone to call the person he's meeting with, but it, of course, doesn't go through.-

    Villiard: Shit. This is the worst possible—

    -He looks up to see Anima standing at the entrance to his office.-

    Villiard: ...Who the hell are you?

    Anima: That's irrelevant. Get it?

    -She calmly walks toward him as her mons file in behind her. Villiard chuckles nervously.-

    Villiard: You... you think... what is this about?! If you're here because of— ...Aaah!

    -Having noticed that his words are not stalling her in any way, he sprints to the cabinet behind him, where he keeps his other Pokéballs. He throws them to send out his other mons, but none of them open.-

    Villiard: Wh... what is this?!

    Anima: So was that Weavile the only mon you had out before I activated the jammer? I'll admit that makes this rather anticlimactic.

    Villiard: Jammer? What... you can't possibly have...

    -He crawls along the floor to the sword that fell earlier. He picks it up and holds it in front of him as he pushes himself back to his feet.

    Villiard: St-stay back!

    -Anima nods, and her Charizard steps forward and knocks the sword aside with a Dragon Claw. Villiard is on his knees once more.-

    Villiard: What is this about...? Can you tell me that, at least?

    Anima: What did Ema mean to you?

    Villiard: Ema? She was my daughter, I loved her with all my—

    Anima: Lies.

    -She kicks him in the head, and he falls onto his side, groaning in pain.-

    Anima: The truth, please. Get it?

    -She grabs a knife from beneath her raincoat and spins it between her fingers.-

    Villiard: You want to know... what I thought about Ema... do you...?

    -He laughs weakly.-

    Villiard: She was incompetent. She couldn't do a thing without my help, and she refused to do what I asked far too many times. She didn't respect me. And no matter what I did to her, she never learned! And on her own, she was useless! She was nothing without me, and yet she clung to the idea that she'd somehow, by magic, be able to fulfill her dreams! No, that was never going to happen. If she wanted that, she should have just listened to me and done exactly as I said, and she couldn't even manage something as simple as that! That useless bitch was no daughter of mine!

    -He gives another weak laugh.-

    Villiard: Is that what you wanted to hear?! Becau—

    -With a disgusted look on her face, Anima plunges the knife into Villiard's chest, and he dies right where he lies. She pulls the weapon out and puts it back beneath her coat. Villiard's blood starts to cover the floor around him in a puddle. Anima steps around it and picks Villiard's antique sword. She stabs it into the open wound left by the knife.-

    -She approaches Villiard's desk, grabs the empty Pokéball and walks back downstairs. She returns the Weavile to its ball, disables the device, and walks out.-

    -Shortly after, the storm passes, and the power returns to the city as if nothing happened. The phone in Villiard's office rings, but no one is there to answer.-

    Shoal Cave Lab, shortly after

    -Anima returns to the lab. Choice isn't around at the moment, but Dane's there. Anima sets the jammer down on the table.-

    Anima: It's done.

    -Dane takes a sip of his water.-

    Dane: I take it everything went well?

    -Anima nods.-

    Anima: Thankfully. I took longer to kill him than you said the jammer could be guaranteed to be reliable. If I didn't have him in a corner away from his Pokéballs he probably could have sent out his mons after all. That would have complicated things.

    Dane: But that's all you need to do! Get them away from their mons before they can use them. I'm sure his fear was a factor in that being so easy. That's probably something you can use again.

    Anima: Yeah. I get it.

    -She pauses. Dane takes another sip of water.-

    Anima: I wasn't invisible enough. I could have killed him before he noticed I was there. The police will surely investigate this.

    Dane: On the other hand, the failure of the power lines and phone towers should be untraceable back to us. They'll just consider it a natural result of this freak storm.

    Anima: But if they find out we started that storm...

    Dane: I wouldn't worry. No one in the police force is that competent.

    -He laughs.-

    Dane: And even if someone is, they're not going to figure everything out before we do this for real. You ready to go through with that now? All your worries have been accounted for?

    -Anima nods.-

    Anima: Most of them, anyway.

    Dane: Excellent, excellent.

    -He takes a sip of water.-

    Dane: You know, I never asked. There were no witnesses, were there?

    Anima: Aside from the Weavile, not as far as I know.

    Dane: And you took care of the Weavile?

    -She sets its ball on the table.-

    Dane: ...I see.

    Anima: We've got a whole colony of Sneasel and Weavile staying here with us. I thought one of them might want to do something with it.

    Dane: Of course, of course.

    -He laughs.-

    Dane: Then I should get started on the preparations. A region-wide disabling of all power and communication lines... I'll never understand how you came up with this.
