    Princesse des Ténèbres

    -the club had no sign on the outside, but a glowing neon sign over the bar notifies Joseph of the name. Princesse des ténèbres, in Kalosian, means "princess of shadows", a name that quickly proves to be apt. While it's a lively place-glasses clink, patrons babble, and the band warms up-it's lit like a morgue. There are outlines of people sitting at tables, raising cups to their lips and getting out of their chairs, but they seem to only exist in silhouettes. All the light in the club is focused on the dance floor as though it lies beneath a giant searchlight, illuminating the jazz quartet that sits by the floor in addition to the dancers waiting for the band to stop messing about and start playing-

    Joseph: Do you think the bartender knows? Bartenders know everything in stories.

    Ratty: Good idea. Do you see Puppet?

    -Joseph turns his head over his shoulder-

    Joseph: Looks like he's trying to get away from everyone. Poor guy doesn't like crowds, either?

    -the quartet starts to play; the rhythm section starts, before the bass starts to pluck and thrum over it-

    Ratty: All the same, we should keep an eye on him.

    Joseph: He's only by the exit! We'll take him with when we're leavi-oh?

    -the saxophone and trumpet (no, not a trumpet; cornet? Was that what it was called?) pair up; that is, they play mostly in time with each other, but not swinging or being tightly knit with the rhythm. It's not music to dance to, but the dancers don't seem to care-

    Joseph: ...odd.

    Ratty: What?

    Joseph: I don't know everything about jazz, but I know enough to know that that song isn't from the twenties.

    Ratty: Hmm...what's the song?

    Joseph: "Lonely Woman", I think. By Ornette Coleman.

    Ratty: Was he big in [New Orleans]?

    Joseph: He spent some time there, I think.

    Ratty: Well, that's not unusual, then. Sometimes, stories take things and keeps them in the same space while taking them out of time. Now, we can't really dwell on th-

    -he's interrupted by a tap on Joseph's shoulder-

    Joseph: Oh?

    -a dark-skinned man, in a white button shirt and a pair of suspenders, looks down at Joseph with a somewhat grave expression-

    Man: Young man, I'm going to have to ask you to take off that coat.

    Joseph: Eh? Why? Is there a dress code?

    -the man quickly shakes his head-

    Man: No. It's just bad magic to have some kid wearing a red coat and hood about the club. You know what I mean, don't you? You can summon something like that.

    -Joseph winces-

    Joseph: Hate to break it to you, but he's already been summoned. It should probably take a while for him to get here, though.

    -the man gulps, wiping sweat off his forehead-

    Man: ...it pains me to say this to a boy your age, but I really need you to get out of this club. We can't afford a run-in with something like that.

    -Joseph frowned, deeply-

    Joseph: Well, I wasn't planning on staying the night. All I need is-

    Man: Food? I feel bad for kicking you out like this, I can do that much.

    -Joseph's stomach answers before he can, with a loud growl-

    Joseph: ...that would be good.

    -the man makes his way behind the bar quickly, taking out a cloth satchel and putting in bread, cheese and beef. He threw in a couple of brown bottles-

    Joseph: That's not-

    Man: Only root beer. You need your wits about you like this. Now-

    -he thrusts the sack into Joseph's hands-

    Man: Go. Quickly, please.

    -Joseph nods, and takes Ratty to the exit, stepping outside. Puppet, gloomily, follows-
