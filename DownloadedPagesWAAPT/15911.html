    397761 Herbert40k19th Jul 2014 03:40:42 PM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
    Imperial Commissar

    Rasp Cavern - Earlier this morning

    -Lucius is currently getting changed out of his costume whilst his coffee is brewing, thinking to himself.-

    Lucius: (Ugh, how did things end up getting so awkward between Sarah and I? They never used to be this... okay, scratch that, they always used to be this awkward, but it was kind of one-sided on my end. And why did Crewe make such a big deal about seeing us together like that? Even if she thought we were... that shouldn't bother her... right?)

    -He sighs.-

    Lucius: (...I need some advice.)

    -He sends out Bishop.-

    Lucius: Hey Bish, you're good at relationships, aren't you? Can you give me some help with issues that I may or may not be imagining?

    Bishop: <...Considering that I have never maintained a romantic relationship in my life, I would hardly describe myself as "good" at it. Still, after having witnessed your behaviour over the last few days, I believe I can hopefully provide some measure of counsel.>

    Lucius: You really think so? I haven't even told you what my issues are yet.

    Bishop: <Then please, go ahead and do so. The least I can do is hear you out.>

    -Lucius sighs.-

    Lucius: Okay, so you know I've been spending a lot of time with Sarah and Crewe over the last couple of days, right? Well, last night Sarah ended up taking her shirt off for me - uh, okay, I know that doesn't sound great, but I swear that it was for entirely innocent reasons - but Crewe walked in on us and got the wrong idea entirely. Now I think we've pissed her off and Sarah's been acting really awkwardly ever since and I've got no idea how to handle the situation assuming that a situation even exists at all.

    Bishop: <Hmm...>

    -He taps his chin with his claw.-

    Bishop: <I think that part of the problem may stem from the fact that you are sending a number of mixed signals.>

    Lucius: What? What does that have to do with anything that I just mentioned?

    Bishop: <To begin with, it may explain Crewe's annoyance. Think for a moment about how this whole situation looks from her perspective. You invite her to take part in a covert activity under the cover of night, grant her full access to your living space, and then specifically request that she keep the details of this arrangement hidden from her boyfriend. Why do you think she was so surprised to see you with Sarah?>

    Lucius: ...Wait, are you telling me that I've been accidentally seducing Crewe?

    Bishop: <Not so much accidentally as subconsciously. Considering how many people know about your alter ego already, your reasoning for not allowing Crewe to disclose her secret identity to Straw falls flat. At the very least, you have the excuse that Illusion could uncover your identity through Samantha. There is a justification there. Whether or not I agree with said justification is another matter, but the justification is there. Does Crewe have a similar reasoning for being unable to inform Straw of her nighttime escapades?>

    Lucius: Well... to be fair, I never insisted that she-

    Bishop: <That was a yes or no question, Lucius.>

    Lucius: ...Then no. No, I suppose she doesn't.

    -His gaze drops to the ground.-

    Lucius: ...You're mad at me now, aren't you?

    Bishop: <No, I'm not mad. I reserve my anger for situations that truly warrant it. At present, I am more disappointed than anything. This kind of behaviour is beneath you, Lucius. Instead of simply allowing a friendship to remain platonic, you went behind your girlfriend's back in order to coerce a girl into concealing her activities with you from her significant other.>

    Lucius: ...See, it sounds so much worse when you put it like that...

    Bishop: <Allow me to specify. It is not so much the action itself that I am condemning as the mindset that it is indicative of. And it must be said that Crewe herself is not entirely without blame, given how willing she was to go along with the whole business of keeping secrets.>

    -He folds his arms.-

    Bishop: <I think it's time you got your feelings in order, Lucius. Much of the time, you seem to be forgetting that you are already in a relationship.>

    Lucius: Hey! I'm only keeping this a secret from Samantha because I care about her. I don't want her to get hurt. I'll admit that we haven't seen each other in ages, but-

    Bishop: <Which is part of the problem, if you ask me. The two of you are both so busy putting yourselves in harm's way that you neglect the other. And whilst we are on the subject, it is worth noting that your relationship with Samantha was essentially the result of a PG-rated one-night stand.>

    Lucius: I... what are you trying to tell me?

    Bishop: <That one way or another, you need to rethink your attitude. You can only lead people on for so long before you end up with another situation like Crewe's on your hands. For example, I have yet to even mention Sarah and her->

    Lucius: But there's nothing to discuss there! We're friends! Sure, I'll admit that things used to be complicated because I was a dumbass and tried to see a connection where there wasn't one, but we're past that now. Ours is a purely platonic relationship.

    -Bishop does the Scizor equivalent of raising an eyebrow.-

    Bishop: <Are you sure about that?>

    Lucius: Yes! And I'm sure that if I asked Sarah, she'd agree.

    Bishop: <Perhaps you ought to go ahead and do that at some point, then. In the meantime, did you require any further advice?>

    -Lucius thinks for a moment, then shakes his head.-

    Lucius: No, I... for the moment, I'm fine. I need some time to think about things rather than more things to think about. Thanks for the advice, Bish.

    -He recalls Bishop, then sits himself down on the cave floor, nursing his coffee.-

    Lucius: (...Looks like Sam and I are going to need to have a talk soon...)
