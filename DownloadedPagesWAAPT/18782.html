    -frowns a bit-

    Samantha: I'd be lying if I said I didn't wish I could just do that, though. Would make keeping my legs smooth a lot easier, but that's beside the point because I'm here to see you about a Purrloin.

    -Joseph perks up a bit at the mention of a Pokemon-

    Joseph: Oh? You mean Seamus?

    Samantha: No. I found a baby Purrloin in an alleyway, and she has...balance problems. I took her in to get an MRI, and it said that she has something called...cerebellar hypoplasia, I think it's called?

    Joseph: Alright...?

    Samantha: But the thing is that I don't know how to deal with it, and whether she's...y'know, capable of living a...

    Joseph: A full and happy life?

    Samantha: Yeah.

    -Joseph runs into the bathroom to wipe the shaving cream off before returning-

    Joseph: Alright. I've only seen a couple of CH catmons in my time as a breeder, but generally they can and do live happy lives. They just need gentle, loving, understanding care. If you don't feel up to that, I can take her in; I'll need to find room for some of my other mons, but-

    -Samantha's face shifts into a frown-

    Samantha: I didn't come here to be insulted, Hudson.

    Joseph: What? What'd I say?

    Samantha: You don't think I love my mons, is that it?

    Joseph: No, not at all! It's just that...well, you're a battler, and a CH catmon isn't going to battle. Are you OK with that?

    Samantha: Of course I am! I just don't want to take care of her and worry that she's longing for death the entire time.

    -Joseph nods, understandingly-

    Joseph: Ooooh, I see. Well, she shouldn't, but send her out so I can see her. I'll get a read on her emotions.

    -Samantha nods, releasing the Purrloin kitten from her Pokeball-

    Purrloin: <Puuuuuurr!>

    -she keeps her claws dug into the carpet, swaying gently from side to side with her tail hiked up in order to balance herself-

    Joseph: Ohohoho, isn't she sweet...?

    -he gently gets down on his knees with the Purrloin-

    Joseph: She doesn't feel suicidal, that's for sure...she can't communicate well, but that's just because she's so young rather than any disability in speaking. She can walk, right?

    Samantha: A little bit.

    -the Purrloin demonstrates by taking a few slow, lopsided steps before flopping onto her side, looking up with a slightly irritated mewl. Joseph, despite himself, chuckles softly and affectionately as he rights her-

    Joseph: You OK there, baby?

    -the Purrloin slowly nods-

    Purrloin: <Purr~>

    Joseph: Oooh, you're an affectionate one! But I'm not your trainer...she is!

    -he holds the Purrloin out to Samantha, who carefully extends her hand. The Purrloin silently looks up-

    Purrloin: <...>

    -she bops at Samantha's hand softly with her head-

    Samantha: What's that mean?

    Joseph: That's called bunting. It's a loving gesture.

    -he smiles-

    Joseph: But I didn't need to know that to know that she likes you a lot!

    -he hands Samantha her Purrloin-

    Joseph: She's perfectly happy! You just need to teach her how to walk better; that's a bit out of my expertise, but you can ask around for that sorta stuff. Good luck!

    -he waves, happily. Samantha, smiling, reciprocates-

    Joseph: Shit, I almost forgot.

    -he shakes his fist in rage-

    Joseph:    KEARNEEEEEY!   

    -Samantha returns-

    Samantha:    HUDSOOOOOON!   

    -the two of them nod and go about their business-
    A tooth for an eye!

    469539 Herbert40k23rd Sep 2015 09:11:09 AM from Merry Olde England Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: I-It's not like I like you, or anything!
    Imperial Commissar

    Sootopolis City

    Dull grey clouds cover the morning sky over Sootopolis, blotting out the sun and raining down drizzle on the handful of people who happen to be up and about this early. Amongst them are a girl in her late teens, with olive skin and long black hair that comes down to her waist, who is hanging around the outskirts of town with a Greninja by her side.

    Greninja: <Tara, I'm coooooooooooold.>

    Tara: I though Water-types were supposed to like the rain.

    Greninja: <Not this Water-type. Why can't we go back inside and read comics or something?>

    Tara: Because if you're going to be stuck like this every night, then you might as well learn how to make the most of it.

    Greninja: <Couldn't we do that some other day, though?>

    Tara: ...Sure. I'll tell Earthwyrm that our next plan should be building a giant, movable disk to permanently block out the sun so that you can go out and be froggy whenever you feel like it.

    Greninja: <Ooooh, sounds like fun!>

    Tara facepalms.

    Tara: Let's just get this out of the way so I can get back to something more fulfilling, like rhythmically banging my head against a wall. What can you do so far?

    The Greninja scratches her head in thought.

    Greninja: <Um... well, I can make bubbles!>

    Tara: That's a start, I suppos-

    Greninja: <Ooh ooh ooh, I nearly forgot! I can make them into shapes too! Take a look~>

    She blows out two bubbles, one in the shape of Shingeko's helmet and the other in the shape of a heart. Tara reaches out and pops them both.

    Tara: Maybe I should rephrase that question. What can you do so far that's actually useful?

    Greninja: <Bubbles aren't useful?>

    Tara: Not unless we're fighting someone with a crippling fear of soap. Let's start off with something simple, like Water Pulse. There's no way you can mess that up.

    Greninja: <Water Pulse? That'll be easy-peasy for a ninja following in the footsteps of Shingeko himself~!>

    She strikes what is presumably meant to be an action-hero pose.

    Tara: ...That said, I've been wrong before.

    She pulls a Pokeball out of her bag and rests it on top of a nearby rock.

    Tara: Just aim a burst of water at the Pokeball and try to knock it off.

    Greninja: <Okie-dokie! But, um... how, exactly?>

    Tara: I dunno, I've never been a Pokemon before. You're the Greninja here. It can't be any harder than making bubbles that look like your crush's head, though.

    Greninja: <You think so? I'll give it a shot...>

    She sticks her tongue out of her mouth - moreso than it already is, anyway - in concentration and aims her front feet at the Pokeball. A pulsating blast of water comes shooting out and knocks the ball straight off its perch. Her face breaks into a grin.

    Greninja: <I did it!>

    Tara: ...Huh. First try. Not bad. Wanna try something a little tougher?

    Greninja: <Yup! See, I knew being a ninja would be as cool as it looks!>

    Tara smiles faintly, then pulls out another Pokeball and holds it out in her hand, tossing it up and down.

    Tara: Think you can hit a moving target?

    Greninja: <Sure I can! Man, this is way easier than I thought it'd be~>

    She fires off another blast of water... which flies off-target and hits Tara square in the face, drenching her.

    Greninja: <Uh... whoops.> ^_^

    Tara: ...I'll admit, I was kind of asking for that one.
