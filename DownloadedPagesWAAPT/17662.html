    441541 ChaoticMarin24th Jan 2015 07:13:52 PM from Nintendoland Get RP Mod , Relationship Status: 700 wives and 300 concubines
    Chiptune DJ

    Dewford Gym — Some time ago.

    Valorie looked between Pangoro and Dragonite. Hm. For a moment she seemed torn. On one hand she really did want to give it a shot, but on the other Dragonite was in a rather poor spot and Brawly had already made it clear what he wanted to see. She supposed he had asked nicely, and there wasn't a whole lot of consequence to returning a pokemon that had already done so much work in a single battle. She produced her pokeball and returned Dragonite, replacing the ball with another. With a twist of her tiny body she reached back and tossed said ball, two large wings froming amidst a white mass and—

    It was Charizard! The pseudo dragon and Valorie both immediately struck a combative pose, taking one step forward and raising a single balled fist aggressively. "Let's do this!" she called out, Charizard emitting an aggressive roar. "Charizard, Sunny Day!" Valorie commanded. The pseudo dragon took a deep breath and spat a ball of condensed superheated gas into the air, mimicking the effects of powerful sunlight with it's light and heat. Charizard's wings spread to catch the sunlight, her thin wing membranes seeming to glow brightly as light passed through them.

    "Pangoro—Bulk Up." Brawly replied calmly, quietly assessing Charizard's threat. That was definitely an unusual glow surrounding her body...

    As if on cue, Valorie decided to reveal her game plan. "Now Charizard, Air Slash!" she commanded. It was a relatively simple yet extremely important rule of thumb: When in a fighting gym, flying type pokemon had an advantage! Charizardrose into the air, her wings shining with a brilliant light. With several powerful flaps of her wings she charged and launched air in the forms of deadly shock waves ripping through the air as they shot through the air at Pangoro. The daunting pokemon was nearly thrown across the gym as the razor sharp waves of energy cut into him, eliciting a cry as he staggered in the process of regaining his footing.

    Brawly was the embodiment of focus, not allowing himself to be fazed by the strength behind the move. If anything he was pleased, but it was time to push the other to her limits. "You can take the heat, Pangoro! Retaliate with Stone Edge!" Brawly ordered. Valorie flinched. "Dodge it, Charizard!" she blurted out, only for... nothing to happen. Pangoro seemed to be struggling to recover his composure after the assault, unable to retaliate. An awkward moment ensued where all four of those present just sort of took in what was happening. Disappointment crossed Brawly's face and glee crossed Valorie's.

    "That's just perfect. Let's show them what we're made of Charizard!" Valorie remarked, a delighted little smirk resting upon her face. <...'We'> Charizard remarked under her breath, resenting not being given a bit more credit in that statement. "Let's go, Fire Blast! This is the power that will dominate the pokemon league!" Valorie shouted, earning herself two brows slightly raised at her arrogance. An inferno brewed within Charizard's mouth, briefly blinding Brawly as a fireball emerged. The blazing fireball took advantage of Pangoro recovering to score an easy hit, enveloping the panda pokemon and erupting in five directions to form the symbol dai as it consumed Pangoro in righteous fire.

    <Graaaagh—What is with you and siiiiize?!> Pangoro cried out, collapsing onto one knee as the flames dissipated. Observing Pangoro's scorched body Brawly came to realize that much like Dragonite, Pangoro was now in a rather poor position to continue fighting.

    "...Pangoro? Stone Edge." Brawly commanded softly, wanting to at least see how Charizard took an attack and find closure in the battle. In truth, his mind was already made up. To Valorie's shock Pangoro responded this time, forcing himself to stand and slamming a charged fist into the ground. A shock wave rocketed out underneath Charizard, a spire of rock ripping free of the ground on a collision course with her.

    "Charizard, get out of the way!" Valorie warned, but they were not fast enough. The rocky spire slammed into Charizard, hitting her hard and ripping her out of the air. With a stuttered cry she slammed into a nearby wall and collapsed. Valorie winced, but she knew Charizard wouldn't fail her after only one attack. The pseudo dragon forced herself back to her feet, injured but not out of the fight quite yet. "Finish Pangoro off with Air Slash, quickly!" Valorie commanded. Charizard stumbled a little, but it passed and she took to the air again, sending Pangoro skidding backwards with another flurry of airborne shockwaves that brought the battle to an abrupt and decisive win. Pangoro collapsed on his face with a thud and a groan, being quickly returned by Brawly.

    "Alright, you've got me. Looks like you know where your want your journey to take you~" Brawly admitted, untensing by doing a couple of relaxation exercises as he moved to close the distance between the two trainers. "When I saw you and your Charizard fight I was surprised by how in-sync you were. It was almost perfect. Just remember that pokemon journies are kind of like waves. You can do your best to prepare for them, but sometimes you gotta let them guide you for a bit if you wanna ride them out, wherever that takes you." Brawly explained, producing a single badge and holding it out in offering.

    Valorie reached up to take the badge, quite eager to add it to her collection. "...Don't worry." she replied cheerily. "Whether it's the sky, the sea or the snow my pokemon will carry me through~" she went on, not paying very much mind to the words of advice. She wasn't sure what Brawly was trying to tell her, but she wasn't about to let it rain on her victory.
    I dropped out of school to become a pokemon master, but then they pulled me right back in to get my doctorate!
