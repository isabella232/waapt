    -Spectrum crashes to the ground and unleashes a rainbow-colored burst of volcanic energy from under Pallad, while Lamarr rushes up and slams into him, cloaked in water.-

    [▓░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░]

    Ellen: Let's see how much that Kabutops has been trained. Hammer Arm!

    -Pallad slams a metal limb into Lamarr, leaving her with little more than a sliver of health left.-

    Lamarr: <Owowowowow>

    Sarah: You can pull through! Waterfall! Spectrum

    -She smiles.-

    Sarah: Rest.

    -Spectrum starts to relax as Lamarr chips away the last bit of Pallad's health.-

    [░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░░]

    Pallad fainted!

    Sarah: Phew that's done although I can't help but wonder, why didn't you send him out last? You've still got one Pokemon left.

    -Ellen smiles-

    Ellen: Because I absolutely love surprises, that's why. C'mon out.

    -Sarah blinks as a metallic figure descends from above, her eyes widening as she recognizes the tattered yellow scarf around the figure's neck.-

    Skye: <Hello, Sarah I never thought I'd be on this side of the field.>

    Sarah: Skye you're on her team now?

    Skye: <As much as I liked going almost entirely human I started to miss the battling life.>

    Ellen: So we decided she'd join my team. I would have asked Edge to fight this time, but the League's really weird about Delta Pokemon in battles.

    -Sarah smiles slightly-

    Sarah: If you were going to do that, why not just step in yourself?

    -Sarah's smile is matched by Ellen's own-

    Ellen: That would have had even more complications. I'd say this was worth it anyway.

    Sarah: Oh, totally.

    -She smirks as Spectrum blinks awake.-

    Sarah: Especially because it gave me an excuse to stall. Lamarr, Smack Down!

    -Lamarr quickly lobs a rock at Skye's wing, denting it.-

    Skye: <Gah!>

    Sarah: Spectrum, follow up with an Earth Power!

    -A burst of technicolor lava explodes from under the now-grounded Skye

    Ellen: <Clever, clever. Skye, Icy Wind.>

    -Spectrum shivers as the cold wind washes over her, pulling her to about ⅔ health. Lamarr simply curls up on the ground, fading in consciousness.-

    Lamarr fainted!

    Sarah: Keep it up, Spec. Earth Power!

    -Skye is knocked down to about ½. She retaliates with another Icy Wind, receives another Earth Power, blows another Icy Wind-

    -And falls to a final Earth Power.-

    Ellen: ...And that's that. You win. Congratulations.

    ???: You sure have a habit of impressing champions, don't you?

    -Sarah blinks, and turns back toward the entrance. In the doorway stand two figures: a tall, attractive man with silver hair, and a shorter girl with a streak of red amongst her mostly black hair.-

    Steven Stone: You sure make things interesting, to say the least.

    Diane: I've known her for years, and I can definitely say that things stay interesting around her. Heck, I wouldn't even be here if she didn't.

    Cynthia: And it was a pleasure to be able to be one of your two final opponents here in Sinnoh.

    -Ellen revives Skye, who stretches her wings.-

    Ellen: And likewise, I'm glad I could finally battle you all-out. Come, there's no point in delaying any further.

    -The group enters through the doorway to the Hall of Fame.-

    Hall of Fame

    -Ellen quickly returns Skye's armband to her, and returns both Pallad's Metagrossite and Key Stone to Steven before the group continues through the hall.-

    Ellen: It's been an awful long time since I've set foot in here. It's actually not really all that different, though.

    Cynthia: The Hall of Fame is a timeless record, it's only natural that the room remain the same.

    Sarah: And yet it still feels so I dunno, imposing. Even after having gone through this same ritual three times before now.

    Steven: You're not planning to stop at four, are you? You've still got Hoenn at the very least to visit.

    Sarah: Oh, of course. As long as you can promise it'll be worth it.

    Diane: You can't possibly think it's not worth it to set foot in a room like this, no matter how many times you've done it already.

    Skye: It sure is worth it now. Even though normally it's only the champion, the challenger, and their teams.

    Cynthia: I'm sure everyone can forgive this exception.

    -Sarah steps up to the record machine.-

    Sarah: Well here goes, for the fourth time

    Ellen: Now wasn't the time to tell her. Not in her moment of victory. And we aren't the ones meant to tell her
