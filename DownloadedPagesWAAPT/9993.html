    The Forge

    | The Chingling above the door sings out as Zeta enters.

    | After a brief moment, in which the sound of shifting items and clattering metal can be heard emanating from the back, a burly, bearded man emerges from behind the counter. He is soon followed by two Pansears.

    ???: Hello. Something catch your eye?

    Zeta: Indeed. I noticed that your wares are forged in the Orren style.

    | At this, the man tenses, and both Pansears jump up onto the counter, their heads heating up.

    Zeta: Woah, woah, woah. Calm down. I ain't here to hurt ya. Hell, I don't even know ya.

    ???: How did you know? Few are knowledgeable enough to recognize the Orren style.

    Zeta: Name's Zeta. Zeta Bismarck. I'm from Orre.

    ???: Bismarck? You Sylvester's girl?

    Zeta: Yeah. What 'bout it?

    ???: Why didn't ya say so?

    | He relaxes, and the 'Sears, taking their cue from him, start to cool down.

    ???: The name's Wayland! Wayland Smith.

    Zeta: Wayland? Gramps used to talk about you.

    Wayland: How is the old bastard?

    Zeta: ...

    Zeta: Dead. Cipher finally got him this past year.

    Wayland: Oh.

    Wayland: Shit.

    Zeta: Yeah.

    Wayland: Damn. I told him to get out while he could, to leave with me.

    Zeta: He always was a stubborn old goat.

    Wayland: Let me go get something.

    | He disappears once more into the back, then reemerges with a dusty, yet well preserved bottle, and two glasses.

    Wayland: Good ole Orren Desert Fire.

    | He pours two glasses, giving one to Zeta and keeping one for himself.

    Wayland: To absent friends.

    Zeta: To absent friends.

    | They knock back the drinks.

    Zeta: That stuff still tastes like burning horse piss.

    Wayland: Yeah. Never did understand why he liked the stuff.

    | They converse for hours, sharing stories about Sylvester Bismarck, sometimes laughing, sometimes crying, sometimes angry, sometimes sad.

    | Eventually, the conversation winds down.

    Wayland: So, what brings you out of Orre? Last I heard, you were sticking it to the Man.

    Zeta: Heh. Yeah.

    Zeta: I came here chasing down Pi.

    Wayland: You mean poor Warden's boy?

    Zeta: Yeah. He left, apparently to chase down his Dad.

    Wayland: That man doesn't want to be found.

    Zeta: I'm not so sure.

    | She shows him the note from Mr. Warden, about the clues in Village Bridge, and explains why she is running this errand for Pi instead of him doing it personally.

    Wayland: Well, I'll be. It'd be nice to see the man again. I miss him dearly.

    Zeta: Yeah.

    Zeta: Hey.

    Wayland: Hmm?

    Zeta: You can come with us.

    Wayland: And what would I do with my shop?

    Zeta: Don't you have a junior, that can handle business until you return?

    Wayland: Well, yes.

    Zeta: Then why not?

    Wayland: ...

    Wayland: You know what, screw it, let's go.

    | He writes a note for his junior, grabs a mobile forge kit, and a sword.

    Wayland: Come on, boys, we're heading out on a little trip.

    | And with that, they exit the shop.

    Reversal Mountain Exterior

    | Zeta, Glossa, Cetus, Pomp, Wayland, and the Sears are making their way to the entry to the cave.

    | As they stroll through the dark grass, they are accosted by a Pansage.

    Pansage: <Oiy, Oiy, Oiy! What're ya doin' on my turf!>

    Zeta: Whadda ya mean?

    Pansage: <This is my turf! You'se be trespassin. I'm gonna hafta punish ya!>

    Zeta: ...

    Zeta: Pomp, Fire-Punch.

    | Pomp flies forward, with fist drawn back. As she swings, it bursts into flame, and knocks the Pansage back onto its behind.

    Zeta: Hah!

    | Zeta throws a Cherish Ball at the Pansage.

    | The Pansage is caught!

    | Zeta sends him back out.

    Zeta: So what's your name, then?

    Pansage: <The name's Sage.>

    Zeta: Alright then, Sage. You have any objections to swearing loyalty to me? Join my group?

    Sage: <Fine. I do swear my loyalty, on my name as a punk, to you.>

    Zeta: Very good.

    Zeta: Wayland, go on ahead to Undella. We'll catch up in just a bit.

    Wayland: Alright. See you on the other side.

    | And he moves on ahead.

    Zeta: Let's see if we can't find you a little something in the caves.
