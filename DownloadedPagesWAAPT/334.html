I was just a sweet, innocent girl who couldn't see anything wrong with the world, but as I got older, things began to change. All of a sudden, I was wandering around the playground alone, wondering why none of my friends would no longer play with me.

    Ribbons: (whispering) < And why didn't they play with you? >

    Well, I guess they outgrew me... And from there, it only got worse. I wanted to keep the friends I had, but things just got harder because we no longer understood eachother as we had become teenagers by then, and I wasn't ready to grow up yet. I eventually stopped trying, deciding I was better off alone.

    Ribbons: (whispering) < I'm sorry to hear that. >

    After I had gotten out of school, I spent most of my time at home back in Sinnoh. I just wanted to keep to myself so I wouldn't have to risk having my feelings hurt again. My mother noticed how lonely I was at that point, and knowing how much I loved Pokémon from all the studying I had been doing, she decided to let me become a Pokémon trainer in hopes that it would help me connect with other people again.

    Ribbons: < Did it work? >

    Well, after I received my starter Pokémon, Hot Stuff. This boy I know who had also started his journey, suggested we go look for the legendary Pokémon said to live at Lake Verity. When I got there, and I heard the voice of Mesprit... I somehow knew that everything was going to be okay now. Sure, I spent most of my time with my Pokémon at first, not really speaking much to other trainers, but at some point I realized that I had made a lot of friends among the trainers of Sinnoh without even trying!

    Ribbons: (smiles, still whispering) < Well, I'm glad everything worked out in the end. >

    Yeah... I guess we should head through Pinwheel forest to search for Jane's Servine later. I hear there a lot of bug type Pokémon there, so it should be a great place to train Chase some more. I also hear that the gym leader in Castelia city uses bug types.
