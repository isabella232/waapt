    Maggie: "You want proof? Let me find something that might be a bit familiar..."

    -She pulls up this video and shows it to Kamdida-

    Kamdida: <...>

    <This...>

    Maggie: "If you want any more proof, I've got more where that came from."

    Kamdida: <...So, all this... All this is just a game to you?>

    Maggie: "What, no? Based on a game or not, my Emboar's still in danger of getting possessed in the end."

    "This just means I know how to get through and maybe save him before it happens."

    Kamdida: <But if this is all paralleling this game as you say...>

    <Doesn't that mean you already know what happens in the end? Isn't it unavoidable?>

    Maggie: "No. There are a lot of things along the way that have been different. The story's generally been the same, but... some things could turn out differently."

    "That's what I've been hoping from the beginning. Even if I know it'll all work out in the end, that doesn't mean I want Squealer to get possessed if I can at all avoid it."

    Kamdida: <Hm.>

    <I... don't know what to think of this, honestly.>

    Maggie: "I don't blame you. Even if this is real life, it is weird to think of it as following the rules of a story."

    Kamdida: <You sound like you're not just referring to me with that.>

    Maggie: "Yeah... I'm kind of talking about the J-Team's adventures in general."

    "I mean... I got used to it through the TV Show and even when I was travelling, I still saw some ways with how things applied. Like how the J-Team always wins and how members never stay dead."

    "I know Tagg is always telling me to be careful because there are things that could kill me."

    "And I am, I could still get dangerously injured or something else bad happen to me, but..."

    "I... have never gotten into anything J-Team related and thought 'I could die from this,' I guess I just don't think that way. I don't want to be disrespectful to Tagg and there have been some things that cast doubt on that, but..."

    Kamdida: <Mm...>

    <I admit I don't know much of what to say about that or... all this... but, I trust you.>

    <It's not like I can go back, anyway.>

    Maggie: "Thanks."

    "And if you're still unsure after all this..."

    Kamdida: <...In truth, at this moment, I feel fairly sure.>

    Maggie: "...What do you mean?"

    Kamdida: <I mean, I want to stay with you.>

    <I can't say I have the most positive experience with Trainers as a whole, but if you continue to be properly respectful to me, I'm willing to be like a loyal soldier.>

    Maggie: "Oh, great!"

    "I mean, the soldier bit's a bit weird, I guess that's what you are, but-"

    Kamdida: <I'd recommend you stop now before I change my mind.>

    Maggie: "Right, sorry."

    "Welcome to the team for real, Kamdida."

    Kamdida: <I hope to be of help.>

    Maggie: "Great."

    "Now... you ready to pay Crump back?"

    Kamdida: <Oh yes...>

    -She and Maggie are about to head off, when a voice interrupts them-

    TEC: ...So, this is betrayal, then?

    -Kamdida turns her head up at the speaker-

    Kamdida: <The X-Nauts betrayed me well before I betrayed the X-Nauts.>

    <And now... I can't say I agree with Grodus' means, even if I would like to see the Alakagross revived.>

    TEC: So, this is what Sir Grodus meant when he was talking about the influence our foes had on peoplemons...

    Maggie: "I mean... what's wrong with it?"

    "It happened to you, didn't it?"

    TEC: ...I was corrupted. That is why Sir Grodus wiped my memories.

    Maggie: "...TEC. You were Squealer's friend. And I don't think that was a mistake."

    "Whatever you decided, it was your decision in the end."

    "Grodus wiped your memories because he didn't like what you decided."

    "What do you want to follow in the end?"

    TEC: I...

    I... will think this over.

    Maggie: "Okay. Sorry about your memories, but..."

    "...Wait..."

    "Do you have some sort of repository for large files or backup or something?"

    TEC: I... do, yes.

    Maggie: "Is there a file in there called throguh?"

    TEC: ...Throguh? There is one, yes.

    I'm not sure what it could mean, though.

    Maggie: "You sent me a text before your mind was wiped."

    "The last line was 'Pull throguh.' I just realized it could refer to pulling files."

    "You might be able to get your memories back from that."

    TEC: I am uncertain.

    Sir Grodus informed me said files were heavily corrupted.

    Maggie: "Well, you don't have to if you're unsure."

    "It's up to you, really."

    "...Though, we should really get going. Think about it?"
