    Imperial Commissar

    Cyllage City

    -Herbert is wandering around thee town rather aimlessly, occasionally glancing down at his Poketch and the balls on his belt.-

    Herbert: Where are those two? They still haven't gotten Flashlight back to me yet, and there's no way he's going to get used to me if he never spends any time with his own trainer...

    Bishop: <Presumably they will have moved onto the next town by now. Which I believe you were meant to be doing once you defeated the Gym Leader here, were you not?>

    Herbert: Yeah, about that...

    -He trails off, trying to think of some excuse for not having challenged the Cyllage Gym, and ends up bumping into a strangely familiar-looking raven-haired trainer.-

    Herbert: Oof!

    -He staggers back a little, and the raven-haired man brushes himself off a little before looking disdainfully at Herbert.-

    Leonard: Are your thoughts always as aimless as your wanderings?

    Herbert: Hey, it wasn't my fault! You weren't paying any attention to where you were going either, or else we wouldn't have crashed into each other in the first place!

    -Leonard narrows his eyes as if to make a retort, but instead ends up tilting his head curiously after getting a closer look at Herbert.-

    Leonard: ...Do I know you from somewhere?

    Herbert: ...I don't think so, but for whatever reason I feel like I should. You look kind of familiar.

    Leonard: Hrm. Well, I imagine that I would be a rather familiar face amongst much of the Kalosian nobility. My name is Leonard Cain, in case that rings any bells.

    Herbert: ...Wait, you're a Cain?

    -Leonard cocks an eyebrow.-

    Leonard: ...No. No I'm not. I'm actually a Kantonian arms dealer working under a false name, and you are the first person to have successfully seen through my cover story.

    Herbert: Yup, definitely a Cain. The unfunny sarcasm's a dead giveaway. Anyway, I'm a Cain too. Name's Lucius, but everybody calls me Herbert.

    Leonard: ...Discounting the obvious question of why, I don't believe I've ever made your acquaintance at any previous family gatherings.

    Herbert: That's because I'm not from Kalos. I'm from Sinnoh. Turns out that the infamous Lucanus got busy after fleeing Ransei, and I'm one of the few surviving results.

    -Leonard's expression sours somewhat.-

    Leonard: Lucanus? That's a name that's been causing us a fair bit of trouble since that damn museum exhibit went live. Business has been down ever since.

    Herbert: You ever had to survive an assassination attempt because of it?

    Leonard: ...Admittedly not.

    Herbert: Then don't talk to me about how your lineage has made your life difficult.

    -Leonard gives a gesture that could be a shrug, then glances back towards the city.-

    Leonard: ...Would you rather we take this conversation inside? The seaside breeze doesn't agree with me.

    Herbert: Sure, I guess. I don't have anything urgent planned. Not that I ever do.

    -He turns to Bishop.-

    Herbert: You going to be okay waiting here for a while?

    Bishop: <...You know, I would be much more comfortable if you went into a stranger's house with me accompanying you.>

    Herbert: I'll be fine, Bishop. I have six other Pokemon with me, after all. Besides, it's not as though I've got anything to worry about in the first place.

    Bishop: <If you insist.>

    Herbert: Well, I don't insist, but seating a six-foot tall insect made of solid steel can be an issue sometimes.

    Bishop: <I'm not made entirely of steel, you know. I doubt I would be able to move if I was, let alone fly.>

    Herbert: Bah, you know what I mean.

    -He turns back to Leonard, who has been silently surveying the conversation between boy and Scizor with an eyebrow raised.-

    Leonard: Ready to go?

    Herbert: Yeah, ready.

    -The two start to walk off, leaving Bishop to his own devices in Cyllage.-
