    Imperial Commissar

    Pyrite Town

    -After a long night spent packing and a quick hoverbike ride, Herbert pulls into the town with Bishop riding in the sidecar.-

    Herbert: Pyrite Town. You will never find a more wretched hive of scum and-

    Bishop: <We've used that line on at least two separate occasions now.>

    Herbert: Have we? Huh. Well, I'm using it again. It seems like quite an accurate way of summing up this place.

    -He hops off the bike, and is about to start heading down the streets when he hears a voice behind him.-

    Alice: Hello, Lucius.

    -He wheels around to face the rather haggard-looking Alice.-

    Herbert: Alice! Man, it's good to see you again. I was worried something might've happened to you. Are you alright? How did you get over here?

    Alice: I am fine, yes. And as for why I am here, I suspect it is for much the same reason as you are - investigating rumours of Cipher activity.

    Herbert: It's more than rumours that we're investigating, but yeah. I've been... well, I'll tell you about the shit I've had to deal with later. It's not been a fun few weeks, to put it lightly. Anyway, are you sure you're okay? You look like you've been run ragged or something.

    -Alice shudders slightly as she remembers her dream about Verax a couple of weeks ago.-

    Alice: Nightmares.

    Herbert: Really? I thought you were the one who was supposed to help me deal with those.

    Alice: That was indeed the case in the past, but as of late I have been rather... perhaps "afraid" is the wrong word, but I certainly have no desire to engage Cipher in direct confrontation. I have been thinking of ways to begin a more passive form of resistance.

    Herbert: What do you mean? We'll take all the help we can get, but...

    Alice: Considering that I am hardly in close collaboration with the rest of your group, I was hoping to establish an independent enterprise, though hopefully one that would work with you and your allies. In essence, I will be attempting to reunite Pokemon snagged by Cipher with their original trainers whilst your group do the actual fighting.

    Herbert: You'd do that? That'd help us out a bunch, certainly, but it sounds like it'd be an administrative nightmare on your part.

    Alice: I have no doubts about that. The process of purifying Shadow Pokemon, determining the ID numbers of their original trainers, and then locating said original trainers to return them to will no doubt be a lengthy one, and inevitably there will always be those who fall through the cracks. But even if it does prove to be an imperfect system, at least it will be something.

    Herbert: Yeah... that's all we can hope to do, really. To help however we can. I snagged a Shadow Machop recently, if you'd like a starting point of sorts.

    Alice: Oh? I will take a look, then. Though perhaps it would be better to do so elsewhere.

    Herbert: That's probably a good idea, yeah. Come on, let's go.

    -The two head off into town. Bishop, however, continues to hang around near the hoverbike.-

    Bishop: <Hmm... I wonder if there is any way I could start a similar endeavour. First, though, I should get to spreading Gogie's message.>

    -He heads off into the town.-
