    Um, hi. Anakin said you wanted to talk to me? Also, I can understand Pokemon. Yeah, don't ask.

    Scyther: <Interesting. And you regularly battle Pokemon?>

    Yeah. My team—Anakin and Qui-Gon, here, and also two others—and I battle against wild Pokemon to get stronger, and also against other trainers. It's a sport.

    Scyther: <A sport, you say? And it makes you stronger? ... Who are these other team members?>

    Anakin: <Obi-Wan and Windu!>

    Yeah. Like Anakin said, the other members are a Pikachu named Obi-Wan and a Metang named Windu. In fact, Windu only just evolved recently.

    Scyther: <So it does make you stronger...>

    Anakin: <It totally does! I was training a lot today, and I'm getting closer to evolving every day.>

    And having a Trainer prevents you from being hurt.

    Scyther: <Hm... well, there don't appear to any serious downsides. I'd like to try being a part of this team.>

    Anakin: <Cool! I'll fight him!>

    Woah, Anakin. You don't have to fight him. I could just catch him without you fighting him. Or not catch him at all; Qui-Gon doesn't have a Pokeball.

    Scyther: <I don't mind having a Pokeball. A place to rest seems all right.>

    Cool. I'll find one for you, then.

    -pulls off my backpack and rummages around in it, pulling out a Premiere ball-

    I found this one. Do you like it?

    Scyther: -steps over and looks it over- <Yes.>

    All right. I just need to—

    -touches the Scyther on the nose with the Pokeball. He is sucked into it and the ball drops, shaking three times before clicking. I pick it up and release the Scyther-
