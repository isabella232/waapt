    ???: That de Nile girl was quite right. You are indeed, quite mad Constantin. Normal people don't hear mysterious voices that don't have a distinct source~

    ???: Of course, there is nothing you'd do much about it, is there Constantin? You're much to noble and proud to seek out any advice or help on the issue. After all, "I'd rather have no son at all than one who has to be admitted to a shrink"

    Constantin stops and turns his head this time. The figure of what appears to be a huddled plague doctor with beady red eyes stands in his shadow.

    Constantin: Is there a purpose to this, Claudeux?

    The figure does not move.

    Claudeux: Be thankful you have that precious field of yours up, otherwise your insanity would be on full display for the whole of Kalos to see~ Besides, you know you can't get rid of me, no matter what methods you implore.

    The ghostly figure brings out large, humanesque feathered arms and wraps them around Constantin, drawing himself closer.

    Claudeux: No silver-tongued words sooth me. No half-hearted prayer removes me. I am going to be here until your dying day, to keep you grounded in reality m'lad~

    Constantin sneers as he continues on his journey down to the civic centre, the figure now being dragged along with him.

    Claudeux: Oh Constantin, when will you learn when to fold your cards? Can't you see that you have lost this battle? She will never be yours.

    Constantin lip twitches.

    Claudeux: You're getting angry. It seems that the only emotions you can register are anger and apathy. What are you, made of tin?

    It seems like Constantin has reached a tipping point, as he quickly turns around to punch "Claudeux". "Claudeux" fades away into dark mist before reforming behind him.

    Claudeux: I see how it is. Well then, don't heed my warning. Watch as you fall from your "great heights" tremendously due to your Fatal Flaw, like a "hero" from the tragedies of old. Don't say I didn't warn you~

    The figure fades away, leaving a visibly shaken Constantin stumbling slowly towards the civic centre.
