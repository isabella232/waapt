    The Internet

    One might come across a blog article that has been making it's way around, especially among the J-Team fandom

    09/15/2015

    The J-Team and the border between Pokémon and Human

    Most have heard of J-Team at this point, like them or hate them; they are known for being a group of not only talented Pokémon Trainers but also for being involved in all kinds of strange and borderline nonsensical situations. This has not been the first time I covered this group, as longtime readers might have read my article about the death and rebirth of Lucius Cain (link), but this is the first time I'll be talking about the group as a whole.

    One of the more interesting things about the J-Team is how they blurr the line between humanity and the Pokémon, not only in the sense of having a strong bond with them, but also in the possibility of its members being even capable of shapeshifting into Pokémon themselves or vice-versa.

    According to the blog of one of those members, Hikari Minami, one of the members known as Blitz is capable of changing into a Greninja and also one of the members, Duke Howlson, is said to actually be a Lucario who assumed human form and is even dating one of the other human members of the team, Lyuri Riese.

    Ms.Minami's words are notoriously deceptive, as she seems to enjoy playing tricks on others by making statements of varying levels of credibility. However, no evidence was presented to the contrary as the team itself seems to be fairly secretive about topics such as this one. Members of the team have also been seen talking to their own Pokémon and seemingly understanding their words and such ability seems to be oddly common between them.

    It is unknown if all members of the team and their Pokémon are capable of performing such shapeshifting acts or if it's exclusive to a few select members and if so, if they were born with such ability or somehow unlocked this elusive power. Nonetheless, it does bring the actual species of the team members into question, such as if they're really human, Pokémon or even some sort of strange hybrid and if so, how it happened.

    A possible explanation was given by the TV show that depicted the team as having gone into a reality where only Pokémon have been shown to exist. However, as it is a TV show, it's a source that's unreliable at best, no matter how much the producers claim that the stories are real.

    The J-Team certainly has a supernatural aura surrounding them and are certainly one of the more interesting groups in this age, as well as being surprisingly big. More research on these people will be needed in order to really uncover the mysteries that surround them.

    (Update: In her recent tumblr posts, Ms.Minami has stated after being questioned about this topic that the people of the team prefer to stay in human form for the most part, only staying as a Pokémon for brief periods of time. However, as stated before, the validity of her statements is questionable at best)

    -M.R.B
