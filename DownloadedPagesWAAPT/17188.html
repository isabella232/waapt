    Stormchaser

    In the temporary mortuary, Salvador is taking a break from preserving the bodies of Alice and Lucius to dig into a bowl of hot snail soup, with a freshly baked baguette on the side. His enthusiasm for the dish is evident due to how quickly the dish is disappearing from the bowl.

    Salvador: Mmmhmm... Granduncle Pierre's snail soup recipe is to die for, even if my attempt pales in comparison. Thanks for fetching the ingredients by the way, Shaka.

    Shaka sits beside him, also digging into a bowl of the soup.

    Shaka: It was no skin off my back, my child. After all, your soup is still delectable.

    Salvador: (Breaking off a bit of the loaf and handing it to Shaka) And especially with the bread fresh from a Kalosian bakery.

    Shaka: (Dipping his bread in the soup) Reminds me of home...

    Salvador: (Also dipping his bread, while taking a bite) Yes, home indeed.

    Salvador quickly finishes off his meal in one large slurp and places the bowl on the table. Shaka finished not long after.

    Shaka: That was wonderful Salvador. Now if you don't mind me, I'm going to take an evening walk in the fresh air. I'm starting to feel the onset of cabin fever, not helped by the reek of perfumes intermingling with the stench of death...

    Salvador: You're excused. I however, have my job to do.

    Shaka: Understandable. I'll see you later.

    Shaka picks up the empty bowls and walks out of the room. Salvador watches Shaka as he leaves, before standing up to stretch while peering around the grisly room.

    Salvador: Hic locus est ubi mors gaudet succurrere vitae...

    He swiftly turns towards the slabs, humming some sort of jaunty tune, and begins examining Alice and Lucius' heads. Specifically, he begins examining their eyes.

    Salvador: Hmm. No good. Eyeballs are all shrivelled up. Not even tissue builder or an eye cap would be able to salvage this organ to a reasonably lifelike level. They will have to be plucked out and replaced with suitable glass counterparts. Gives the corpse a more full, lifelike look.

    Salvador then laughs as if someone had made a funny joke, and pats Alice on the head.

    Salvador: Oh no need to be so uneasy, you two aren't the first who've needed this procedure. I can assure you that you will receive the embalming treatment reserved for only Kalos' finest individuals. You two are in good hands.

    Salvador turns towards the tools table and takes another look at the profile photos of his subjects. Within his cowl, some rustling is seen as a female Meowstic emerges from her confinements.

    Thérèse: Mya?

    Salvador looks over his shoulders and pets his cat, who reciprocates by nuzzling into his hand. After pats are given, Salvador pulls out his ribbon and iron tape measure and begins measuring the diameter and circumference of the two's eye sockets.

    Salvador: Hello Thérèse. Anything you want?

    The Meowstic begins rubbing Salvador's right cheek.

    Thérèse: Mya?

    Salvador: No Thérèse, you don't want these eyes. They've withered away and would be hard and unpleasant to taste by now.

    Salvador writes down the measurements, and reaches over for a tool that looks like some sort of scoop.

    Thérèse: Myaa...

    Salvador: Oh alright. If you must insist, chat.

    Thérèse: Mya!

    Salvador begins to pluck out the rotted eyes of Lucius and Alice. As he removes one, he flings his tool to toss the organ away. As he flings them, Thérèse jumps up to catch the eyes and quickly devours them.

    Thérèse: ('Rolling around on the ground, satiated'') Myaa~

    Salvador: Even when they're dried up like sultanas, you still like to make a meal of the eyes - Don't you Thérèse?

    Thérèse: Zzzz...

    Salvador rolls his own eyes and chuckles at the cat, before picking up a small bowl of shells on the table.

    Salvador: Now, I'll have to go into town to get your new eyes commissioned. So in the meantime, these small shells will suffice in the interim. You wouldn't believe it, but for thousands of years we used to use shells like these to actually replace eyes proper. But it wasn't only shells we used, do you want to know what else we used?

    There is no reply.

    Salvador: Onions. Onions marked to resemble eyes. I guess that's why they always make you cry, huh?

    Salvador chuckles as he sits down. He picks up one of his scalpels and pulls out a razor strop, and begins to sharpen his surgical knife.

    Salvador: ...So Lucius... If what I understand is correct, your father was a man of the syndicate - a criminal type - I can sympathise with that. My father, he was also a criminal man. He even rebooted the syndicate he was a part of in order to realise their fallen leaders dream - As I know your father did. Did you know him, Constantin Minucius Rufus Perilloux?

    The only sound in the room is the sound of a blade being sharpened.

    Salvador: Yes, the tall, aloof and somewhat odd-but-amicable blond Kalosian. He is my father. He was an ardent and dogmatic supporter of Lysandre's Flare, though he believed at that time that Lysandre didn't go far enough with his beliefs. He committed many acts against humanity - up to and including murder - during his short life. He got quite close to achieving his goals, but everything collapse around him at the last second - and he barely managed to escape with his life due to some divine intervention. That is when he found the error of his ways...

    Salvador stops sharping his scalpal.

    Salvador: The reason why you caught on is... well... he's just that good at masking his true self should the need arise. His main skill was interrogation and manipulation. He essentially played you like the strings of a harp in hope that you might've been of some use to him down the line. Especially because of your disdain for Tagg.

    Salvador takes a quick swig of his water bottle, and raises an eyebrow at the bodies.

    Salvador: Thank you for calming him down Alice.

    As for why I still consider him a good man, and I love him as my father despite all of his wrongdoings. It's because of my philosophy regarding people. A man can change, quite drastically, if you allow them too. If you ostracise people saying they can never be redeemed, then they never will be. Every bad man can become good, if given the chance to see the light and learn the error of their ways - as my father did.

    Salvador yawns and reclines in his chair, still peering at the corpse of lucius on the slab.

    Salvador: I wonder if your father would want to see you again for one last time. Despite the errors of his ways, and your tumultuous relationship with each other. I'm sure he loved you to the depths of his soul. It might be a bittersweet and harrowing endeavour, but one worth pursuing... but we'll pass that mountain when it comes...

    Salvador's eyes start drooping as he yawns again, shutting his eyes and drifting off into the recesses of his sleep.
