I suppose its silly of me to expect a big tourist attraction or something here.

    <Sinnoh?>, she asked, giving her trainer a look. He nodded, causing the Ponyta to let out a soft chuckle as she shook her head. <I didnt expect to visit home again so soon after being traded.>

    At least youre in a familiar place, right? he asked, gently rubbing at her sides. That should make the journey easier.

    <Yes, I suppose.> She turned her head to him, her amber eyes locked onto his face. Upon noticing he was being stared at, Rex tilted his head, wondering if hed offended her in some way.

    OhIm sorry. Are we not familiar enough for petting? he asked, withdrawing his hand gently.

    Oh... He blinked at that, looking a little awkward. WellI just wanted someone to talk to, to be honest. I figured Id get to know one of the new team members a bit betterthats all.

    <I see.> She gently whinnied, stepping away from him to nibble at a nearby shrub. <Wellin the event you did need to move around quickly, I would not be loath to help.>

    Okay. He smiled, giving her a little space and smiling in her direction. Thank you.

    Ohthats a Ponyta?

    Rex blinked, turning around to see a blue-haired girl staring at the pair of them curiously. She wore a burgundy jacket, a quartet of pink buttons keeping it shut at the moment. A white scarf was wrapped around her neck, fitting well with the white handbag that was slung over her left shoulder. Her thighs, oddly enough, were exposed despite the weather, and Rex made a point not to let his eyes linger. From her knees down, she wore a pair of long socks which were also covered by a pair of pink boots. Atop her head was a white hat, a pink Pokéball symbol stamped on the front. Overall, her appearance said casual, but he couldnt help but feel there was an air ofsophistication to her.

    E-ermyeah, it is, he answered finally, though the girl had already began to step past him and over to the Pokémon. She smiled at it warmly, admiring the creature from a near, yet respectful distance.

    Shes very pretty, she murmured, turning back to him with a small smile. You know, when I was younger, I always wanted to have a Ponyta. She chuckled softly, a far-off look coming into her eyes. If Id had my way, it wouldve been my first Pokémon. Clearing her head of those thoughts, she grasped the hem of her jacket, giving a polite curtsy. My name is Dawn.
