    Imperial Commissar
    Thanks to Blitz and Moon for helping me to spag this incredibly long set of supervillainous shenanigans.

    Somehwere, deep within the depths of Sootopolis Volcano...

    A trio of not-so-nefarious supervillains are gathered on the main floor of what looks to be a large factory built within an even larger cavern, full of turbines, generators and old industrial equipment that has all but rusted away.

    Earthwyrm: Ah-HA! This is PERFECT! From this subterranean fortress, we shall at last be able to generate the power we need to kickstart PHASE ONE of my plan for world domination!

    Helion: I keep telling you, I have a Heliolisk. Power's not the issue. It's-

    Earthwyrm: Take your PIEHOLE and CLAMP IT SHUT, minion! The device is ready, is it not?

    Helion: ...Sure, I suppose. It'll take a couple of hours to warm up, but once that's done it should work just fine.

    Earthwyrm: Then it is DECIDED! By the time the sun sets on this fateful day, all of Sootopolis will be trembling in fear before their new overlord, THE UNDE- I mean, EARTHWYRM! Now then, Batastrophe!

    The blue-clad bat girl snaps to attention.

    Batastrophe: Whatcha need?

    Earthwyrm: Head up to the transmitter tower and ensure that it's tuned to the correct frequency. Keep an eye out for any disruptive do-gooders seeking IN VAIN to interfere with our plans.

    Batastrophe: You can count on me, boss~

    She skips away, leaving Helion with Earthwyrm.

    Helion: And me?

    Earthwyrm: Descend into the BOWELS of the server room-

    Helion: Never say "bowels" again.

    Earthwyrm: -and install the device. You are capable of hacking their FEEBLE security measures, yes?

    Helion sighs.

    Helion: It's not hacking. Using a packet analyser to capture the server's passwords and disable the firewalls would be hacking. All I'm doing is plugging something in and waiting for a progress bar to fill.

    Earthwyrm: IRREGARDLESS! Time is lacking, so whatever it is you're doing, do it QUICKLY!

    Helion: Fine. I'll get everything up and running so that you can go ahead with your incredibly dumb plan.

    She turns and descends deeper into the caves, adjusting the gauntlets on her wrists as she does. Earthwyrm rubs his hands together as he looks over one of the many assorted consoles nearby.

    Earthwyrm: Finally... after months of plotting, I am a mere GNAT'S WHISKER away from my goal! My grand conquest will soon be complete, and the world shall bow before the wrath of- oooh, what does this button do?

    He presses it.

    Sootopolis City

    At the same moment, Shun is walking through the city when suddenly his attention is caught by a cave mouth somewhere on the volcano starting to glow a deep, pulsating crimson colour. The transmitter tower sticking out the top of it also lights up like a Christmas tree.

    Shun: Hmm? That looks-

    ???: Oh, hey! I remember you!

    Shun turns around to find himself being waved at by a bleary-eyed young woman in a white lab coat.

    Scientist: You're the pipsqueak police detective who helped us deal with that crazy girl at the Space Center, right?

    Shun folds his arms indignantly.

    Shun: Police detective, yes. Pipsqueak, no.

    Scientist: Oh, right, yeah. I almost forgot about the chip on your shoulder. Anyway, I don't think I ever said thanks. Because of you and your cosplaying friends, we can all sleep a little easier. Well, except for me, obviously. I still can't sleep easily. Or at all.

    Shun tilts his head.

    Shun: ...If you work at the Mossdeep Space Center, what are you doing here?

    Scientist: Me? I live here. The daily commute's a lot more interesting when you do it on the back of a Hawlucha.

    Shun: In that case, can you tell me what that is all about?

    He points to the glowing red cave entrance.

    Scientist: Oh, that? It's the old Sootopolis Geothermal Plant. They figured there was no better place to build it than in the side of a volcano. Unfortunately, nobody involved in its construction realised that the volcano was dormant until after they finished building it.

    Shun: ...I see. And it has a transmitter tower because...?

    Scientist: Well, how else were the workers supposed to get a good reception in a cave?

    Shun: ...That at least makes a certain degree of sense.

    He looks up at the plant curiously.

    Shun: Is it supposed to be glowing a particularly malevolent shade of red?

    Scientist: Now that you mention it... no, I don't think it's meant to do that. In fact I'm pretty sure that means there's someone inside who probably shouldn't be there.

    Shun: That was the conclusion I arrived at as well.

    Suddenly Shun gets a text.

    From: Shingeko

    To: Shun

    Hey, I figured I'd tell you the Greninja that took that shot for you is doing fine now. Try not to anger any more criminals in the future so that things like this don't happen again, yeah?

    Shun looks down at his X-Transceiver, brows furrowed.

    Shun: How very convenient. Though I don't believe I ever gave him my number...

    He quickly composes a response.

    To: Shingeko

    From: Shun

    I'm glad to hear that. Though on the subject of angering criminals, I've just been informed of a possible break-in at the Sootopolis Geothermal Plant. Can I expect your presence at the scene shortly?

    And he soon gets a reply.

    To: Shun

    From: Shingeko

    There's a geothermal plant in this place? I never heard of it.

    And wow you went from blocking our entry to the crime scene to inviting us there? I guess saving someone's life changes a lot of things.

    Anyway, yeah, expect me to drop by with my partner soon.

    To: Shingeko

    From: Shun

    According to my source, this plant has been defunct for quite some time. I'm sending you a screenshot now.

    [creepyredcave.jpg]

    As for why I'm requesting your help, my inference that you were in the immediate vicinity of the crime scene proved to be accurate. That means you have a response time faster than any support I could request from my superiors or the local authorities. It's purely a matter of convenience.

    To: Shun

    From: Shingeko

    Always the serious guy huh? Fair enough, like I said, we'll be there shortly. I'll be sending some of my assistants ahead just in case.

    Cute file name btw.

    Indeed, not too long after the message was sent, Shun could see duo of Greninja arriving at the scene, though from looks alone it would be hard for him to tell if either were the same one from last time. Shun sighs and taps his foot.

    Shun: I was hoping to speak to the ringmaster rather than the sideshow acts.

    Both of them looked less than impressed with Shun's reaction and they made it very clear with their expressions. However, one of them began looking at the cave and trying to see if he could see anything further inside. Almost nothing is visible, save for a set of stairs leading further down into the caves.

    Sootopolis Geothermal Plant, Transmitter Tower

    From atop her perch on the tower, Batastrophe suppresses a squee as she watches the two Greninja poking around outside the entrance to the plant.

    Batastrophe: Eeeeeee~ If they're here, then that means he's here too! Oh, my heart aches every time we're apart... I just hope he hasn't brought that dumb attention seeker he calls a sidekick...

    She looks over at the radio by her side.

    Batastrophe: Should I warn the boss? Hm... if I do, I might miss my chance to see Shingeko again! I can't risk it.

    Sootopolis City

    Back on the ground, Shun rolls his eyes.

    Shun: ...Fine. If I have to go in by myself, then I will. There's every chance that this is merely an equipment malfunction or suchlike that I can resolve on my own initiative.

    He steps past the Greninja and into the plant, hand on his Pokeballs as he starts heading down the stairs. One of the Greninja quickly grab him in order to to make him stop though.

    Shingeko: Man, aren't you hasty.

    Shingeko then arrives alongside a third Greninja and Gekkobi. Shun turns around, looking slightly miffed at being grabbed.

    Shun: I could hardly wait for you forever. That, and I lack your ability to communicate with your "assistants", so I'm not sure how you expected us to effectively cooperate.

    Gekkobi: You could start by talking to them? They're perfectly capable of understanding you.

    He gave a nonchalant shrug.

    Gekkobi: Anyway, we're here now.

    Shingeko: Yeah, they're not dumb, you know? You should try being a little more respectful towards them instead of calling them "sideshow acts".

    Shun glowers, but takes a deep breath and manages to compose himself.

    Shun: ...I apologise. That was uncalled for. A product of my own frustration, largely. However, I will say that making an issue of it only serves to delay us further and encourage hostility during what is supposed to be a joint effort.

    Shingeko: Right. If the power plant is active now, are there any problems it might cause?

    The scientist chimes in.

    Scientist: It could blow up. You'd have to really get it pumping for that to happen, but if it did, there's a good chance that the resulting landslide might take half the city with it too. Not that anyone's dumb enough to do something like that.

    Gekkobi: ...You mean the kind of "really pumping" that results in glowing lights of death?

    Scientist: That kind, yeah.

    Shingeko: There's always someone dumb enough to do that.

    He sighs.

    Shingeko: Let's go inside before something happens.

    Gekkobi merely nods as they head into the cave. Shun sighs, and follows silently behind the two superheroes, wondering at what point his career went downhill.

    Sootopolis Geothermal Plant, Main Floor

    Inside, the entire plant is bathed in red light by the emergency lighting fixtures, all of which seem to be flashing with some urgency. The stairs lead down onto the main floor of the plant, which seems to be home to various types of rusted industrial equipment. Almost all of them appear to be active and on the verge of shaking themselves apart. Nothing about the place can be described as particularly stable. Two more stairwells can be seen leading off from the main floor: one leads up and out of the cave, whilst the other goes further down into it.

    Shun: This looks serious... if our intruder did enter with the intention of causing some form of pyrotechnics, then they may well be close to succeeding. Perhaps we ought to split up to cover more ground.

    Gekkobi: At least there won't be any in-fighting that way.

    He pauses.

    Gekkobi: Sorry, bad joke. I can take upstairs.

    Shingeko: Then I'll go downstairs.

    Shun: And I will cover the ground floor. Search the area, don't push any buttons unless you're certain that it's going to fix everything, and regroup down here once you've finished your sweep. Hopefully this will go better than last time...

    He starts slowly making his way along the rows of industrial machinery, whilst Gekkobi takes the stairs leading upwards and Shingeko leads his Greninja down to the lower levels.

    Helion: If it helps, I don't have anything against you personally. You seem like a pretty decent guy. I think the whole supervillain thing was a stupid idea from the word go, but Batastrophe seems to like it, so here we are.

    Helion: At least being the villain lets me make stuff like this.

    Shingeko: Smokescreen!

    One of the two behind him then jumps out and fires a big cloud of black smoke at Helion. However, it doesn't appear to have much of an effect.

    Teishi: <We have more than just defensive tricks.>

    Helion: I hate having to do this. There was stuff down here I could've used.

    Heliolisk: <Hate to break it to you, but you're not the only one who knows this trick.>

    Helion: ...I want you to know I feel bad about this. Not bad enough to not do it, but kinda bad nonetheless.
